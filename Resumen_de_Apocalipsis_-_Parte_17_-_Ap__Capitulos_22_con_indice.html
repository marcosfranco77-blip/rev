I

Introduction: The New Heavens and New Earth (NH&NE)

The Greatest Mystery in the Book
  • ✦
    Most Mysterious ContentThis final lesson concludes with the most mysterious content in Revelation and in the entire Bible.
  • ✦
    John’s ChallengeJohn is called to describe the New Heavens and New Earth, which represents an enormous challenge for him.
  • ✦
    Focus on the Capital CityJohn does not tell us much about the world itself; the little that is revealed focuses on the capital city: the New Jerusalem.
What We Already Know About the NH&NE
  • ❶
    No more weeping, pain, tears, or deathJohn already received the introduction to the new world as a place free from all the miseries of the fallen world.
  • ❷
    Total Fulfillment of PromisesIn the NH&NE everything God promised in the Bible is completely fulfilled.
  • ❸
    Not Revealed in the Old TestamentThis world was never revealed to the Old Testament saints; the prophets before Christ were only told about the Kingdom period, nothing beyond.
  • ❹
    Israel as a TypeWhat was true for Israel in the Kingdom will finally become reality for all humanity in the NH&NE.
II

The New Jerusalem: First View and the Bride of the Lamb

Revelation 21:9–14

“Come, I will show you the bride, the Lamb’s wife.” And he carried me away in the Spirit to a great and high mountain, and showed me the great city, the holy Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God, having the glory of God. Her light was like a most precious stone, like a jasper stone, clear as crystal. Also she had a great and high wall with twelve gates, and twelve angels at the gates, and names written on them, which are the names of the twelve tribes of the children of Israel… Now the wall of the city had twelve foundations, and on them were the names of the twelve apostles of the Lamb.

The City as the Bride of Christ
  • ✦
    Title TransferredIn the New Testament, “Bride of Christ” was the title of the Church. In the NH&NE that title is transferred to the city itself.
  • ✦
    Dwelling of the Spirit“Bride of Christ” refers to the dwelling place of His Spirit. In the Church Age, the Bride was the body of believers (His Temple).
  • ✦
    Eternal AgeIn the eternal age, Christ dwells with the Father in the Temple of the City, making the city the Bride of Christ. We live in the city, therefore we live with Christ.
Characteristics of the City
  • ✦
    Perfect CubeThe city is shaped like a perfect cube: length, width, and height are equal. Its first view is extraordinary.
  • ✦
    Glory of God as Its BrillianceThe city shines with the glory of God like a transparent diamond; brighter than our current sun.
  • ✦
    Wall with 12 GatesSurrounded by a high wall with three gates on each side, for a total of 12 gates with guardian angels.
  • ✦
    Gates Never ClosedThe gates are never shut (v.25). They are not defensive; they are monuments that remember God’s redemptive work.
III

The Twelve Tribes and the Twelve Apostles: Eternal Memorial

The Twelve Tribes of Israel on the Gates
  • ✦
    Each Gate: One TribeEach gate bears the name of one of the twelve tribes of Israel. The gates are points of entry; naming them this way reminds us that Israel is the gateway to redemption.
  • ✦
    No Israel, No EntryWithout Israel there would be no covenants, prophets, Law, temple, or Christ. Everyone enters through the legacy of Israel (Rom 9:4–5).
Romans 9:4–5

…who are Israelites, to whom pertain the adoption, the glory, the covenants, the giving of the law, the service of God, and the promises; of whom are the fathers, and from whom, according to the flesh, Christ came…

Numerical Significance: 3 and 12
  • ✦
    The Number 3 = the DeityThree is the number of God. From any side you enter you find 3 gates. The whole work was God’s alone.
  • ✦
    The Number 12 = Government Through People12 represents God governing through people, specifically the sons of Jacob. The gates forever remind us that the Lord used Israel to lead us into the city.
The Twelve Apostles in the Foundations
  • ✦
    Twelve Layers of Precious StonesThe foundations of the wall contain twelve layers of precious stones that represent the twelve apostles called by Jesus.
  • ✦
    Apostolic RoleThe apostles established the Church, opened the opportunity for Gentiles to enter, and spread the gospel. It is fitting that they are foundation layers.
  • ✦
    Israel and the Church: Distinct but UnitedThe commemoration of both groups reinforces that Israel and the Church are distinct in God’s plan and yet work together for His glory. (Eph 2:12–22)
Ephesians 2:19–20

…you are no longer strangers and foreigners, but fellow citizens with the saints and members of the household of God, having been built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Jesus Christ Himself being the chief cornerstone.

IV

The Measurements of the City: Extraordinary Dimensions

Revelation 21:15–21

…And he measured the city with the reed: twelve thousand furlongs. Its length, breadth, and height are equal. Then he measured its wall: one hundred and forty-four cubits… The construction of its wall was of jasper; and the city was pure gold, like clear glass. The foundations of the wall of the city were adorned with all kinds of precious stones… The twelve gates were twelve pearls: each individual gate was of one pearl. And the street of the city was pure gold, like transparent glass.

Amazing Dimensions
  • ✦
    1,500 miles in each directionThe city spans 1,500 miles long, 1,500 miles wide — covering most of the continental United States.
  • ✦
    Cube Reaching into SpaceThe city extends 1,500 miles upward, reaching the range of orbiting satellites. Earth’s current atmosphere only goes about 50 miles.
  • ✦
    Radically Different WorldThis city requires that the world sustaining it be completely different from the current one, with a different atmosphere or no need for one.
Greek Measurements and Their Meaning
  • ✦
    12,000 furlongs and 144 cubitsThe original Greek measurements —12,000 furlongs and 144 cubits— are multiples of 12, the number of God’s perfect government through men.
  • ✦
    Memorial of the Perfect PlanThe city is a memorial of God’s perfect plan fulfilled in Christ through Israel and the Church, all reflected in the numerical pattern.
  • ✦
    12 Jewels and Streets of GoldTwelve precious stones adorn the foundations, solid pearl gates, and transparent gold streets. What is most valuable today will be pavement then.
V

The Inhabitants and the Glory of the City (Rev. 21:22–27)

Revelation 21:22–27

But I saw no temple in it, for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are its temple. The city had no need of the sun or of the moon to shine in it, for the glory of God illuminated it. The Lamb is its light. And the nations of those who are saved shall walk in its light, and the kings of the earth bring their glory and honor into it. Its gates shall not be shut at all by day (there shall be no night there). But there shall by no means enter it anything that defiles…

No Temple: God Is the Temple
  • ✦
    Direct PresenceThere will be no temple building because the Trinity of God is the temple of the city. In the past, the temple separated sinful man from Holy God.
  • ✦
    Full Communion RestoredWith all sin erased, the Deity in fullness will dwell with us in His Person. Since Adam in the garden, this had not been possible.
  • ✦
    God All in All (1 Cor 15:28)The Father, Son, and Holy Spirit will be fully present; the Son subjects Himself to the Father, that God may be all in all.
God Is the Light; the Nations Walk
  • ✦
    No Sun or MoonThere will be no sun or moon because the glory of God is the source of all light in the new world.
  • ✦
    Saved NationsThe redeemed nations that inhabit the new earth walk in the light of the Lamb.
  • ✦
    Kings Return Their GloryThe kings of the earth bring their glory to God; all authority is returned, so that all glory belongs to God and is for God (cf. 1 Cor 15:24).
  • ✦
    Only the RegisteredNothing unclean shall enter, only those written in the Lamb’s Book of Life.
Is the New Earth Flat?
  • ✦
    Open QuestionIf God resides in the city and is the only source of light, this could suggest a flat world — a sphere would leave the back side in darkness. Or perhaps the entire universe is equally illuminated, or the laws of physics have changed. The answer is not clear.
VI

The Design of the First Creation: Why Seas, Night, and Sun Existed

Water: Material and Judgment
  • ✦
    Peter Explains WaterPeter says (2 Pet 3:5–6) that the earth was formed out of water and by water, and perished being flooded with water. The use of water reflects God’s long-term plan.
  • ✦
    Anticipation of JudgmentGod knew He would use water to bring judgment (Noah’s flood), so water was present from the beginning.
  • ✦
    The Sea as Image of SheolThe Lord uses the depths of the sea as an image of Sheol (place of the dead). In Hebrew, “tehom” (abyss/deep) and “sea” are the same figure: endless darkness.
Light and Darkness: Sin and Illustration
  • ✦
    Light Before the Sun (Day 1)Light existed on Day 1 before the sun existed. This confirms that light can exist without the sun — as will happen in the NH&NE.
  • ✦
    Darkness as Illustration of SinThe Lord created the alternation of light/darkness to illustrate righteousness and sin. (Jn 8:12: “I am the light of the world. He who follows Me shall not walk in darkness.”)
The Sun and Moon: Creation of Time
  • ✦
    Purpose of Sun and MoonThe sun and moon were not created primarily to provide light — light already existed — but to mark seasons, days, and years (Gen 1:14). Their function was to measure time.
  • ✦
    Signs of the EndGod uses the sun and moon as signs of the end of the age (sun darkened, blood moon). They are instruments of the divine countdown.
  • ✦
    Time Counts DownUnbelievers say the earth is eternal and we are temporary, but it is the opposite: we are eternal and the earth is temporary. God counts time backward, not forward — it is a countdown to the end.
No Night = No “Days”; No Sun = No “Years”
  • ✦
    No Need to Count TimeIn the NH&NE it will not be necessary to count time in the same way, nor will seasons or signs be needed, so the objects created for that purpose disappear.
  • ✦
    Time Is EndlessTime is endless in the NH&NE, although months continue to be counted (for observing celebrations and memorials of Christ in cycles of 12).
VII

The River of the Water of Life and the Tree of Life (Rev. 22:1–5)

Revelation 22:1–5

And he showed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding from the throne of God and of the Lamb. In the middle of its street, and on either side of the river, was the tree of life, which bore twelve fruits, each tree yielding its fruit every month. The leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations. And there shall be no more curse, but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it, and His servants shall serve Him. They shall see His face, and His name shall be on their foreheads. There shall be no night there: They need no lamp nor light of the sun, for the Lord God gives them light. And they shall reign forever and ever.

The River of the Water of Life
  • ✦
    Origin: the ThroneFrom the throne of God and of the Lamb flows a river of crystal-clear water that runs through the center of a great avenue of the city.
  • ✦
    Parallel with EzekielIn the Kingdom, Ezekiel and Zechariah described a river coming out of the temple that brought life everywhere. The river of life is part of God’s design both in the Kingdom and in the NH&NE.
The Tree of Life
  • ✦
    Known Since the Garden of EdenThe last time we saw this tree was before the Fall. Adam and Eve ate from it and had eternal life; after sin they were excluded (Gen 3:22–24).
  • ✦
    Twelve Fruits MonthlyThe tree produces fruit in a monthly pattern — a different fruit each month — marking the cycle of 12 that will measure time in the NH&NE.
  • ✦
    Healing Leaves for the NationsThe leaves of the tree have healing power for the nations that live in the NH&NE.
Why Does the Tree Exist? Memory Mechanism
  • ✦
    All Life Comes from GodThe tree is sustained by the water that comes from the throne; it clearly communicates that all life comes from God. Every time we eat from the tree we are reminded of the source of life.
  • ✦
    Prevent ForgettingWithout death or disease to remind us of our mortality, we could easily take immortality for granted and forget that the source of all life is God. The tree prevents that forgetfulness.
  • ✦
    Months for CelebrationsThe monthly cycle of 12 probably serves to observe certain celebrations and memorials of Christ, honoring God through the pattern of observance.
VIII

No More Curse: Perfect Life in the Presence of God

The Curse Removed (Rev. 22:3)
  • ✦
    Fulfilled and RemovedThe curse God pronounced in Genesis 3 on the earth because of Adam — physical death, painful labor, and eventual destruction of the Earth — has been carried out and eliminated.
  • ✦
    Perfect and Flawless WorldWithout the curse, the world is perfect. There are no weeds, no heavy labor, no sin or death.
Life in the Direct Presence of God
  • ✦
    We Will See God’s FaceOur perfect nature allows us to serve in His very presence and see His face — something impossible for sinful man.
  • ✦
    His Name on UsHe will mark us as His own with His name on us, a seal of guarantee that we will never again face death or sin.
  • ✦
    No Darkness AnywhereGod’s light will exist in all places: no dark room, no shadows. Possibly we will not even sleep on a cyclic schedule.

The Lesson of Design: God Anticipated Everything
  • ✦
    Design with Redemptive PurposeBefore man existed or the Fall occurred, the Lord incorporated characteristics into the first creation anticipating the arrival of sin, so that those characteristics would serve to illustrate spiritual truths about sin and redemption.
  • ✦
    Darkness Illustrates SinHe created light and darkness to be able to use those terms and speak of sin and righteousness (“I am the light of the world” — Jn 8:12).
  • ✦
    The Sea Illustrates Judgment and DeathHe created the sea to use it in the judgment of the flood, and uses its depths as an image of Sheol and eternal punishment.
  • ✦
    The NH&NE Needs No IllustrationsIn the NH&NE, sin no longer exists and never will again. God designs a different world — without those illustrations — because the things they illustrated no longer exist.
X

Predestination, the Purpose of Creation, and the Full Character of God

Ephesians 1:5–6

having predestined us to adoption as sons by Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the good pleasure of His will, to the praise of the glory of His grace, by which He made us accepted in the Beloved.

Predestination: Chosen Before the Foundation
  • ✦
    God Knew Sin in AdvanceGod chose us before the foundation of the world, knowing the entrance of sin and our redemption in His Son Jesus. Without being the author of sin, He anticipated it and accommodated its arrival.
  • ✦
    Creation Was Made to Know God CompletelyCreation had to know sin and the judgment that sin requires, in order to fully appreciate the love of God and glorify Him for who He really is.
The Full Character of God
  • ✦
    God Is More Than MercyTo truly know God, we need to know both sides: He is not only a God of mercy, but of wrath; not only of forgiveness but of judgment; not only of love but One who hates sin.
  • ✦
    To Love Him We Must Know Him TotallyIf we are going to love God, we must have the opportunity to know both sides of His character. We must understand that He is a God of wrath and judgment in order to praise Him for His grace.
  • ✦
    We Remember in the NH&NEIn the new creation, we will always be exposed to the side of blessing, but we will remember the other side because we knew it. The New Jerusalem, the tree, the river, the light — everything reminds us.
XI

The Epilogue: Christ’s Final Words to John (Rev. 22:6–15)

Revelation 22:6–9

“These words are faithful and true. And the Lord God of the holy prophets sent His angel to show His servants the things which must shortly take place. Behold, I am coming quickly! Blessed is he who keeps the words of the prophecy of this book.” Now I, John, saw and heard these things… I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel who showed me these things. Then he said to me, “See that you do not do that… Worship God!”

Faithful and True Words
  • ✦
    The Same Spirit of the ProphetsThe Holy Spirit who gave the prophets their words (Isaiah, Daniel) is the same who gave John these words. The fulfillment of Isaiah and Daniel validates the certainty of Revelation.
  • ✦
    “Soon” = 2,000 Years?In Greek, “soon” refers to the fact that once the events begin, they will move quickly. In light of eternity, 2,000 years is nothing.
John’s Wrong Response — and the Correction
  • ✦
    John Bowed to Worship the AngelUpon seeing all this, John fell at the feet of the angel to worship him. The angel rebuked him: “Do not do that! I am your fellow servant… Worship God!”
  • ✦
    Lesson for UsWe must not elevate any servant of God — whether man or angel — beyond what is appropriate.
Blessed Is He Who Keeps the Words
  • ✦
    Not Just Intellectual KnowledgePaying attention means allowing the reality of these coming events to influence the decisions we make in life. It is not like reading a novel: it is a change of thinking, of direction, of hope.
  • ✦
    Living with Eyes on EternityJesus says He is coming quickly (reference to the Rapture) and that we must be thinking about the end at all times, prepared for His return.
The Prophecy Is NOT Sealed (vs. Daniel)
  • ✦
    Daniel Sealed; John Does Not SealDaniel was told to seal his vision because it would not be revealed until the days of John (Rev 11). Daniel’s prophecy was so far ahead that it was not useful in his days. John is told to leave the prophecy unsealed because the time is near.
  • ✦
    Meaning Becoming ClearerThe meaning of the book becomes clearer as we approach the end. That growing clarity is itself a sign that we are near.
  • ✦
    The Book Brings BlessingThis is not a book to be feared or avoided; on the contrary, the book pronounces a blessing on those who study it.
XII

The Final Call: To Believers, to Unbelievers, and the Warning About the Scriptures

Revelation 22:12–21

“And behold, I am coming quickly, and My reward is with Me, to give to every one according to his work. I am the Alpha and the Omega, the Beginning and the End, the First and the Last.” Blessed are those who do His commandments, that they may have the right to the tree of life, and may enter through the gates into the city. But outside are dogs… For I testify to everyone who hears the words of the prophecy of this book: If anyone adds to these things, God will add to him the plagues that are written in this book; and if anyone takes away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part from the Book of Life… “Surely I am coming quickly.” Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus!

For the Believer
  • ✦
    Jesus Comes Without WarningJesus says He will come quickly — without prior notice — and that He brings a reward: He will reward us for our works done in His service.
  • ✦
    Blessed Are Those Who Wash Their RobesThose who are cleansed from sin by the blood of Jesus will have the right to the tree of life and will enter through the gates of the city.
  • ✦
    The Spirit and the Bride Say “Come”Since the first century, the Spirit of God and the Church have been calling the world to know Jesus. Whoever is thirsty for righteousness may take the water of life freely.
For the Unbeliever
  • ✦
    A Call to Desire Good ThingsJesus calls unbelievers to desire: to be cleansed from sin by His blood, to eat from the tree of life instead of dying in the lake of fire, and to enter through the gates of the city.
  • ✦
    “The Dogs Will Be Outside”Those who practice and love the unjust acts of sin and do not repent will remain outside the city. The lake of fire will be somewhere outside the city in the new earth.
  • ✦
    Not About Losing SalvationJesus is not saying that committing sins automatically makes us unsaved. He describes the behaviors that characterize the lifestyle of unbelievers.
The Warning About Tampering with the Scriptures
  • ✦
    Adding: Will Bring the Plagues of the BookWhoever adds to the words of this prophecy, God will bring on him the plagues described in the book.
  • ✦
    Taking Away: Will Lose His Part in the Book of LifeThe language of v.19 uses parallel Greek wording: “Take away My words, and I take away from you.” It is not that someone loses salvation, but that doing this demonstrates unbelief.
  • ✦
    The Desire to Undermine the Word Is UnbeliefAltering the Word of God is a sure sign that the person has no relationship with the Spirit who is the Author of the work.
  • ✦
    Applies to the Whole BibleSince the Spirit is the Author of the entire Bible as one work, this warning logically extends to all the books of Scripture, not only Revelation.
Jesus Identifies Clearly and Closes the Book
  • ✦
    The Root and Offspring of DavidJesus testifies that we must trust and believe what He has given us, and trust in Him as the Messiah, the descendant of David.
  • ✦
    The Only Book in First PersonIt is the only book of Scripture in which Jesus speaks to the reader in the first person as the incarnate Christ, and the only one where He emphasizes with such force that the word must be trusted and heard.
  • ✦
    “Surely I Am Coming Quickly”The last promise of the biblical canon. John responds: “Amen. Come, Lord Jesus” — the cry of hope of every believer through the centuries.
✦

The New Heavens and New Earth are not simply the “after” of divine judgment — they are the culmination of the eternal plan of a God who designed the first creation anticipating sin, in order to fully express His character: His wrath and His mercy, His justice and His grace. The New Jerusalem, with its gates of the twelve tribes, its apostolic foundations, its tree of life, its river of glory, and its light which is God Himself, is the eternal memorial of everything God accomplished in Christ. In that perfect world there will no longer be night, sea, artificial sun, or curse — because the illustrations of sin will no longer be necessary. And the final call of the biblical canon continues to resound: the Spirit and the Bride say “Come!” — and let the one who is thirsty come.
