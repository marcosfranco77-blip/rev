Chapter covered: Revelation 20 (first part) Cross references: Daniel 9, 12 · Isaiah 2, 26, 65 · Matthew 25 · 1 Corinthians 13, 15 · Jeremiah 24, 31, 50 · Ezekiel 11, 44 · Zephaniah 3 Historical period: The 75-Day Interval Between the Tribulation and the Millennial Kingdom
I

Global Perspective: The Interval Between the Tribulation and the Kingdom

The Long-Awaited Moment
  • ✦
    Fulfillment of the promiseThe world has waited for the Lord’s return since Genesis 3:15. The ancient world, the patriarchs, the prophets and kings of Israel, the apostles, and the Church have eagerly awaited this coming day.
  • ✦
    The timeline culminatesChrist returns to the earth, ending the Tribulation and the Age of the Gentiles. The world is silent and the enemy defeated.
  • ✦
    The promised inheritanceBelievers will join Jesus on a land free of wars, injustice, and pandemics to enjoy the promised inheritance.
The Time Interval
  • ✦
    What is the interval?There is a brief interval of time between the Tribulation and the Kingdom. It is not part of the Tribulation nor part of the Kingdom time, but links one to the other and accommodates certain events that must take place in preparation for the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    Total durationThe interval lasts 75 days in total: two distinct periods of 30 days and 45 days, each with specific purposes.
II

Daniel’s Timeline: Prophetic Anchors

Daniel 9:27

"Then he shall confirm a covenant with many for one week; But in the middle of the week He shall bring an end to sacrifice and offering. And on the wing of abominations shall be one who makes desolate, Even until the consummation, which is determined, Is poured out on the desolate."

The Three Anchors of the Tribulation
  • ❶
    The beginningThe signing of a covenant between the Antichrist and Israel, which will allow the Jews to restart sacrifice in a newly built temple on the Temple Mount. This event begins the seven years.
  • ❷
    The middle — 3.5 years (1,260 days)The Antichrist ends the sacrifice in the temple and assumes a new and dangerous role in the world. The abomination of desolation — the image of the Antichrist — is installed in the temple.
  • ❸
    The end — 7 yearsComplete destruction upon the Antichrist and the return of Christ. The Tribulation consists of two periods of 1,260 days (two times 3.5 years using the Jewish 360-day calendar).
Daniel 12: Extension of the Timeline
Daniel 12:11–12

"And from the time that the daily sacrifice is taken away, and the abomination of desolation is set up, there shall be one thousand two hundred and ninety days. Blessed is he who waits, and comes to the one thousand three hundred and thirty-five days."

  • ✦
    The 1,290 days30 additional days after the Second Coming of Jesus to remove the abomination from the temple and cleanse the temple. The image remains standing for a time until it is removed.
  • ✦
    The 1,335 daysAnother 45 additional days. The "blessed of the Lord" are those who wait and reach this day: a period to identify who can enter the Kingdom and give them eternal bodies.
III

The First Period: 30 Days of Earth Restoration

The State of the Earth
  • ✦
    Global disasterThe judgments of the Tribulation have resulted in a global natural disaster of biblical proportions. Mountains leveled, cities destroyed, water turned to blood, no sun, moon, or stars. The earth has very little left.
  • ✦
    The Kingdom earthYet this same damaged earth is the 1,000-year Kingdom earth. If we are to enjoy the world with Christ during the Kingdom, this disaster must be supernaturally cleaned.
Isaiah 65: Renewal, Not New Creation
Isaiah 65:17–19

"For behold, I create new heavens and a new earth; And the former shall not be remembered or come to mind. But be glad and rejoice forever in what I create; For behold, I create Jerusalem as a rejoicing, And her people a joy. I will rejoice in Jerusalem, And joy in My people; The voice of weeping shall no longer be heard in her, Nor the voice of crying."

  • ✦
    Correct sense of “new”The original Hebrew indicates re-creation, not creation from nothing. The best sense is “creating the heavens again and the earth again” — renewing something that has been ruined, not a “brand new start.”
  • ✦
    Difference with Revelation 21Do not confuse with Revelation 21, where John describes a radically different world (in Greek “fresh new”): no seas, no darkness, no physical sky, no moon. The world of Isaiah 65 still has sin and death.
The Meaning of the 30 Days
  • ✦
    Purpose of the timeGod could have restored the earth instantly, but He takes 30 days as He did with the 6 days of creation: numbers and everything God does have purpose and multiple levels of revelation.
  • ✦
    The number 3 (Deity)The number 3 is the number of the Deity, reminding us that God is at the center of this work and the coming world.
  • ✦
    The number 10 (Testimony)The number 10 is the number of testimony in the Bible. The entire Kingdom is a testimony to the righteous government of Jesus and His right to the inheritance the Father has given Him. (30 = 3×10)
Other Events of the 30 Days
  • ❶
    The Antichrist and the False ProphetThey die immediately at the Second Coming of Jesus. Their souls descend to hell where the kings mock them. Then they are cast into the Lake of Fire eternally — the first two in that place, alone for 1,000 years.
  • ❷
    Removal of the bodiesThe armies that fought against Jesus were killed with His sword at His return. Their bodies must be removed with the help of the birds, a process that takes time within these 30 days.
  • ❸
    The New Temple of the KingdomAccording to Daniel 12 and Ezekiel, a new and majestic temple will be built for the opening of the Kingdom. The topography itself will change: new mountains and rivers around Jerusalem (Isaiah 2:2–3).
IV

The Second Period: 45 Days of Judgment and Blessing

Numerical Significance
  • ✦
    The number 9 (Judgment)The number 9 is the number of judgment. This period is a time of judging hearts to determine who enters the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    The number 5 (Grace)The number 5 is the number of grace. In the midst of judgment, there will be grace for some. (45 = 9×5) — Judgment and grace simultaneous.
Binding of Satan (Rev. 20:1–3)
Revelation 20:1–3

"Then I saw an angel coming down from heaven, having the key to the bottomless pit and a great chain in his hand. He laid hold of the dragon, that serpent of old, who is the Devil and Satan, and bound him for a thousand years; and he cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal on him, so that he should deceive the nations no more till the thousand years were finished. But after these things he must be released for a little while."

  • ✦
    The Father remains in heavenDuring the entire 1,000-year reign of Christ, the Father remains separate from the Son, out of reach of Creation. Only through His Son can anyone approach the Father, for there will still be sin on the earth.
  • ✦
    Satan boundGod could have bound Satan at any time, but chose to do it this way for His own glory and purposes. He will be released at the end of the kingdom for a short time (v.7).
The Three Destinies: Abyss, Hell, and Lake of Fire
The Abyss

A place in the depths of the earth where God holds the spirits of demons until the day of their judgment. It is not hell. Satan will remain here during the 1,000 years of the Kingdom. At a future time it will be emptied for final judgment.

Hell (Sheol/Hades)

A place of detention for sinful humans in the depths of the earth. It is not the final state. The Antichrist and the False Prophet will pass here while waiting to be cast into the Lake of Fire.

The Lake of Fire — Permanent Destiny

The permanent and final residence for both demons and unbelieving humanity. The Antichrist and the False Prophet will be the first two to arrive, alone during the first 1,000 years of the Kingdom. Neither the Abyss nor Hell are final states — both precede this final judgment.

The Five Groups of Kingdom Citizens

Overview of the Five Groups
Group Description Body Status Resurrection
#1 Church Saints Believers of the Church Age, including the reader Glorified (Rapture) At the Rapture, before the Tribulation
#2 O.T. Saints Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, David, John the Baptist… Glorified (interval) During the 45 days of the interval
#3 Tribulation Martyrs Souls under the altar (Rev. 6), beheaded for their faith Glorified (interval) During the 45 days of the interval
#4 Jews Alive at Christ’s Return Remnant of Petra + Jews in Israel who confessed Jesus Natural (possibly mixed) Debate: possibly glorified or in natural bodies
#5 Living Gentile Believers "The sheep" — Gentiles who protected the Jewish people Natural Not dead; enter the Kingdom in natural bodies
Key Implication: Two Natures in the Kingdom
  • ✦
    Glorified bodiesDo not sin, do not marry, do not have children, do not die. They are like the resurrected Jesus. Called to rule the natural world of sinners.
  • ✦
    Natural bodiesStill carry sin in their bodies, marry and have children. Their descendants will be unbelievers and sinners who need to be ruled.
  • ✦
    Fascinating coexistenceThe coexistence of these two distinct natures leads to fascinating consequences for life in the Kingdom that will be studied next.
Marriage in Glorified Bodies
Mark 12:24–25

"Jesus answered and said to them, “Are you not therefore mistaken, because you do not know the Scriptures nor the power of God? For when they rise from the dead, they neither marry nor are given in marriage, but are like angels in heaven."

  • ✦
    Why marriage is no longer neededMarriage was instituted because it was not good for the man to be alone in the face of sin. As resurrected ones, we will be married to Christ and will not need a partner. Multiplication to populate the earth is no longer required.
VI

Group #2: Resurrection of Old Testament Saints

Daniel 12:1–2

"At that time Michael shall stand up, The great prince who stands watch over the sons of your people; And there shall be a time of trouble, Such as never was since there was a nation, Even to that time. And at that time your people shall be delivered, Every one who is found written in the book. And many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, Some to everlasting life, Some to shame and everlasting contempt."

The Resurrection Described
  • ✦
    The context of Daniel 12:1The salvation of Israel at the end of the Tribulation triggers the resurrection of the O.T. saints. “At that time” Daniel’s people will be saved and many of those who “sleep” will awake to everlasting life.
  • ✦
    Unbelievers do not resurrect hereUnbelieving Jews are promised disgrace, although their resurrection does not take place at this point. Their time will come after the Kingdom (second resurrection).
Isaiah 26 Confirms It
Isaiah 26:19

"Your dead shall live; Together with my dead body they shall arise. Awake and sing, you who dwell in dust; For your dew is like the dew of herbs, And the earth shall cast out the dead."

  • ✦
    A wonderful momentFor the first time we will see Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, David and even John the Baptist in the flesh. We will be able to walk and interact with the men and women of the Bible stories. We can ask Adam good questions!
1 Corinthians 13:12

"For now we see in a mirror, dimly, but then face to face. Now I know in part, but then I shall know just as I also am known."

VII

Group #3: Resurrection of Tribulation Martyrs

Revelation 20:4

"And I saw thrones, and they sat on them, and judgment was committed to them. Then I saw the souls of those who had been beheaded for their witness to Jesus and for the word of God, who had not worshiped the beast or his image, and had not received his mark on their foreheads or on their hands. And they lived and reigned with Christ for a thousand years."

Their Prior State and Resurrection
  • ✦
    Souls under the altarThese are the Tribulation saints martyred for their faith, seen in Revelation 6 under the altar. Until now they have lived in Heaven only as souls, without physical bodies.
  • ✦
    "Came to life"Souls never cease to exist, but they were “dead” in the sense of lacking physical bodies. “Came to life” is a description of resurrection — their moment of receiving new bodies, analogous to the Rapture for the Church saints.
The Thrones of Government
  • ✦
    Authority to judgeAfter binding Satan, John sees thrones established for ruling. This confirms that these events are part of the 45-day period of judgment and blessing described by Daniel.
  • ✦
    Millennial reignThese martyrs “lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years” — their resurrection and enthronement occur within the 75-day interval as preparation for the Kingdom.
VIII

Group #4: Jews Alive at Christ’s Return — Will They Be Resurrected?

The Promises for Israel in the Kingdom
  • ✦
    Jeremiah 31:31–34 — The New CovenantMade with Israel (not originally with the Gentiles). The promise: all Israel will know the Lord perfectly, with no need to teach, sins forgiven, and hearts transformed.
  • ✦
    Jeremiah 24:7 and 50:19–20Israel will receive a heart to know the Lord and will turn to Him with all their heart. Even if one searches for sin in Israel, it will not be found. 100% of the nation will be 100% obedient.
  • ✦
    Ezekiel 11:19–20 and Zephaniah 3:9–13God gives Israel a new heart and a new spirit, perfectly obedient. The remnant of Israel will do no injustice, nor speak lies, nor will a deceitful tongue be found in their mouth.
The Problem: There Are Marriages, Deaths, and Teaching in Israel
Ezekiel 44:21–26

"No priest shall drink wine when he enters the inner court… Neither a widow nor a divorced woman shall they take as wife… They shall teach My people the difference between the holy and the unholy…"

  • ✦
    Apparent contradictionIf Israel were completely glorified, there would be no marriages, no death, no need for teaching. But Ezekiel 44 describes priests who marry, have families, and teach the people — inconsistent with full glorification.
  • ✦
    The best answer: both groupsSome Jews will be glorified (the resurrected O.T. Saints). But other Jews will enter the Kingdom in natural form, marry, have offspring, and possess sin. Kingdom Israel will be a combined group.
IX

The First and Second Resurrection

The First Resurrection: Judgment of Believers
Revelation 20:6

"Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection. Over such the second death has no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with Him a thousand years."

  • ✦
    First and second = time of judgmentThe terms “first” and “second” resurrection do not refer to the time of the resurrection, but to the time of judgment. Two judgments: first for believers, second for unbelievers.
  • ✦
    Resurrection in parts“Having part” reflects that the first resurrection occurs in stages over time, not in a single event.
The Five Parts of the First Resurrection
① The Resurrection of Christ
Christ was the firstfruits of the resurrection (1 Cor. 15:20), the first to receive a new body. The basis and guarantee of all subsequent resurrections.
② Church Saints — The Rapture
The believers of the Church Age, including the reader, receive new bodies at the Rapture, before the start of the Tribulation.
③ The Two Witnesses
The Two Witnesses resurrect in the middle of the Tribulation, being seen by the whole world (Rev. 11).
④ Old Testament Saints
They resurrect during the 45-day period of the interval, at the end of the Tribulation, according to Daniel 12.
⑤ Tribulation Martyrs
They resurrect after the Tribulation during the 75-day interval, according to Revelation 20:4.
The Second Resurrection: Judgment of Unbelievers
Revelation 20:5

"But the rest of the dead did not live again until the thousand years were finished. This is the first resurrection."

  • ✦
    The “rest of the dead”By process of elimination, this can only refer to the unbelieving dead. All unbelievers will be resurrected in a second event at the end of the 1,000 years.
  • ✦
    No blessingThe second resurrection is not a blessed event. It is not for reward but for final judgment by works, for perdition. The unbelievers were not saved from sin by rejecting Christ.
  • ✦
    The Judgment Seat of ChristThe judgment for believers (first resurrection) is a judgment of reward — the “Judgment Seat of Christ” (2 Cor. 5:10). To receive reward in the Kingdom, the believer must obtain his “heavenly ticket” — his new body — before the Kingdom begins.
X

Group #5: The Living Gentiles — Sheep and Goats (Matthew 25)

The Separation of the Nations
Matthew 25:31–33

"When the Son of Man comes in His glory, and all the holy angels with Him, then He will sit on the throne of His glory. All the nations will be gathered before Him, and He will separate them one from another, as a shepherd divides his sheep from the goats."

  • ✦
    The Greek “ethnos”The Greek word ethnos is commonly used for Gentiles. It makes sense because the Jews are already with Jesus. This is the judgment of the Gentile survivors of the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    The place of judgmentJesus waited 30 days for the temple to be cleansed and rebuilt. Perhaps the throne will be in the new temple, which would explain why the judgment waits until the 45-day period.
  • ✦
    Right and leftIn Eastern culture, the right was the place of honor (sheep = believers) and the left was dishonor (goats = unbelievers). The judgment of dignity will be based on faith, not works (Habakkuk 2:4).
The Sheep: Gentile Believers
Matthew 25:34–36

"Then the King will say to those on His right hand, ‘Come, you blessed of My Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world: for I was hungry and you gave Me food; I was thirsty and you gave Me drink; I was a stranger and you took Me in; I was naked and you clothed Me; I was sick and you visited Me; I was in prison and you came to Me.’"

  • ✦
    The fruit, not the salvationJesus does not say that social works caused salvation — He describes the fruit of the Spirit visible in believers during the Tribulation. They will be known by their fruit, not saved by it.
  • ✦
    The unique fruit of the TribulationIn that period there can be no church services, missionaries, or worship albums on the internet — the Antichrist would come and behead them. But the fruit will be visible in another way.
Works of Mercy: For Whom?
Matthew 25:40

"And the King will answer and say to them, ‘Assuredly, I say to you, inasmuch as you did it to one of the least of these My brethren, you did it to Me.’"

Who are “these My brethren”?
  • ✦
    By eliminationThey cannot be Gentiles (they are the ones doing the works). They cannot be believing Jews (they are protected under the Lord’s care). The only remaining group: the persecuted unbelieving Jews during the Tribulation.
Suicidal Acts of Mercy
  • ✦
    Extreme dangerCaring for the sick, delivering precious water, clothing, visiting imprisoned Jews during the Antichrist’s reign of terror was potentially suicidal. Only faith in Christ would motivate them to take such risks.
The Surprise of the Sheep
  • ✦
    New believers without instructionThese sheep will be so absent from all discipleship during the Tribulation that they will be surprised to learn they are to be rewarded. They did not know they were serving Jesus.
  • ✦
    Faith does not depend on maturityThe book of Acts is full of such people. The Spirit brings life and faith does not depend on spiritual maturity. They simply lived in the Spirit and were led — and that produced fruit.
  • ✦
    Every act was service to JesusThey did not realize they were serving Jesus until the moment He Himself told them. They were motivated to protect Israel — that is the fruit of the Spirit.
The Goats: Unbelieving Gentiles
Matthew 25:41, 46

"Then He will also say to those on the left hand, ‘Depart from Me, you cursed, into the everlasting fire prepared for the devil and his angels… And these will go away into everlasting punishment, but the righteous into eternal life."

  • ✦
    No fruit of the SpiritThe goats lack the fruit of the Spirit because they had no faith. They die instantly and enter hell to await the Second Resurrection. Later they will enter the Lake of Fire.
  • ✦
    Final outcomeThe believing Gentiles (sheep) will live and be welcomed into the Kingdom in natural bodies. The unbelieving Gentiles (goats) die immediately and enter hell to await the Second Resurrection.
XI

Entry into the Kingdom: Children Born During the Millennium

A Fascinating Fact
  • ✦
    Natural men and womenThere will be men and women with natural bodies who enter the Kingdom — both Jews (possibly) and Gentiles. These natural human beings still carry sin in their bodies.
  • ✦
    Reproduction in the KingdomThose with natural bodies will be able to marry and have children. When they reproduce, they will produce natural children like us: unbelievers and sinners who need to be ruled.
  • ✦
    The calling of the resurrected saintsThe resurrected saints are called to rule the natural world of sinners and the sin offering they produce. We are called to be part of this government.
Upcoming Study Topics
  • ❶
    The purposes of the KingdomNow that the citizens of the Kingdom have been established, the study will advance to the Kingdom period itself, beginning with its purposes.
  • ❷
    The coexistence of the two naturesHow the group of glorified bodies and the group of natural bodies coexist and interact harmoniously within the Millennial Kingdom.
  • ❸
    The Kingdom TempleThe new temple described by Ezekiel will become the centerpiece of life in the Kingdom. The dwelling place of Jesus during the 1,000 years and the place from which He will rule.
  • ❹
    The reunion of the DeityEventually the Father will join the Son. This extraordinary event will be studied in Revelation 21.
✦
The 75-day interval between the Tribulation and the Kingdom is not a simple historical parenthesis, but the moment when God fulfills with mathematical precision everything promised to His saints. In 30 days He renews the shattered earth to make it worthy of the presence of His Son; in 45 days He resurrects, judges, and positions every group of believers to rule or inhabit the Kingdom. The First Resurrection, completed in five stages from the resurrection of Christ to the martyrs of the Tribulation, reveals that God never forgets any of His own. And the Gentile sheep — nameless, uninstructed believers who risked their lives to protect the persecuted Jewish people — demonstrate that true faith always produces fruit, even if the believer himself does not know it.
