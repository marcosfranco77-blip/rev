XIV

Current Situation at the Start of the Lesson: Israel Has Cried Out, Jesus Descends

The Starting Point
  • ✦
    All Israel Has Come to Faith As a result of the outpouring of the Holy Spirit, the nation of Israel has come to faith in Jesus. All Israel has cried out to Jesus as the condition for His return, thus fulfilling Romans 11:26.
  • ✦
    Jesus Leaves Heaven Toward Earth Jesus is leaving heaven heading toward earth in response to the Jews’ call, fulfilling Paul’s words: “The Deliverer will come out of Zion, And He will turn away ungodliness from Jacob.”
  • ✦
    Israel in Despair Without Hope The Jews in Jerusalem think they have lost hope of mercy — they assume they killed their Messiah and that it is already too late. They are desperate even while crying out to God for mercy.
Isaiah 59–60: The Promise of Redemption
Isaiah 59:19-21 · 60:1-2 "The Redeemer will come to Zion, And to those who turn from transgression in Jacob," Says the LORD. ... "Arise, shine; For your light has come! And the glory of the LORD is risen upon you. For behold, the darkness shall cover the earth, And deep darkness the people; But the LORD will arise over you, And His glory will be seen upon you."

Paul in Romans 11 quotes Isaiah 59, which describes the Second Coming of Jesus in response to Israel coming to faith. This is the moment we have reached: Jesus sets His feet on the earth again to establish the Kingdom for Israel.

The Two Fronts of the War at the Start of the Final Stages
⚔ Front 1 — Jerusalem
  • ✦
    The Antichrist attacks Jerusalem. He has already killed or exiled half the residents. He threatens to exterminate the rest. The other half lives in panic, knowing they killed their Messiah.
⚔ Front 2 — Bozrah / Petra
  • ✦
    The believing Jewish remnant is under divine protection in Bozrah since the middle of the Tribulation. The Antichrist’s forces sent from Babylon have not managed to penetrate the stronghold but persist in their attack.

Both fronts are about to reach their end with the return of Jesus. The Second Coming does not begin in Jerusalem — the first place of return is Bozrah, where the believing Jews are.

XV

Typology: Joseph and His Brothers — The Perfect Picture of Christ and Israel

The Story of Joseph as Typological Prophecy
Event with Joseph Fulfillment in Christ
Joseph was Jacob’s favorite son; his brothers rejected him out of jealousy upon seeing him as a potential ruler. Jesus, the beloved Son of the Father, was rejected by His brothers (Israel) when He offered them the Kingdom under His rule.
The brothers sold Joseph to Gentile merchants (Egyptians) near Dothan. Jesus’ brothers (Israel) sold Him into the hands of the Gentile Romans.
Joseph rose to rule Egypt (Gentile power), second in command under Pharaoh. Jesus became the Lord of the Gentile Church, second position under the authority of God the Father.
God brought famine on the land so that Jacob would seek help in Egypt. God brought the Tribulation on the earth to bring Israel back to Him through Jesus.
The brothers appear before Joseph in desperation. Joseph decides when and how to reveal himself. At the right moment, he “opens their eyes.” First reaction: terror. Israel, desperate under the Antichrist’s attack, cries out to Jesus. Jesus decides the moment of revelation. Israel, upon recognizing Him, first weeps in terror thinking it is too late.
Joseph says: “Do not be grieved nor angry with yourselves because you sold me here; for God sent me before you to preserve life.” It was not you who sent me here, but God. (Genesis 45:3-8) Israel’s rejection of Jesus was part of God’s sovereign plan so that the whole world would be blessed — producing the Church, the bride of Christ, saved from spiritual perdition just as Egypt was saved from famine.
Under Joseph not only would Israel be under his rule, but also all Egypt (the Gentiles). The Kingdom Jesus establishes will include not only Israel but a great host of Gentile saints — the Church.

Israel’s rejection of Jesus at His first coming was part of God’s plan to guarantee that the whole world would be blessed in the end.

XVI

Stage IV of Armageddon: Jesus Destroys the Armies at Bozrah (Isaiah 34)

Isaiah 34: The Lord Descends for Battle at Bozrah
Isaiah 34:1-6 For the LORD is angry with all nations, And His fury is on all their armies; He has utterly destroyed them, He has given them over to the slaughter... All the host of heaven shall be dissolved, And the heavens shall be rolled up like a scroll... For My sword shall be bathed in heaven; Indeed it shall come down on Edom, And on the people of My curse, for judgment... The sword of the LORD is filled with blood... For the LORD has a sacrifice in Bozrah, And a great slaughter in the land of Edom.
Keys to Interpreting Isaiah 34
  • ❶
    The army is the Antichrist’s The Antichrist is the leader of the whole world, so his army is the army of all nations. Isaiah describes the Lord’s wrath against “all nations and all their armies.”
  • ❷
    The context is the Tribulation Verse 4 mentions that “the heavens shall be rolled up like a scroll and all their host shall fall down” — a description consistent with the Tribulation judgments already studied.
  • ❸
    The sword descends from heaven to earth “My sword shall be bathed in heaven” — it has already reached its maximum point. Then it descends: that key word communicates the transition from heaven to earth, the Second Coming of Christ.
  • ❹
    The destruction begins at Bozrah, in Edom The Lord has prepared a sacrifice in Edom, specifically in Bozrah. The bodies of the dead will be found on the dissolved mountain with their blood — reference to the mountain ring surrounding the open area of Petra.
Characteristics of Stage IV
  • ✦
    Only the Lord fights The text does not mention any other person or entity fighting. Only the Lord fights at Bozrah to defend the camp of His people in Petra and destroy all who attack it.
  • ✦
    First destination of the return The Lord descends on a white horse (Revelation 19) with armies following Him and arrives first at Petra, quickly destroying the Antichrist’s forces there.
  • ✦
    The sealed door like Noah’s Ark The Lord supernaturally opens the “door” of protection at Bozrah, just as Noah’s ark had a door that God closed before the flood and opened when the situation was safe. It is the Lord who sealed it and who breaks the seal.
Micah 2:12-13: The Remnant Is Rescued — The “Breaker” Opens the Door
Micah 2:12-13 "I will surely assemble all of you, O Jacob, I will surely gather the remnant of Israel; I will put them together like sheep of the fold, Like a flock in the midst of their pasture; They shall make a loud noise because of so many people. The one who breaks open will come up before them; They will break out, Pass through the gate, And go out by it; Their king will pass before them, With the LORD at their head."
  • ✦
    Bozrah in Hebrew = the fold The word “Bozrah” is the literal Hebrew word for a sheepfold and is also the proper name of the current town of Petra. Micah names the place of Israel’s protection by describing its nature as a protective enclosure.
  • ✦
    The “Breaker” is the Lord The Hebrew word for “the one who breaks open” (breaker) means someone who destroys or breaks the enclosure. It is the Lord Himself who frees those who have been held, leading them out like a shepherd leads sheep from the fold to pasture.
  • ✦
    A quiet exodus — like the Exodus from Sinai It is a peaceful exodus: sheep quietly following their shepherd. Just as Moses led the people of Israel through Sinai, Jesus now leads His people through the open desert back to Jerusalem.
XVII

The Lord Returns from Bozrah to Jerusalem: Isaiah 63 — The Prophet’s Perspective

The Scene: Isaiah on the Walls of Jerusalem
Isaiah 63:1-2 Who is this who comes from Edom, With dyed garments from Bozrah, This One who is glorious in His apparel, Traveling in the greatness of His strength? “I who speak in righteousness, mighty to save.” Why is Your apparel red, And Your garments like one who treads in the winepress?

Isaiah writes from a first-person perspective: he is standing on the walls of Jerusalem during the Antichrist’s attack. He looks east, toward the Mount of Olives, and sees a majestic figure approaching from the east, from Bozrah, with bloodstained clothes.

The Lord’s Answer: Isaiah 63:3-6
Isaiah 63:3-6 "I have trodden the winepress alone, And from the peoples no one was with Me. For I have trodden them in My anger, And trampled them in My fury; Their blood is sprinkled upon My garments, And I have stained all My robes. For the day of vengeance is in My heart, And the year of My redeemed has come. I looked, but there was no one to help... Therefore My own arm brought salvation for Me; And My own fury, it sustained Me."
  • ✦
    The Lord fought alone at Bozrah The Lord Himself confirms that He engaged in the battle completely alone. The blood on His robe comes from the battle at Bozrah — it does not come from heaven but from action on earth.
  • ✦
    First destination confirmed: Bozrah From Isaiah’s perspective, he sees the Lord coming to Jerusalem from Bozrah with His robe already bloodstained. This demonstrates that Bozrah was the first destination of Christ’s return.
Comparison: Revelation 19 and Isaiah 63 Complement Each Other
  • ✦
    Two perspectives of the same event John describes Jesus’ appearance from heaven — robe dipped in blood, eyes like flame, sword in His mouth. Isaiah describes it from the earth — a majestic figure with red garments coming from the east.
  • ✦
    The two descriptions match exactly John’s description (Rev 19:12-15) and Isaiah’s description (Is 63:1-6) complement each other perfectly: splendor, red color, furious wrath, the winepress of the wrath of God.
Will Jesus Find Faith on the Earth at His Return?
Luke 18:7-8 "And shall God not avenge His own elect who cry out day and night to Him, though He bears long with them? I tell you that He will avenge them speedily. Nevertheless, when the Son of Man comes, will He really find faith on the earth?"

The Lord predicted that at His return He would find a world of intense and immovable unbelief and hatred toward God. He does not return because the world acclaims Him in faith, but returns solely for His elect. The strong language of Isaiah 63 reflects the fierce wrath of God against the impiety of the world He finds at His return.

XVIII

The Journey from Bozrah to Jerusalem: Natural or Supernatural?

The Distance and Time
  • ✦
    75 miles in a straight line Bozrah (Petra) to Jerusalem is approximately 75 miles in a straight line. The question is how this group can cover that distance in the available time: the entire final event lasts only three days.
  • ✦
    Supernatural transport Jesus has the ability to move without limitation, and His armies can go wherever He goes. It is assumed that Jesus moves the remnant supernaturally, transporting them quickly to Jerusalem, similar to the Exodus but across the open desert.
Habakkuk 3: The Lord Comes from the South
Habakkuk 3:3-13 (selection) God came from Teman, The Holy One from Mount Paran... His glory covered the heavens, And the earth was full of His praise. His brightness was like the light... The sun and moon stood still in their habitation... You marched through the land in indignation; You trampled the nations in anger. You went forth for the salvation of Your people, For salvation with Your Anointed.
  • ✦
    Teman and Mount Paran = Edom = Bozrah These are geographical references to the ancient names of Edom, where Bozrah is located. The Lord comes to Jerusalem from the south, confirming that Bozrah was His first destination.
  • ✦
    The wrath was not against creation Habakkuk rhetorically asks if God’s wrath was against the rivers, mountains, and seas. The answer (3:12-13): No — the Lord was furious against the armies and nations of the world, not against creation itself.
XIX

Stage V of Armageddon: Jesus Arrives at the Mount of Olives and Defends Jerusalem (Zechariah 14)

Zechariah 14:3-5 — The Final Battle Step by Step
Zechariah 14:3-5 Then the LORD will go forth And fight against those nations, As He fights in the day of battle. And in that day His feet will stand on the Mount of Olives, Which faces Jerusalem on the east. And the Mount of Olives shall be split in two, From east to west, Making a very large valley; Half of the mountain shall move toward the north And half of it toward the south. Then you shall flee through My mountain valley... Thus the LORD my God will come, And all the saints with You.
The Topography of the Place
  • ✦
    The Mount of Olives to the east If the Lord stands on the Mount of Olives, He has a view of the entire city across the Kidron Valley, witnessing the Antichrist’s attack. Jesus approaches from the east (coming from Bozrah) while the Antichrist attacks from the west.
  • ✦
    "His feet will stand" — does not mean He descends there initially Zechariah says Jesus is there that day. It does not imply He descends directly on the Mount of Olives — Isaiah confirms He arrives there coming from Bozrah.
  • ✦
    Position of the three actors Jesus to the east, the Antichrist to the west, and what remains of Jerusalem in the middle. The Lord does not charge downhill fighting kung fu style — He fights remotely, changing the topography.
The Splitting of the Mountain — An Escape Channel
  • ❶
    The mountain splits in two Jesus commands from the Mount of Olives that the mountain be split in two, from east to west. One half moves north and the other south, creating a channel or very large valley that runs eastward from the city.
  • ❷
    Complete protection inside the channel Remember: the world no longer has mountains at this point (destroyed by previous judgments), except the mountain on which Jerusalem sits and the ridge surrounding it. When it splits, it creates a channel with complete protection.
  • ❸
    The Jews flee through the channel to the east The attack comes from the west (left); the escape route is to the east (right) through the new valley, in the direction opposite the enemy. It recalls the crossing of the Red Sea: Israel fleeing from Pharaoh from west to east through a valley of water.
  • ❹
    Only then does Jesus enter the city Zechariah says the Lord enters the city with all His saints following Him. Only after the innocents are removed from the middle of the battle does the Lord advance in His attack.
XX

The Death of the Antichrist: The Sword from the Mouth of Christ (Habakkuk 3 · 2 Thessalonians 2)

Habakkuk 3:13 — The Death of the Leader of Evil
Habakkuk 3:13 (NKJV) You went forth for the salvation of Your people, For salvation with Your Anointed. You struck the head from the house of the wicked, Laying bare from foundation to neck.
  • ✦
    "The head of the house of the wicked" = the Antichrist The Antichrist is the head of the world and of all that is evil. The Lord’s blow literally splits him open from feet to head — a graphic description of the Antichrist’s death by the sword of Christ.
2 Thessalonians 2:8 — Paul’s Confirmation
2 Thessalonians 2:8-9 And then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord will consume with the breath of His mouth and destroy with the brightness of His coming. The coming of the lawless one is according to the working of Satan, with all power, signs, and lying wonders.

The appearance of the Lord will result in the end of the Antichrist. His end comes by the breath of the mouth of Christ. The word (or breath) of Christ is called a “sword” in Scripture.

The Power of the Word of Christ — The Force That Sustains the Universe
Hebrews 4:12 · Hebrews 1:3 For the word of God is living and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing even to the division of soul and spirit, and of joints and marrow... who being the brightness of His glory and the express image of His person, and upholding all things by the word of His power...
  • ✦
    Jesus fights only with His Word The word of the Lord is a force in itself — the only true force in the Universe that holds everything together and sustains all things. It has the power to unite and sustain, as well as to separate and destroy.
  • ✦
    The Word is the “glue” of the Universe Hebrews 1:3 says that Jesus “upholds all things by the word of His power.” The word of Jesus is more than enough to destroy anything, because it is the only thing that holds all things together. At that word, the Antichrist’s body splits open, his armies die and the battle ends quickly.
XXI

The Aftermath of the Battle — The “Blood Tsunami” and the Great Supper of God (Revelation 19:17-21)

Revelation 14: Preview of the Winepress
Revelation 14:18-20 And another angel came out from the altar, who had power over fire, and he cried with a loud cry to him who had the sharp sickle, saying, "Thrust in your sharp sickle and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth, for her grapes are fully ripe." So the angel thrust his sickle into the earth and gathered the vine of the earth, and threw it into the great winepress of the wrath of God. And the winepress was trampled outside the city, and blood came out of the winepress, up to the horses’ bridles, for one thousand six hundred furlongs.

All the blood of thousands (perhaps millions) of human beings is suddenly released, filling the Kidron Valley. A blood tsunami as high as the horses’ bridles flowing 200 miles (1,600 furlongs) down the valley.

Revelation 19:17-21 — The Great Supper of God
Revelation 19:17-21 Then I saw an angel standing in the sun; and he cried with a loud voice, saying to all the birds that fly in the midst of heaven, "Come and gather together for the supper of the great God, that you may eat the flesh of kings... Then the beast was captured, and with him the false prophet who worked signs in his presence... These two were cast alive into the lake of fire burning with brimstone. And the rest were killed with the sword which proceeded from the mouth of Him who sat on the horse. And all the birds were filled with their flesh."
Two Perspectives That Merge
  • ✦
    John’s heavenly perspective John describes the events from his position in heaven — also where the saints of the Church will witness with gratitude. It does not seem that John sees the battle itself, but encounters the aftermath and sees the remains of the carnage.
  • ✦
    The earthly perspective of the prophets The Old Testament prophets give the view from the earth — whether in Bozrah or Jerusalem. Habakkuk and John agree: a violent end with many dead.
  • ✦
    "Mid-heaven" where the birds are The angel calls the birds in the "mid-heaven" (original Greek). Although that term usually denotes outer space, here it is understood as the zenith — the highest point of the visible sky. Ezekiel 39 confirms that God uses birds and beasts to devour the corpses of war.
End of the Age: What Ends with Stage V
  • ✓
    Stage V of the Battle of Armageddon The final battle of the human age comes to its conclusion.
  • ✓
    The Age of the Gentiles The period of Gentile rule over Israel, which began with Nebuchadnezzar, ends definitively.
  • ✓
    The Seven-Year Tribulation Daniel’s 70th Week, which served its purpose of bringing Israel to faith, comes to an end.
  • ✓
    The earth is at rest from war For the first time in seven years of devastation, the world enters rest. The Antichrist’s reign of terror has ceased.
XXII

The Surviving Groups at the End of the War

Who Remained After the War?
Group Status Note
The enemy army Completely destroyed All the Antichrist’s armies have been eliminated by the sword from the mouth of Christ.
The Jews All believers, all saved All Jews on earth are believers and have been rescued from the Antichrist’s attack. All Israel was saved (Rom 11:26).
Tribulation Gentile believers Alive Gentiles who came to faith during the Tribulation and evaded the Antichrist’s persecution. They remain alive at Jesus’ return.
Unbelieving Gentiles Alive Gentiles who were not in the Antichrist’s army and therefore are not killed in the final battle. They remain alive. Their fate will be studied in the next Part (the judgment of the nations).
XXIII

The Fate of the Instigators: The Antichrist and the False Prophet

Their Nature and Condition
  • ✦
    Human beings inhabited by the enemy Both the Antichrist and the False Prophet are human beings. During the second half of the Tribulation they become instruments of Satan: the Antichrist inhabited by Satan after his resurrection in the middle of the Tribulation; the False Prophet probably inhabited by one or more powerful demons.
  • ✦
    When the body dies, the spirits are released We know the Antichrist was killed when Jesus returned (Habakkuk and Paul confirm it). When the bodies die, the spirits are released: Satan and his demons are left free (Jesus will deal with them later) and the souls of the Antichrist and the False Prophet descend to Sheol.
The Journey of the Antichrist’s Soul to Sheol — Isaiah 14
Isaiah 14:9-16 (selection) Hell from beneath is excited about you, To meet you at your coming; It stirs up the dead for you, All the chief ones of the earth... They all shall speak and say to you: 'Have you also become as weak as we? Have you become like us? ... How you are fallen from heaven, O Lucifer, son of the morning! How you are cut down to the ground...'
  • ✦
    Context: Israel already restored This scene in Isaiah occurs when the nation of Israel has already been restored and God has settled His people in their land. “Babylon” (Satan’s kingdom) has ended.
  • ✦
    Reception in hell The kings and princes who preceded him in Sheol rise to receive him with mockery: “Have you also become as weak as we?” They are surrounded by their own kind, with memories of each other and of the life they left behind. This is the reception all great unbelievers receive.
  • ✦
    He will not join his people in burial (v.20) His “people” — the nations that followed him — went to hell at the Second Coming. But the Antichrist will not remain with them, for he will be taken out of Sheol almost immediately.
The Final Destination: The Lake of Fire — The First to Arrive
  • ✦
    Cast alive into the Lake of Fire (Revelation 19:20) The Antichrist and the False Prophet are taken out of Sheol almost immediately and resurrected in new eternal bodies. They are cast directly into the Lake of Fire — being the first beings sent to that place. Just as the Lake of Fire awaits them, it awaits all unbelievers equally, but at the later judgment.
  • ✦
    The false trinity reflects the true even in death Satan, the Antichrist, and the False Prophet formed a false trinity copying the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. Unwittingly, they reflect that counterfeit even in their death.
  • ✦
    The Antichrist as the “firstfruits” of resurrection to eternal death Christ is the firstfruits of the resurrection to eternal life — the first human being who rose in an eternal body that never dies. Similarly, the Antichrist will be the firstfruits of the resurrection among those going to eternal death: the first to be resurrected and cast into the Lake of Fire.
  • ✦
    All human beings are resurrected All human beings are resurrected to new bodies — both believers and unbelievers. The difference is where they spend eternity living.
