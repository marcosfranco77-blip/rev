Current Situation at the Start of the Mid-Trib

Summary Upon Reaching the Mid-Trib
  • ✦
    3½ years elapsedFirst part of the 7 years completed.
  • ✦
    50% of humanity dead4 billion dead, 4 billion alive. First ¼ died (seal 4) and then ⅓ (trumpet 6) of the remaining.
  • ✦
    Earth devastated⅓ uninhabitable, ⅓ of the water undrinkable, ⅓ of the seas unnavigable.
  • ✦
    First two Woes completedThe worst is yet to come with the Third Woe: the 7 Bowls of God's wrath (the Great Tribulation).
  • ✦

The Mid-Trib — Nature and Structure

II

The Angel with the Little Scroll — Chapter 10

Description of the Angel (Rev. 10:1–7)
Revelation 10:1–2 I saw still another mighty angel coming down from heaven, clothed with a cloud. And a rainbow was on his head, his face was like the sun, and his feet like pillars of fire. He had a little book open in his hand. And he set his right foot on the sea and his left foot on the land.
  • ✦
    Mighty heavenly angelSimilar to the angel of chapter 7. So great that he can stand on the sea and the land at the same time. He carries a small book already open.
  • ✦
    The 7 thundersHeaven responds with 7 thunders. John was shown something but was ordered not to write it — to keep it secret. There are mysteries of God that remain sealed.
  • ✦
    The solemn oath"There should be delay no longer" (v.6). At the sound of the seventh trumpet, the mystery of God would be finished, as He declared to His servants the prophets. There will be no more delay.
Connection with Daniel 12 — The Sealed Prophecy
Daniel 12:4, 9 But you, Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book until the time of the end… Go your way, Daniel, for the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end.
  • ✦
    Same vision, different timeIn Daniel 12 a mighty angel appears similarly over land and water. There is great similarity between Dan. 12 and Rev. 10 because one is the continuation of the other within the same prophetic vision.
  • ✦
    Daniel: the first human being to knowDaniel was given the details of the end of the Tribulation (the last 3½ years), but was ordered to seal it. He was the first — and for a long time the only — one who knew how it would all unfold.
  • ✦
    When did the last days begin?Hebrews and James confirm that the last days began when the Messiah was revealed. Daniel's prophecy opens in the last days — in John's time.
  • ✦
    The angel waits 600 yearsThe book of Daniel was kept by the angel ~600 years, and now is given to John — because John is the one to whom is revealed in the last days what was sealed in Daniel's time.
John Eats the Little Scroll — The Analogy of Sweet and Bitter (Rev. 10:9–11)
Revelation 10:9–10 Take and eat it; and it will make your stomach bitter, but it will be as sweet as honey in your mouth… and it was as sweet as honey in my mouth. But when I had eaten it, my stomach became bitter.
  • ✦
    SweetThere is a certain sweetness in knowing that the coming events will finally be revealed. We long for the end of the current age and the beginning of a new one: the coming Kingdom with Christ — what all believers yearn for.
  • ✦
    BitterIn knowing the transition, there are things difficult to swallow. This generation will not pass into the new age without the judgment of the Great Tribulation occurring first.
  • ✦
    Can God avoid this judgment?Literally billions of souls must face judgment. Asking God to fulfill only the blessings but not the curses of disobedience would be asking Him to be unjust and unfaithful. If God were not faithful to His promises of judgment, how could we trust His promise of salvation?
  • ✦
    Must prophesy againv.11 — John must prophesy "about many peoples, nations, tongues, and kings." The little scroll contains the revelation that will fill the remaining chapters of Revelation.
III

The 1,290 Days — The Midpoint Defined with Precision

Daniel 12 Confirms the Mid-Trib
Daniel 12:11 And from the time that the daily sacrifice is taken away, and the abomination of desolation is set up, there shall be one thousand two hundred and ninety days.
  • ✦
    The covenant marks the beginning and the midpointThe Tribulation begins with the signing of the agreement (Daniel 9). That same covenant, when broken at the midpoint, marks the mid-trib with the abolition of sacrifices.
  • ✦
    1,260 days = exactly 3½ yearsA time, times and half a time = 1,260 days. Duration of the second part of the Tribulation, confirmed by Daniel 12:7.
  • ✦
    30 additional daysDaniel 12:11 describes 30 more days until the abomination of desolation is completed and cleansed. This will be seen in detail in chap. 19.
  • ✦
    The 75-day intervalThere are another 45 days after the 30 (30+45=75), before the Millennium begins. It is called "the 75-day interval."
The Revealed Purpose of the Great Tribulation
Daniel 12:7 …when the power of the holy people has been completely shattered, all these things shall be finished.
  • ✦
    The ultimate goal for IsraelThe second part of the Tribulation exists to completely shatter Israel's resistance toward God and toward Jesus specifically. The purpose: to bring Israel to faith in Christ and to fulfill the promises made to that nation.
  • ✦
    The second part "sets the plan in motion"All the events of chapters 11–15 occur simultaneously. They are a moment that fundamentally alters the world to execute the end.
  • ✦
    Daniel wrote firstDaniel was given the details of the second part, even though the first part had not yet been revealed. It proves that God knows the end from the beginning.
IV

The Third Temple — Chapter 11

John Measures the Temple (Rev. 11:1–2)
Revelation 11:1–2 Rise and measure the temple of God, the altar, and those who worship there. But leave out the court which is outside the temple… for it has been given to the Gentiles. And they will tread the holy city underfoot for forty-two months.
  • ✦
    The Temple exists and is functioningIt is confirmed that the Jewish Temple has been rebuilt — something impossible for the last 2,000 years. The sacrifices have been restarted thanks to the treaty signed at the start of the 7 years.
  • ✦
    Standing in spite of everythingDespite wars and supernatural calamities, the Temple is standing. The Lord has protected Israel. It agrees with Rev. 6:6 "do not harm the oil and the wine" — a possible reference to Israel's preservation.
  • ✦
    The Times of the Gentiles have not ended"They will tread the holy city underfoot for 42 months" — classic reference to the mid-trib. The existence of the Temple does not imply the end of Gentile dominion.
Fundamental Changes Presented in Chapters 10–11
  • ✦
    Focus on the Middle EastThe habitable world has been reduced to a small area. The rest is uninhabitable and half the population has died.
  • ✦
    Message for Israel"God is on your side." That the Temple is standing amid so much devastation is a silent message of God's constant mercy toward Israel.
  • ✦
    Temple–Witnesses connectionBoth appear adjacent in the text. During the same first 3½ years that the Temple operates, the Two Witnesses also operate from it. The Witnesses are probably part of the Temple's protection.
V

The Two Witnesses — Ministry, Identity, and Purpose

Description and Powers (Rev. 11:3–6)
Revelation 11:3–6 And I will give power to my two witnesses, and they will prophesy one thousand two hundred and sixty days, clothed in sackcloth… And if anyone wants to harm them, fire proceeds from their mouth… They have power to shut heaven… and to strike the earth with all plagues, as often as they desire.
  • ✦
    1,260 days — first halfThey operate during the first half of the Tribulation. If they had operated in the second, their resurrection would have occurred after the Second Coming of Christ — which makes no sense.
  • ✦
    Clothed in sackclothRough garment, traditional for prophets in mourning or suffering — related to their work of witnessing.
  • ✦
    The same powers as JesusFire from their mouths. Shutting heaven (no rain). Turning waters to blood. Plagues. These are the same interventions Jesus has been making from heaven — now physically visible on earth through two people.
Why Two Witnesses — Legal Foundation
Deuteronomy 19:15 One witness shall not rise against a man concerning any iniquity or any sin that he commits… by the mouth of two or three witnesses the matter shall be established.
  • ✦
    God's legal standardBefore condemning the world, sin must be established by at least two witnesses. These two testify to sin from the beginning — a testimony of condemnation upon the world prior to the final judgment.
  • ✦
    Two possible outcomesThe testimony can bring salvation (repentance) or condemnation (exposure of sin). Only God knows the outcome for each person.
  • ✦
    Church vs. WitnessesThe Church was a witness for salvation. These two are witnesses primarily for condemnation, though salvation remains open.
Identity — The Two Olive Trees of Zechariah 4
Zechariah 4:14 / Revelation 11:4 Zech: "These are the two anointed ones, who stand beside the Lord of the whole earth." — Rev: "These are the two olive trees and the two lampstands standing before the God of the earth."
  • ✦
    Zechariah 4 in its historical contextWritten during the reconstruction of the second Temple under Zerubbabel. The Lord promised that Zerubbabel would finish the Temple, but Zerubbabel never did. The promise remained pending — pointing to a future fulfillment in the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    The "sons of oil"In Hebrew it does not say "the two anointed ones" but "the two sons of oil" — the two anointed by God to finish His work. The lamp and the oil = God's work through His Spirit (not human effort). Zechariah 4:14 would make no sense without Revelation 11.
  • ✦
    Impact on all the earthTheir ministry will impact every person living on the earth. Supernatural powers and divine protection guarantee worldwide reach.
  • ✦
    Speculations about their identityIt has been proposed that they are Moses and Elijah, Enoch and Elijah, symbolic, or even the Twin Towers of 9/11. The text does not clarify — meaning that this is not important information. What matters is their significance and purpose.
The Main Purpose — Why Are These Two Needed?
  • ✦
    The central problemNo one can see what Jesus does in heaven. The inhabitants of the earth only see what happens on earth.
  • ✦
    The solutionTwo physically visible people produce the same events. The world identifies them as the authors of what comes from God — connecting all supernatural activity with God's explanations.
  • ✦
    They eliminate excusesNo one will be able to blame global warming, extraterrestrials, or any other explanation. The supernatural cause is unambiguously identified.
  • ✦
    ScapegoatsGod wants the world to blame these two for everything that has happened and to take it out on them. They are the focus of worldwide hatred for 3½ years. When the mid-trib arrives, their time is up.
  • ✦
    Without global technologyAt mid-trib, there should be no television or internet at a global level. Their works are known not through media but experienced firsthand by every person on earth.
VI

Death, Disgrace, and Resurrection of the Two Witnesses

Their Death at Mid-Trib (Rev. 11:7–10)
Revelation 11:7–8 When they finish their testimony, the beast that ascends out of the bottomless pit will make war against them, overcome them, and kill them. And their dead bodies will lie in the street of the great city which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt, where also our Lord was crucified.
  • ✦
    The beast from the abyssBy killing them he gains enormous worldwide recognition. It is a brief introduction to the character described in detail in coming chapters.
  • ✦
    Jerusalem = "Sodom and Egypt"Sodom: extreme depravity (the time of Lot). Egypt: where idolatry was first introduced into Israel. At this point, Jerusalem is spiritually as corrupt as these cities.
  • ✦
    Unburied · 3½ daysLeft visible to publicly dishonor them. The first global celebration in history: all the inhabitants of the earth celebrate and send gifts to one another, because these two had tormented them. They are hated by the entire world.
Their Resurrection (Rev. 11:11–13)
Revelation 11:11–12 Now after the three-and-a-half days the breath of life from God entered them, and they stood on their feet, and great fear fell on those who saw them. And they heard a loud voice from heaven saying to them, "Come up here." And they ascended to heaven in a cloud, and their enemies saw them.
  • ✦
    The terror of the resurrectionThe world is terrified: the resurrection could mean they are returning to their ministry. Moreover, it confirms that they are true messengers of God.
  • ✦
    God's message to the worldEverything they said and did came from God. The fear should not be based on them but on the One behind them: God. Just as the resurrection of Jesus proved He was sent by God.
  • ✦
    The earthquakeA tenth of Jerusalem collapses, 7,000 men die. The rest are terrified and give glory to the God of heaven.
  • ✦
    Connection with Zechariah 4Zech. 4:14: "the two anointed ones who stand beside the Lord of the whole earth." Their resurrection fulfills the promise that they would have a ministry from God witnessed by all the earth.
Location of Their Ministry on the Timeline of the Three Woes
1st Woe — Trumpet 5 (first half) Demonic scorpions from the abyss. Torment of 5 months without being able to die. The 144,000 and believers protected.
2nd Woe — Trumpet 6 → leads to the mid-trib 200 million demons kill ⅓ of mankind. This Woe leads us directly to the mid-trib. Right here the Two Witnesses are killed and resurrected. Rev. 11:14: "The second woe is past. Behold, the third woe is coming quickly."
3rd Woe — Trumpet 7 (second half = Great Tribulation) The 7 Bowls of God's wrath. It culminates with the Second Coming of Christ.
VII

The Seventh Trumpet — Proclamation of Victory (Rev. 11:15–19)

Heaven Proclaims the Reign of Christ
Revelation 11:15, 17–18 The kingdoms of this world have become the kingdoms of our Lord and of His Christ, and He shall reign forever and ever!… We give You thanks, O Lord God Almighty… because You have taken Your great power and reigned. The nations were angry, and Your wrath has come, and the time of the dead, that they should be judged.
  • ✦
    Corresponds to the 7 BowlsThe Seventh Trumpet contains the judgments of the 7 Bowls, which in their culmination lead to the Second Coming of Christ.
  • ✦
    Great rejoicingThe 24 elders fall down giving thanks and glory to the One who has achieved the victory. It is the anticipated proclamation of Christ's eternal reign.
  • ✦
    The purpose of the glorified bodyThe position of the resurrected saints is to return to the earth conquered by Christ. The physical body was designed for earth, not to remain in heaven.
The True Ark in Heaven (Rev. 11:19)
Revelation 11:19 Then the temple of God was opened in heaven, and the ark of His covenant was seen in His temple. And there were lightnings, noises, thunderings, an earthquake, and great hail.
  • ✦
    The true ArkThere truly is an Ark in heaven, of which Moses received a replica. It becomes visible at the very moment the greatest supernatural disasters are about to be unleashed upon the earth.
  • ✦
    Prelude to the final judgmentsLightnings, voices, thunderings, an earthquake, and great hail — the characteristic prelude announcing what is coming in the second half.
VIII

The Woman and the Dragon — Chapter 12

Hermeneutical Rule: The Sign vs. the Fact
Revelation 12:1 Now a great sign appeared in heaven: a woman clothed with the sun, with the moon under her feet, and on her head a garland of twelve stars.
  • ✦
    A sign announces, it does not describeA sign is an indicator that something is approaching — not that we have already arrived at what it announces. It is like seeing the sign "San Antonio" and saying "we are already in the city." Many interpretive errors come from confusing sign with fact.
  • ✦
    The error of the Catholic ChurchIt interpreted the image as the Virgin Mary, taking it as something real rather than as a sign. It would be like interpreting the sign of the child as literally an infant holding an iron rod.
  • ✦
    Correct interpretation process(1) In the immediate text. (2) In the same book / same author. (3) Back in the Bible.
The Woman = Israel · The Child = the Messiah
Genesis 37:9–10 — Joseph's dream Behold, the sun, the moon, and the eleven stars bowed down to me… Shall your mother and I and your brothers indeed come to bow down to the earth before you?
  • ✦
    The woman = IsraelFather Jacob interpreted Joseph's dream: Jacob = sun, his wife = moon, the brothers = stars. Therefore, the woman clothed with the sun with 12 stars = the nation of Israel. (Also confirmed in Jer. 3:8)
  • ✦
    The child = the MessiahThe sign of the caught-up child represents the Messiah, the King of Kings who will rule all nations with a rod of iron. (Ps. 2:7–9)
  • ✦
    The first great signIt represents Israel producing the Messiah — all of biblical history summarized in a single visual image.
The Dragon = Satan (The Second Sign)
Revelation 12:3–4 And another sign appeared in heaven: behold, a great, fiery red dragon having seven heads and ten horns, and seven diadems on his heads. His tail drew a third of the stars of heaven and threw them to the earth.
  • ✦
    Only a sign — not a photoThis verse does not describe what Satan looks like physically. It is a sign of his power. The stars = demonic angels, his army.
  • ✦
    7 heads, 7 diadems, 10 hornsRelated to Daniel 7. The 10 horns = 10 kings at the end of the age. From among them rises the eleventh (the Antichrist) who overthrows 3. Result: 1 man ruling, 7 puppet kings, 10 territories controlled by Satan.
  • ✦
    Satan: the god of this world2 Cor. 4:4 — "the god of this age has blinded the minds of unbelievers." Satan controls the seven kings and the ten kingdoms. This structure culminates at mid-trib.
History of Satan's Opposition to the Plan of Salvation
Genesis 3:15 — The shortest summary of the Bible And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel.
  • ✦
    Two comings in one verseThe wound in the heel = the cross (first coming). The fatal wound to the head = the final defeat of Satan (second coming).
  • ✦
    Satan is not omniscientHe cannot know who the Messiah will be, so he attacks every possible flank throughout history: Cain kills Abel, fallen angels corrupt humanity (Noah), attempts to annihilate the Jews (Esther, WWII), Herod kills children, he controls Judas.
  • ✦
    Above all, the Messiah is bornAll of this history is summarized in Gen. 3:15: bring forth the Messiah while Satan opposes. It is practically the entire Bible in a single verse.
IX

The Fourth Empire Evolves into the Fourth Beast — Daniel 7

From the Roman Empire to the System of 10 Kings
The original Roman Empire The fourth and most powerful empire of Daniel. It divides into east and west (iron and clay — Dan. 2).
Transformation — Imperialist alliance The remnants of the Roman Empire shape the present world. Collectively, all countries maintain the reign of the Gentiles, preventing Israel from having its promised kingdom.
At the end of the age — 10 kings The kingdom takes its final form of 10 kings dominating the entire planet. Daniel 2: the 10 toes of the statue. Daniel 7: the 10 horns on the head of the beast.
The eleventh — the Antichrist He rises among the 10 and overthrows 3. Final result: 1 man ruling + 7 puppet kings + 10 territories belonging to Satan. (Dan. 7:8, 24)
Mid-Trib — reference from Dan. 7:25 "He shall speak pompous words against the Most High, and shall persecute the saints of the Most High… the saints shall be given into his hand for a time and times and half a time." Total control of the world under Satan through his power structure.
X

The Battle in Heaven — Satan Cast Out Forever

Michael Casts Out Satan (Rev. 12:7–12)
Revelation 12:9–10, 12 So the great dragon was cast out… the accuser of our brethren, who accused them before our God day and night, has been cast down… Woe to the inhabitants of the earth and the sea! For the devil has come down to you, having great wrath, because he knows that he has a short time.
  • ✦
    Not a historical accountThis war is different: Satan loses access to the throne of God forever. Until this moment he has had access to accuse — as confirmed by Job 1:6 where Satan presents himself before God.
  • ✦
    Heaven rejoicesSatan is overcome by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of the testimony of the saints who did not love their lives to the death.
  • ✦
    But woe to the earth!At the same time heaven rejoices, the earth enters its darkest period. Satan descends with great wrath, knowing he has little time.
Satan's Panic and Wrath
  • ✦
    The ultimatumThis battle puts Satan on notice that the time is coming. What he has always feared is about to come to pass. He reacts with great fear and wrath upon the only ground remaining to him: the earth.
  • ✦
    The earth suffers as never beforeAt a level never seen before. If this had happened earlier in history, it would have brought great suffering prematurely — God's wisdom in allowing it only at this specific moment.
  • ✦
    The primary targetHe focuses on believers in Jesus and those aligned with God in general. These become his primary target.
  • ✦
    The fundamental change of the mid-tribBefore, Satan attacked the inhabitants in general. Now, having been cast out of heaven, his attacks are unleashed with full intensity specifically upon Israel and believers.
XI

The Remnant of Israel — Supernatural Protection in Petra

The Flight into the Desert (Rev. 12:6, 14–16)
Revelation 12:14 But the woman was given two wings of a great eagle, that she might fly into the wilderness to her place, where she is nourished for a time and times and half a time, from the presence of the serpent.
  • ✦
    The wings of an eagleExodus 19:4 provides the key: "I bore you on eagles' wings and brought you to Myself." God carried Israel supernaturally through the desert — 200 miles, including the elderly, women and children, day and night without stopping. Now He repeats it.
  • ✦
    1,260 days of protectionThe remnant remains protected throughout the entire second half of the Tribulation, in the place prepared by God with guaranteed food and water.
  • ✦
    The river — a failed attackThe enemy pours out "water like a river" to sweep away Israel. But the earth opens its mouth and swallows the river supernaturally — like the deliverance at the Red Sea before Pharaoh's army.
  • ✦
    Everything happens in an instantSatan is cast from heaven, immediately comes with great wrath to destroy the remnant, which at the same time is protected by God. All simultaneous at mid-trib.
Petra — Bozrah — The Impregnable Fortress
Micah 2:12 / Isaiah 33:16 Mic: "…I will surely assemble all of you, O Jacob, I will surely gather the remnant of Israel; I will put them together like sheep of Bozrah…" — Isa: "He will dwell on high; his place of defense will be the fortress of rocks; bread will be given him, his water will be sure."
  • ✦
    Petra fulfills this literallyLocated in Jordan, in the desert not far from Jerusalem. A high place in the mountains. Ruins of the Arab empire of the Nabataeans (400–106 BC).
  • ✦
    The Siq CanyonEntry through a very narrow canyon (famous from Indiana Jones). Easy to defend against people on foot or on horseback.
  • ✦
    Flash floodsThe area has deep channels prone to flash floods from rainstorms — a natural threat to any intruder. There are currently warnings in the desert about this danger.
  • ✦
    3½ years of divine provisionThe Lord protects, feeds, and provides water to the remnant in this fortress throughout the entire Great Tribulation, in the presence of the enemy who cannot reach them.
The Remnant — God's Eternal Promise to Israel
Romans 11:2–5 God has not cast away His people… I have reserved for Myself seven thousand men who have not bowed the knee to Baal. Even so then, at this present time there is a remnant according to the election of grace.
  • ✦
    Who is the remnant?Jews who believe in Jesus. There has always been a group in the OT (Isa. 37:31–32), in the NT (Paul himself), and there will be one during the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    Why God must protect themIf God allowed Satan to eliminate the remnant, He would go against His eternal promise to always preserve it. The Lord will never allow faith to cease within Israel.
  • ✦
    Who specifically flees?Jews primarily in Jerusalem who believe the testimony of Jesus in Matthew 24:16. This flight is a test of faith: they believe the Word of God and act. Jews who do not believe in Jesus do not read or believe this warning — and do not flee.
  • ✦
    Rescued vs. JudgedGod here separates the believing Jews, taking them to Petra, and leaving the unbelievers in Jerusalem. An act of sovereign mercy toward those who have faith.
Mid-Trib According to Jesus — Matthew 24:15–21
Matthew 24:15–16, 21 Therefore when you see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing in the holy place (whoever reads, let him understand), then let those who are in Judea flee to the mountains… For then there will be great tribulation, such as has not been since the beginning of the world until this time, no, nor ever shall be.
XII

The Rest of the Offspring — Those Who Were Left Behind

Two Persecuted Groups (Rev. 12:17)
Revelation 12:17 And the dragon was enraged with the woman, and he went to make war with the rest of her offspring, who keep the commandments of God and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.
  • ✦
    Who is "the rest"?Those who are NOT in Petra — two distinct groups: (1) Those who keep the commandments of God, and (2) Those who have the testimony of Jesus Christ.
  • ✦
    Group 1 — Unbelieving orthodox JewsDevout people who keep the law of Moses and know the true God, but do not recognize Jesus as the Messiah. Therefore they did not read or believe the warning of Matthew 24. They will be persecuted and many will be killed.
  • ✦
    Group 2 — Gentiles converted during the TribulationThose who have the testimony of Jesus Christ — converted during the Tribulation. There is no promise from God to preserve a remnant group of believing Gentiles. God's plan for the Second Coming depends on Jews, not Gentiles.
An Important Distinction — Commandments of God vs. Testimony of Christ
  • ✦
    It is unusual to call a Christian thisIn the NT, those who follow Jesus keep the commandments of Jesus, not the commandments of God of the OT (the law of Moses). This suggests they are two different groups.
  • ✦
    The distinction marks different behaviorsThe Pharisees: fasting, stoning the adulteress, an eye for an eye. The disciples: turning the other cheek, forgiving 70×7, loving the enemy. These are two distinct ethical systems.
  • ✦
    Gentiles are not in PetraThe majority of these converted Gentiles will die as martyrs during the Great Tribulation. However, some survive until the end of the Tribulation.
Groups persecuted during the Tribulation ① Believing Jews — remnant protected in Petra. ② Unbelieving orthodox Jews — persecuted. ③ Believing Gentiles converted during the Tribulation — persecuted. ④ Others (???). The Great Tribulation is called that because of what Satan does during this period — the bowl judgments come only at the end.
✦

Chapters 10–12 describe the most critical moment in human history: the Mid-Trib. The little scroll of Daniel finally opens, revealing what was sealed for 600 years. The Two Witnesses complete their ministry as the last visible witnesses of God before the world, dying and rising to confirm their divine authority. The battle in heaven casts Satan out of the throne of God forever, unleashing his final wrath upon the earth — but the Lord has already prepared the refuge in Petra for His remnant. Everything that occurs is under the sovereign control of God, who protects His own, fulfills His eternal promises, and advances inexorably toward the final victory of Christ.
