I

Summary: Creation, the Earth, and Government in the Kingdom

Changes in the Natural World
  • •
    Return to Eden
    The natural world will begin to return to the state it knew before the fall of Adam, with a partial reversal of the curses pronounced upon the earth.
  • •
    Transformed animal relationships
    The animal kingdoms will no longer experience predator-prey relationships. Humans will not be threatened by animals nor they by us. We will be able to have pets of any species.
  • ⚠
    Exception: the serpent
    The serpent will not be reversed and will continue to crawl on the ground. It is a permanent memorial of Satan’s deception, who although bound, remains for a time.
Geography and Israel
  • •
    Abundance without toil
    The land of Israel will produce abundant crops without effort, with mountains having streams at their summits, making water plentiful everywhere.
  • •
    New river from the Temple
    A new river will flow from the temple dividing toward the Mediterranean Sea and the Dead Sea. Upon reaching the Dead Sea, it will make its waters fresh and produce many varieties of fish.
  • •
    Expanded borders of Israel
    Israel will return to its land with enormously expanded borders, approaching the limits of Solomon’s time. Jerusalem will be the world capital of government.
Government of the Kingdom
Jewish Government
  • •
    The Jewish nation will be governed by David and the twelve apostles, together with the glorified nation of Israel.
Gentile Government
  • •
    The Gentile nations will be governed by the glorified saints of the Church included in the Rapture, and the Tribulation martyrs, in a structure still unknown.
Romans 13:3–4 “For rulers are not a terror to good works, but to evil. Do you want to be unafraid of the authority? Do what is good, and you will have praise from the same. For he is God’s minister to you for good. But if you do evil, be afraid; for he does not bear the sword in vain; for he is God’s minister, an avenger to execute wrath on him who practices evil.”
Isaiah declares that Jesus will answer instantly any invocation from anywhere — a direct and perfect communication with the King. Government exists because sin exists, and without offenders there would be no need for authority.
II

The Five Groups of Believers Entering the Kingdom

Classification of the Groups
Group Type of Body Function in the Kingdom
① Glorified saints of the Church (Rapture) Glorified, sinless, eternal Govern Gentile nations
② David + 12 Apostles + Glorified Israel Glorified, sinless, eternal Govern the Jewish nation
③ Tribulation Martyrs Glorified, sinless, eternal Govern with Christ
④ Surviving Jewish believers (without dying) Natural / earthly with sin Be governed; marry and reproduce
⑤ Surviving Gentile believers (without dying) Natural / earthly with sin Be governed; marry and reproduce
Glorified Bodies
  • a
    Sinless and eternal
    The first three groups enter with glorified bodies. They have no sin, do not need to be governed, and execute the will of Jesus perfectly.
  • b
    Government with a rod of iron
    Under Christ’s direction, these rulers ensure the world is governed with a rod of iron in perfect justice.
Natural Bodies
  • a
    Condition of entry
    Groups 4 and 5 never died during the Tribulation. They enter with natural bodies inherited from Adam, still possessing sin. They are saved by faith but are not sinless.
  • b
    Marriage and reproduction
    These two groups can marry and reproduce to create new generations of humanity during the Kingdom.
  • c
    The “true Millennials”
    The new generations born in the Kingdom will also possess natural bodies with a sinful nature and, unlike their parents, will not be born believing.
III

Sin Contained: Government with a Rod of Iron

Sin Exists but Does Not Reign
  • •
    Controlled sin
    Descendants born in the Kingdom will make mistakes. But when they try to act on sinful thoughts, the government will intervene to prevent those thoughts from growing.
  • •
    The true meaning of “rod of iron”
    No sin will have the opportunity to grow or cause harm because it will be contained quickly and perfectly. Government exists precisely for this.
A Ruling Superclass
  • •
    The glorified class
    For 1,000 years the world will be ruled by a class of humanity with glorified bodies that do not die, cannot sin, and do not procreate.
  • •
    The natural class
    Those we govern will live in natural bodies that can die, possess sin, and can reproduce. Over time they will repopulate the entire earth.
IV

Death in the Kingdom — Analysis of Isaiah 65:20

Demographic Calculation and the Need for Death Control
If only 10,000 people entered the Kingdom in natural bodies and 2% of couples had one child per year with no mortality, after 1,000 years the population would be 4 trillion. Therefore God controls death to manage the population.
Isaiah 65:20 (rearranged according to Hebrew poetry) (1) No more shall there be in it an infant who lives but a few days,
(3) For the child shall die one hundred years old;
(2) Nor an old man who has not fulfilled his days;
(4) But the sinner being one hundred years old shall be accursed.
The Four Rules of Death
  • a
    No infant mortality
    There will be no children who die young. There will be no fatal diseases or tragedies in the early years of life. God guarantees the life of every child.
  • b
    Minimum 100 years for all
    The Lord guarantees that every person born in the Kingdom will live at least 100 years. Dying at 100 will be considered an early death, as it will be considered dying young.
  • c
    Believers: indefinite life
    Those who believe in Jesus as Lord become immune to death before 100 years and can live hundreds of years. Life expectancy returns to the Genesis era (Methuselah, 969 years; Adam, 930).
  • d
    Unbelievers: die exactly at 100
    The 100th birthday is the decisive point. The sinner one hundred years old shall be accursed — falls under divine judgment (eternal judgment in hell). It is an all-or-nothing proposition.
God Controls Life and Death
  • •
    Divine control always present
    As Job declares, the day of birth and the day of death were chosen by God before our birth. God has always had control of life and death.
  • •
    Resurrection of those born in the Kingdom
    Believers born in the Kingdom must receive new bodies at some point. The Bible is silent on exactly when. Speculated possibilities are: at 100 years, at any time thereafter, or at the end of the Kingdom.
  • •
    The Kingdom is for believers
    The precision and severity of this judgment sends a clear message: the Kingdom is to be enjoyed by believers. Jesus’ style of government is consistent: ruling with a rod of iron.
V

How Is Unbelief Possible with Christ Ruling?

Three Reasons Unbelievers Will Exist in the Kingdom
  • a
    Reason 1: Miracles do not produce saving faith
    Jesus walked the earth showing power and great miracles, and yet people did not believe. Israel in the wilderness saw the plagues, the Red Sea, and the presence of God at Sinai, and yet Hebrews calls them “a generation of unbelievers.” Salvation is believed in the heart, not only with the mind. An unconverted heart cannot discern God’s spiritual world until the Holy Spirit opens it by the new birth.
  • b
    Reason 2: No evidence of the previous world
    Unbelievers in the Kingdom were born during that time and do not know another era. There will be no physical evidence of previous ages (neither of Adam, Noah, the crucifixion, the Tribulation). The renewal of the earth left no archaeological evidence. They will take everything for granted, just as today people deny belief in ancient stories.
  • c
    Reason 3: Unbelievers will never see Jesus
    Ezekiel 43 declares that the Lord dwells in the Most Holy Place of the Temple without interruption during the full 1,000 years. The unconverted will never see Jesus nor have access to the inner courts. They will only know by testimony that the Lord resides in the temple, but they will have no visible proof of it.
VI

The Shekinah Glory of God Returns to the Temple

History of the Shekinah Glory
Exodus Era
The Shekinah glory of God manifested as a cloud and pillar of fire in the wilderness, as a cloud on Mount Sinai and covering the tabernacle when Moses spoke with the Lord.
Departure — Before the Babylonian invasion
When the Lord delivered dominion of the nations to the Gentiles, the glory of God departed through the east gate of the temple, toward the Mount of Olives and disappeared. It has not returned since.
Age of the Gentiles (present)
The Shekinah glory of God has been absent throughout the Age of the Gentiles. The current temple lacks the manifest presence of God.
Beginning of the Millennial Kingdom
The same glory returns to the Temple in the same way it departed: through the east side. Jesus will not have the appearance He had in the Gospels, but His appearance of Shekinah glory.
During the 1,000 years
The Lord dwells in the Most Holy Place without interruption. The glory is only visible at certain times: on Sabbaths, the feasts, and new moons. The rest of the days the door to the inner courts is closed.
Differentiated Access to the Glory
  • ✓
    Believers: access permitted
    Only believers are allowed to enter the inner courts and see the glory of the Lord during the appointed feasts. In the past, Gentiles could not enter; in the Kingdom this changes.
  • ✗
    Unbelievers: no access
    The unconverted will never be permitted to enter the courts nor see the glory of the Lord. They only see from outside that there is a temple on a high mountain in Jerusalem and depend on the testimony of others.
Ezekiel 43:4–7 “And the glory of the LORD came into the temple by way of the gate which faces toward the east... And He said to me, ‘Son of man, this is the place of My throne and the place of the soles of My feet, where I will dwell in the midst of the children of Israel forever.’”
VII

Faith: The Only and Permanent Means of Salvation

Why Jesus Is Not Visible in the Kingdom
  • •
    Salvation by “sight” would nullify faith
    If Jesus were visible in His glorified form to the unbelieving world, everyone would recognize Him, but that recognition would not be based on faith. It would be self-evident truth and would not result in salvation.
  • •
    Every knee will bow — in judgment
    The day Jesus is visible to all in all His glory will be the day of final judgment. At that moment every knee will bow and every tongue confess — even those under the earth — but that confession will not bring salvation.
Hebrews 11:1 “Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.”
The Appearance of the Glorified
  • •
    Not dramatically different
    Our physical build must reflect what we have now, though without sin, disease, weakness, or old age. We should expect that our appearance does not nullify the faith of unbelievers.
  • •
    Jesus lived unrecognized
    Jesus lived a sinless life and yet none of the Jews, not even His own brothers who lived in the same house, believed His claims. If the sinless Lord could live beside unbelievers, so can we.
  • •
    Only the Holy Spirit converts
    In the end, unbelievers will only be moved by the Holy Spirit for genuine conversion. No external evidence — neither miracles, nor glorified presence, nor our sinlessness — substitutes for the work of the Spirit.
Philippians 2:9–11 “Therefore God also has highly exalted Him and given Him the name which is above every name, that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those in heaven, and of those on earth, and of those under the earth, and that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.”
Paul lists three locations: above the earth, on the earth, and under the earth — including souls awaiting eternal judgment. Even they will confess Christ, but will remain in judgment because their confession will be evident recognition, not faith.
VIII

The Quality of Daily Life in the Kingdom

Relationships and Social Joy
  • •
    Reunification of relationships
    The glorified will reestablish relationships with those they knew on earth and establish new relationships with believers of all ages.
  • •
    Surprises in the Kingdom
    Jesus says we will be surprised by who makes it into the Kingdom and who does not. Some who lived terrible lives believed at the end; some with known ministries never knew the Lord personally.
  • •
    1,000 years of tourism
    We will have 1,000 years to meet saints of history: have coffee with Noah, talk with Moses about the parting of the Red Sea, ask Aaron what he was thinking when he made the golden calf.
A Life of Full Satisfaction
  • •
    Everything we like, nothing we don’t
    A life full of satisfaction instead of disappointment. With meaning and purpose. With rhythm and stability that eliminates fear. Without the prospect of aging in decay.
  • •
    Work that is enjoyed
    Our days will be filled with work we enjoy, hobbies that do not harm us or others, and possessions that never fade.
  • •
    Grace for the unbeliever
    For the unbeliever, the same will be true for at least their first 100 years. There will be grace for them during that time. And in the midst of that almost perfect world, they will be told they must believe in Jesus if they want to continue.
The Paradox: Prosperity Makes Faith Harder
What we assume
A world so grand under Jesus’ perfect government should facilitate obtaining faith. With evidences of divine blessing everywhere, everyone should believe.
The biblical reality
The reality will be exactly the opposite. When the world experiences such bliss, faith will be harder. As today: when people feel comfortable, they find few reasons to seek repentance.
IX

Jerusalem Covered by Glory: Isaiah 4

The Cloud and Fire over Mount Zion
Isaiah 4:2–6 “In that day the Branch of the LORD shall be beautiful and glorious... And there will be a tabernacle for shade in the daytime from the heat, for a place of refuge, and for a shelter from storm and rain.”
  • •
    Cloud by day
    A persistent cloud will cover the mount during the day providing constant shade and keeping the area cool.
  • •
    Fire by night
    At night there will be flaming fire in the sky to provide constant illumination over the temple.
  • •
    Protection from storms
    The combined effect will prevent storms or rain from falling on this zone — like a supernatural protective roof.
Dimensions of the Holy Mount
  • •
    The great plain
    The temple is on a great mountain with a flat plain at the top of 50 square miles, which also includes Jerusalem.
  • •
    The city of Jerusalem
    Jerusalem will also be included in that plain, with 10 square miles.
  • •
    The temple itself
    The temple itself is almost 1 square mile. It is the pinnacle of government and the presence of God on earth.
  • •
    Holiness in Jerusalem
    Everyone who remains in Zion will be called holy. There will be no unbelievers in Israel, although there will be natural non-glorified Jews.
X

The Life and Function of the Gentiles in the Kingdom

Gentiles in the Temple (Isaiah 56)
Isaiah 56:6–7 “Also the sons of the foreigner who join themselves to the LORD, to serve Him... Even them I will bring to My holy mountain, and make them joyful in My house of prayer. Their burnt offerings and their sacrifices will be accepted on My altar; for My house shall be called a house of prayer for all nations.”
  • •
    Gentile priests
    Gentile believers will become workers and probably priests in the temple, offering sacrifices before the altar. This is new: in previous times only Jews could be priests.
  • •
    House of prayer for all peoples
    In the Kingdom, the temple will be known as a house of prayer for all peoples. Gentiles will also serve there, marking an unprecedented change in the history of the temple.
The Return of Israel and the Gentile Role (Isaiah 66)
Isaiah 66:18–21 “For I will set a sign among them, and send those who escape... Then they shall bring all your brethren from all nations as an offering to the LORD... And I will also take some of them for priests and Levites,” says the LORD.
  • •
    The saints as a sign
    The surviving glorified ones sent to govern the nations will be a sign to those nations by their testimony of justice and faith.
  • •
    Reversal of the Diaspora
    Gentile believers will bring the Jews back to their land in honor, reversing previous dispersions. Where nations once expelled Israel, now they bring them back as an offering to the Lord.
Servants of Honor in Israel (Isaiah 14:1–2)
Isaiah 14:1–2 “For the LORD will have mercy on Jacob... Strangers will join them... And the house of Israel will possess them as servants and maids in the land of the LORD.”
Some Gentiles will remain in the land of Israel as servants to Israel. Not in a position of mistreatment or slavery, but in a position of honor and privilege that gives them the opportunity to serve directly in the kingdom, in the very country where Jesus resides with His people.
Consequence for Disobedience — The Feast of Tabernacles
Zechariah 14:16–18 “And it shall come to pass that everyone who is left of all the nations... shall go up from year to year to worship the King... and to keep the Feast of Tabernacles. And it shall be that whichever family of the earth does not go up... there will be no rain on them.”
  • •
    Mandatory annual worship
    All nations will be required to come to the temple for the Feast of Tabernacles (the final feast of the Jewish calendar) every year.
  • •
    Drought as judgment
    The only response from the Lord to disobedience will be drought. Since the land produces without effort because water is abundant (no irrigation systems needed), a drought will have an immediate and devastating effect — an effective deterrent.
Life in the Kingdom according to Isaiah 65:21–23
Isaiah 65:21–23 “They shall build houses and inhabit them; they shall plant vineyards and eat their fruit. They shall not build and another inhabit; they shall not plant and another eat; for as the days of a tree, so shall be the days of My people, and My elect shall long enjoy the work of their hands. They shall not labor in vain, nor bring forth children for trouble.”
Security of possessions
No one will raid the land or take away the house or its fruits. Possessions will belong to each one to keep without fear, given as inheritance by the Lord. The harvest will never fail to be received.
Longevity and family
The days of life will be like those of a tree (some live +2,000 years). There will be no infant mortality. Parents will obtain the joy of raising their children without fear of losing them. Life expectancy will never end.
XI

The Great Silences — What the Scriptures Do Not Reveal

Questions without Scriptural Answer
  • ?
    What level of technology?
    Will we enjoy current comforts? Will we be rescued from the hectic nature of life and constant social media connection?
  • ?
    Will there be entertainment?
    Will there be book writing, sports, art? Can we move to new countries? Will there be money and economy? The Scriptures do not cover it.
  • ?
    Resurrection of those born in the Kingdom
    This is one of the great silences of the Bible. No Scripture has been found addressing when believers born in the Kingdom will receive their glorified bodies.
  • ?
    Details of Judaism in the Kingdom
    There is some mystery about whether all children of Jewish believers will always come to faith. Ezekiel mentions death among the families of the priests, which adds complexity.
The Divine Intention of Mystery
  • •
    Endless surprises
    Perhaps the Lord has intentionally left many things unanswered so that Kingdom life will be an endless series of surprises.
  • •
    Next studies
    In the next class the operations of the temple will be analyzed, why the sacrifices return, worship in the Kingdom and a tour of the temple building. Then the events that end the 1,000 years.
✦

The Millennial Kingdom reveals the perfect wisdom of God in designing a world where sin exists but does not reign, where faith remains the only path to salvation, and where the glory of Christ dwells in the Most Holy Place awaiting the day of final judgment. The life of the glorified will be one of joy, service, and eternal discovery — while for those still living in natural bodies, 100 years of grace offer the definitive invitation to believe. Everything the Bible describes about the Kingdom confronts us with this truth: this world is only a pale shadow of what the Lord has prepared for those who love Him.
