The Temple of the Kingdom according to Ezekiel
  • ✦
    Nine chapters of detail
    Across nine chapters (Ezekiel 40–48), Ezekiel provides a complete tour of the building, its dimensions, its operation, and its priests.
  • ✦
    Does the Tribulation Temple continue?
    Daniel 12 indicates that the Tribulation Temple will be cleansed of the Beast’s image in 30 days. But the Kingdom Temple will be a new one, far better than that, with unprecedented dimensions in all of history.
  • ✦
    Purpose of the temple
    Its size reflects the need for millions of people from all over the world to visit it. This temple is not only for Israel: it will serve all humanity, not only the Jewish people.
Inaugural Vision of the Temple (Ezekiel 40:2–4)
Ezekiel 40:2–4 “In the visions of God He took me into the land of Israel and set me on a very high mountain; on it toward the south was something like the structure of a city. He took me there, and behold, there was a man whose appearance was like the appearance of bronze. He had a line of flax and a measuring rod in his hand... ‘Son of man, look with your eyes and hear with your ears, and fix your mind on everything I show you; for you were brought here so that I might show them to you. Declare to the house of Israel everything you see.’”
The temple is located on a high mountain and the structure is so massive that it looks like a city in itself — very different from the current topography of Jerusalem. Ezekiel is instructed to measure with precision so that we can appreciate the scale.
XIII

Architectural Tour: Dimensions of the Millennial Temple

General Structure — A Perfect Square
  • ❶
    Outer wall with three gates
    The first wall is 10 feet high. Passing through it leads to the first gate — the main entrance is the east gate, which measures 100 feet high and 50 feet wide. There are no gates on the west.
  • ❷
    The gate building: symbolism of the numbers
    The gate forms a hall with 6 chambers (3 on each side). The measurements in cubits increase in increments of 5 and 6: the number 5 represents grace, the number 6 the sinful man. Symbolically, the gate declares: “here sin meets grace.”
  • ❸
    First outer court
    A large open court inside the first gate. Tall palm trees mark the entrances and exits so that millions of people can easily orient themselves from a distance.
  • ❹
    Second gate — inner court
    It also has tall palm trees and tables where the priests perform the sacrifices. In the center of the inner court there is a great altar with steps leading to the temple.
  • ❺
    Guard at the entrance
    The fact that there are guards at the entrance indicates that there is still sin in the world and that the holiness of the place needs to be protected.
The Interior of the Temple
  • ✦
    No door or separating veil
    Unlike previous temples, there is no door or veil separating the Holy Place from the Most Holy Place. Symbolic access to God is no longer blocked.
  • ✦
    Holy Place (⅔ of the temple): one single piece of furniture
    In the first ⅔ of the temple there is only the altar of incense. The other pieces of furniture (lampstand, table of showbread, etc.) have been removed because they represented Jesus — and now Jesus is present. The incense remains because it represents intercession, which is still valid.
  • ✦
    Most Holy Place (⅓ of the temple): completely empty
    Ezekiel declares that the Most Holy Place has no furniture — it is completely empty. Only the Shekinah Glory of Jesus dwells there.
Additional Buildings and Elements
  • ✦
    Priestly chambers
    They surround the temple on the north and south sides. Here the priests begin their workday, changing clothes before and after service.
  • ✦
    Observation galleries
    There are galleries for viewing the burnt offerings in the temple court.
  • ?
    Mysterious building (“Separate Place”)
    A third building at the back left of the temple whose purpose is not defined in Ezekiel — only its measurements are known.
  • ✦
    The river from the temple
    A small river emerges from under the foundations of the temple, flows through the court through the east gates, and grows until it becomes wide and deep. It divides toward the Mediterranean Sea and the Dead Sea, turning the latter into fresh water. It symbolizes the living water that proceeds from the presence of Christ.
XIV

Christ in the Temple: The Departure and Glorious Return

Christ: The Only Physical Manifestation of God
Colossians 1:15 “He is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn over all creation.”
Whenever there has been a physical manifestation of God within His creation — the Angel of the Lord, the Shekinah Glory — it has been through the second person of the deity: the pre-incarnate Jesus. In Moses’ Tabernacle, Christ occupied the Most Holy Place as the Shekinah above the ark. In Solomon’s temple, He entered with greater majesty at its dedication by Solomon.
The Departure of the Glory — Ezekiel’s Journey (600 B.C.)
Step 1 — Leaves the Most Holy Place
The glory of the Lord left the Most Holy Place, moving through the Holy Place toward the entrance door. As it did so, the temple was filled with the cloud of the glory of God (Ezekiel 10:4).
Step 2 — Stops at the eastern gate
The glory moves to the outer east gate, escorted by cherubim, and pauses (Ezekiel 10:18–19).
Step 3 — Over the Mount of Olives
The glory crosses the Kidron Valley eastward and settles on the top of the Mount of Olives, where it pauses again (Ezekiel 11:23).
Step 4 — Disappears (and has not returned)
From the Mount of Olives, the glory departed permanently. Since then it has not returned in any of its forms to this day. Jesus entered Jerusalem in human form, but never in Shekinah glory to occupy the Most Holy Place.
Acts 1 — Jesus ascends from the same place
The disciples see the risen Jesus ascend from the Mount of Olives. The angels declare that He will return in the same manner they saw Him depart.
Beginning of the Kingdom — The Return to the Temple
Jesus returns to the Mount of Olives, splitting it in two, delivers His people, destroys the Antichrist and his forces, and enters the temple through the east gate as the Shekinah Glory to dwell there forever (Ezekiel 43:1–9).
The East Gate Closed Forever
Ezekiel 44:1–2 “This gate shall be shut; it shall not be opened, and no man shall enter by it, because the LORD God of Israel has entered by it; therefore it shall be shut.”
  • ✦
    Symbolism of the closed gate
    The outer east gate remains closed forever. Its closure symbolizes that the work of redemption occurred once and for all. Entering and exiting would imply repeating the act of redemption.
  • ✦
    David’s office
    Since the outer east gate is permanently closed, David, the prince of the Lord, uses that closed space as his office in the temple: he enters at dawn to conduct business and leaves at the end of the day.
  • ✦
    No one will have coffee with Jesus
    Jesus never leaves the temple, for leaving and returning would suggest He still needs to qualify Himself. He resides in the temple out of sight of the unbelieving world, in His Glory, without condescending to sin.
How Worshipers Can See the Glory
  • ✦
    North entrance → south exit (or vice versa)
    Ezekiel establishes that worshipers enter by the north or south gate and exit by the opposite one. This forces them to cross the court and see for an instant the radiance of the Glory in the Most Holy Place (Ezekiel 46:9).
  • ✦
    Days of access — Believers only
    Only believers may enter the inner courts. The permitted times are: Sabbaths, new moon festivals, Passover, and the Feast of Tabernacles. The rest of the days the inner gate is closed.
  • ✗
    Unbelievers: exterior access only
    Unbelievers may come to the outer court and make sacrifices with the help of the priests, but they will never see the glory of the Lord (Ezekiel 44:9).

The Feasts of the Kingdom — From Seven to Two

The Seven Feasts of the Law of Moses and Their Fulfillment
Feast Season What it represented Status in the Kingdom
① Passover Spring The Lamb who died for our sins (First Coming) ✅ Remains — still points to redemption
② Unleavened Bread Spring Christ carried our sin, removing it from our midst Fulfilled — not celebrated in the Kingdom
③ Firstfruits Spring The resurrection of Christ, firstfruit of the sacrifice Fulfilled — not celebrated in the Kingdom
④ Pentecost Summer The Holy Spirit dwelling among us — the Church Age Fulfilled — the Church no longer exists in that form
⑤ Feast of Trumpets Fall The Rapture at the last trumpet Fulfilled — the Rapture has already occurred

The Two Feasts of the Kingdom
  • ❶
    Passover — Eternal Redemption
    It represents Jesus’ redemption through His sacrifice on the cross as our Lamb. It still has full spiritual meaning because the work of the cross is the eternal foundation of salvation.
  • ❷
    Feast of Tabernacles — Lived Fulfillment
    It represents God dwelling among His people — something that in the Kingdom is experienced literally. Christ dwells in the temple in the midst of humanity.
Other Moments of Worship
  • ✦
    Sabbaths and new moons
    In addition to the two annual feasts, worshipers may go to the temple on Sabbaths and new moon days (Ezekiel 46:3). These are times of regular worship.
  • ✦
    Permanent direct communication
    The glorified also communicate constantly with Jesus and have seen Him before in the throne room in heaven. The visit to the temple is not the only point of contact.
XVI

The Priesthood in the Kingdom — Levites, Sons of Zadok, and Gentiles

Historical Evolution of the Priesthood
Era Priests High Priest Place of sacrifice
Old Testament Levites (mediators between God and Israel) Pre-incarnate Christ (the true intercessor in heaven) Tabernacle / Temple (building)
Church (current age) All believers — “royal priesthood” (1 Peter 2:9) Glorified Christ interceding before the Father The believer’s body (temple of the Holy Spirit)
Millennial Kingdom Sons of Zadok + some believing Gentiles Christ in the Temple (eternal High Priest) Ezekiel’s Temple (physical building)
The Unfaithful Levites — Reduced Role
Ezekiel 44:10,13–14 “And the Levites who went far from Me, when Israel went astray, who strayed away from Me after their idols, they shall bear their iniquity... They shall not come near Me to minister to Me as priest... but they shall bear their shame and their abominations which they have committed. Yet I will make them doorkeepers of the house...”
The Levite priests facilitated idolatry before the Babylonian destruction. In the Kingdom, their role is limited to custodial work: they will do the “dirty” work of killing and cutting up the animals in the burning pits — a role closer to the worshiper than to the official priest.
The Sons of Zadok — Rewarded Faithfulness
Ezekiel 44:15–16 “But the priests, the Levites, the sons of Zadok, who kept charge of My sanctuary when the children of Israel went astray from Me, they shall come near Me to minister to Me; and they shall stand before Me to offer to Me the fat and the blood... They shall enter My sanctuary, and they shall come near My table to minister to Me, and they shall keep My charge.”
  • ✦
    Who was Zadok
    Zadok was the high priest who remained loyal to David when the house of Saul fought for the throne. He took the ark of the covenant and followed David while he fled from Absalom, demonstrating that David was the Lord’s anointed.
  • ✦
    Lesson of faithfulness
    God takes into account what we sow in this world. Just as with the martyrs of the Tribulation, the Lord assigns opportunities to serve in the Kingdom according to the faithfulness demonstrated here.
Joint Jewish-Gentile Priesthood
  • ✦
    Isaiah confirms Gentiles as priests
    As we saw in Isaiah 56 and 66, the Lord will take some believing Gentiles and allow them to serve as priests in the temple as well. It will be a joint Jewish-Gentile priesthood unprecedented.
  • ✦
    Distribution of roles in the court
    The “unfaithful” Levites perform the slaughter of the animal. The sons of Zadok and authorized Gentiles officiate the rituals before the altar. The burning pits in the corners of the outer court are used by the Levites to burn what does not fit on the altar.
XVII

Why Do Sacrifices Return? — Theological Foundation

The Central Question and the Apparent Tension with Hebrews
Hebrews 10:10–14 “By that will we have been sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once for all... But this Man, after He had offered one sacrifice for sins forever, sat down at the right hand of God... For by one offering He has perfected forever those who are being sanctified.”
If Christ was always our once-for-all sacrifice, why are physical animal sacrifices required? Throughout the centuries, many theologians have been troubled by this prospect. The answer requires understanding why there have been temples and sacrifices from the beginning.
Sin and Its Two Effects — The Story of Adam and Eve
Effect 1: Physical — Separation between people
When Adam and Eve sinned, they instinctively covered themselves from each other. Sin corrupted their nature and turned them into adversaries: they could no longer be fully known, nor fully know the other person. Secret thoughts, sinful desires, and shame were born. Sin separates human beings from one another physically.
Effect 2: Spiritual — Separation from God
Then they hid from God. They instinctively felt that sin demanded the wrath of God. They no longer felt comfortable in His presence. The spirit within them sensed its vulnerability. Sin separates man spiritually from God, who also could not dwell among men without protecting them from His glory.
God’s Two Provisions from Eden
  • ❶
    Spiritual provision — By faith
    The Lord preached the first gospel (protoevangelium) in Genesis 3:15: “And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel.” Adam and Eve were saved by faith in this future promise. All the saints of all times — O.T., N.T., and Kingdom — obtain the same spiritual covering in the same way: by faith in the promised Messiah.
  • ❷
    Physical provision — By sacrifice
    God Himself killed an animal (probably a lamb) and clothed Adam and Eve with its skin (Genesis 3:21). This physical sacrifice: (a) covered their shame, restoring fellowship between them, and (b) taught about the greater sacrifice of Christ to come, for seeing an animal lose its life makes the price of sin tangible.
The Order and Purpose of Sacrifice
  • ❶
    First: spiritual covering by faith
    Reconciliation with God always comes first, only by faith in the Messiah.
  • ❷
    Then: physical sacrifice to restore fellowship
    Physical sacrifice restores fellowship between people and before God. It is an act of worship, not of salvation. It cleanses the guilty conscience.
  • ✦
    Marriage analogy
    When a married couple enters days of disagreement, the marriage covenant is not dissolved (like salvation), but a reconciliation act is required to restore fellowship. Our spirit does not sin, but our flesh does.
  • ✦
    Constant pattern throughout the Bible
    Noah believed (spiritual covering) and practiced animal sacrifices (physical covering). Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob were righteous by faith and also sacrificed. The Law of Moses codified this pattern. The Church is not the exception.
XVIII

The Sacrifices of the Church Today — The Body as Temple

The Key Principle: Sacrifice Occurs Where God Dwells
Era Where God dwells Where physical sacrifice occurs
Genesis (Garden) God attends directly in the garden In the garden where the Lord officiated the first sacrifice
Patriarchs The Angel of the Lord (pre-incarnate Christ) visits His people On altars placed where the Angel of the Lord appeared
Moses — O.T. In the Tabernacle / Temple (physical building) In the Tabernacle/Temple, before the presence of God
Church (today) In the body of every believer (the Holy Spirit dwells in us) In our body — sacrificing the flesh, serving the interests of Christ
Millennial Kingdom In Ezekiel’s Temple (physical building, Most Holy Place) In the Temple, before the Shekinah Glory
The Physical Sacrifice of the Church according to Paul
Romans 12:1–2 “I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service. And do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind...”
1 Corinthians 6:19 “Or do you not know that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have from God, and you are not your own?”
What Do Our Sacrifices Consist of Today?
  • ✦
    Sacrificing pride and self-interest
    By sacrificing our pride and self-interest to show love for others, we restore fellowship within the body. This is what the New Testament priesthood refers to.
  • ✦
    Killing the flesh so that Christ lives in us
    Presenting our body as a living sacrifice to the Lord is not done for salvation — salvation came through Christ’s sacrifice. It is a physical covering: it restores relationships, teaches about Christ, and is an act of worship.
  • ✦
    Modeling the ministry of Christ (Philippians 2)
    Christ, being God, emptied Himself and became a servant. When we do the same, our sacrifices also represent Christ before the world.
Why God Chose to Dwell in the Church During This Age
Philippians 2:5–8 “Let this mind be in you which was also in Christ Jesus, who, being in the form of God, did not consider it robbery to be equal with God, but made Himself of no reputation, taking the form of a bondservant, and coming in the likeness of men. And being found in appearance as a man, He humbled Himself and became obedient to the point of death, even the death of the cross.”
When the time came for God to set Israel aside temporarily to make Himself known to the Gentiles, He could not simply move His temple to a Gentile country — that would have sent the wrong message that He was abandoning Israel permanently, favoring one specific Gentile nation. Instead, God chose a completely new and beautiful solution: to establish His temple in every individual believer, in every nation equally. In that way, every Gentile nation would have access to that temple in a perfectly equitable manner.
XIX

The Sacrifices in the Kingdom — Ezekiel 43

Double Nature of Sacrifice in the Kingdom
  • ①
    Spiritually — always by faith in Jesus
    Spiritual reconciliation with God continues to be only by faith in Jesus, with no change from all previous ages.
  • ②
    Physically — animal in the temple
    Since God once again dwells in a physical building, all physical sacrifices must occur in that place, before His presence. People will come regularly to sacrifice animals according to the Lord’s instructions.
Who Are the Sacrifices For?
  • ✦
    Only for those who have sin
    Sacrifices cover sin, so this system is used only by those who have sin — believers and unbelievers with natural bodies.
  • ✦
    Glorified: do not participate in sacrifices
    Glorified saints have no sin and will not make sacrifices. Their role will be mainly to rule, although some may be supervising priests.
  • ✦
    Believers with natural bodies
    Both believers and unbelievers with natural bodies must make physical sacrifices. Believers will also have the spiritual covering of Christ. Without faith it is impossible to please God.
The Ritual of Consecration of the Altar (Ezekiel 43:18–27)
Ezekiel 43:18–27 — Ordinances of the Altar Day 1: a young bull for a sin offering — its blood on the four horns and corners of the altar, then burned outside the sanctuary. Day 2: a goat for a sin offering, purifying the altar as with the bull. From Day 2 onward: bull + ram without blemish. Salt thrown on all. Seven days of continual atonement. From the eighth day onward: the burnt offerings and peace offerings of the people will be accepted.
  • ✦
    Purpose of the sacrifices in the Kingdom
    They accomplish exactly the same as the previous physical sacrifices covered: they restore fellowship among the people of God, point to and teach about the meaning of Christ’s sacrifice.
  • ✦
    Consequence of not participating
    If people do not participate in the sacrifices, they will be cut off from fellowship with the people of God — just like the drought brought upon the nations that do not worship at the feasts of Jerusalem.
✦

The Millennial Temple is not a contradiction with the work of Christ but its fullest expression: Christ dwells in the Most Holy Place forever, the closed east gate proclaims that His sacrifice was sufficient and final, and the system of physical sacrifices continues God’s eternal pattern — first faith for salvation, then physical sacrifice to restore fellowship between people and before God. What changes is not the principle but the place where God dwells; and there, always, is where the sacrifice occurs. The Church was not the exception: our body was the temple, and our crucified pride was the sacrifice.
