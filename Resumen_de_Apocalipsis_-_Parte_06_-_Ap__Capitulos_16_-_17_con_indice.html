XIV

Babylon and the Redemption of Christ — The Two Great Themes of the Bible

Revelation 16:19 Now the great city was divided into three parts… and great Babylon was remembered before God, to give her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of His wrath.
The Two Themes That Run Through All of Scripture
  • ✦
    Redemption vs. BabylonThe destruction of Babylon is one of the two main themes of the Bible. The other is redemption through Christ. Both themes play against each other throughout all of Scripture.
  • ✦
    Babylon as the antagonistBabylon serves as the opposite of the protagonist, who is Christ. Just as the identity of Jesus was slowly revealed throughout Scripture, Babylon is also unveiled in stages.
  • ✦
    The bowls as vessels of wrathBowls are used as symbols in Scripture that store God's wrath for a later time, when it will be poured out upon those objects deserving of that wrath.
The Five Dimensions of Babylon
  • ❶
    House of sinThe territory where sin began on earth and where Satan's dominion was established.
  • ❷
    Origin of all idolatryThe primary source of false worship and religious rebellion.
  • ❸
    First Gentile nation to conquer Israel and JerusalemGod's first instrument for disciplining His people.
  • ❹
    Central headquarters of the Antichrist's powerThe physical and spiritual capital of the last days.
  • ❺
    Symbol of all false religionThe mother of all harlots and abominations of the earth.
XV

Babylon Dimension 1 — House of Sin: The Home of Satan

The Garden of Eden and the Fall
  • ✦
    Satan loses his heavenly placeAfter Satan's fall from heaven and his removal from the heavenly Eden (Ezek 28:13), he sought his place in the earthly Eden, which is an image of the heavenly one (Genesis 2:8). The earthly Eden was located in what is today Iraq, in the region of Babylon.
  • ✦
    Satan's jealousy of humanitySatan's jealousy over the creation of man and woman — who rivaled his beauty and wisdom — led him to attack them and bring humanity into sin.
  • ✦
    Satan gains dominion over the earthWhen Adam disobeyed God and obeyed Satan, he handed over the dominion God had given him. Satan thus gained dominion over the earth.
Babylon: Ground Zero of Spiritual Warfare
  • ✦
    The first area surrendered to SatanBabylon is ground zero — the first area of land surrendered to Satan, the beginning of sin, and the start of his battle with God for control of the earth.
  • ✦
    Satan's eternal strongholdForever, Babylon has been Satan's homeland, his stronghold. Today this region continues to be heavily guarded by his dark forces, with an especially intense demonic activity.
  • ✦
    Rule of associationWhenever we hear the word Babylon, we must associate it with the home of Satan and sin.
XVI

Babylon Dimension 2 — Source of All Idolatry: The Tower of Babel

Nimrod and the Tower of Babel
  • ✦
    Nimrod: the antichrist of his ageGenesis 11 records how men gathered in Babel under the leadership of Nimrod, an all-powerful world leader for all the tribes of those days. His story serves as a foreshadowing of the Antichrist.
  • ✦
    The first false religionNimrod demanded a new kind of worship and a construction project of a tower that would reach to heaven. The tower was the first false religion: it attempted to replace obedience to God with a man-made way to reach heaven.
  • ✦
    The idea Satan plantedBehind the tower were hearts that believed for the first time that it was possible to work their way to God through their own efforts. Satan used Nimrod to establish this idea: men could define their own way to reach heaven. God placed eternity in man's heart to seek Him, but Satan corrupted it.
God Responds: The Confusion of Languages
  • ✦
    The Lord scatters mankindThe Lord responded by confusing language to ensure that such worldwide rebellion would be more difficult in the future. No government functions if people do not share a common language. Since then, languages have kept men separated rather than organizing against God.
  • ✦
    The global language of the internetWestern culture and the internet have increasingly brought the world together under a common language: English. Automatic translators integrate a large population into global unity. This has caused the Tower of Babel to grow in population. Every person integrated can be easily influenced and directed toward specific objectives.
  • ✦
    A new rebellion against GodAs the world rapidly overcomes the language barrier, a new worldwide rebellion against God becomes possible. Our minds are manipulated by algorithms designed to make us buy distorted ideas about the world and especially against a genuine relationship with God.
XVII

Babylon Dimension 3 — First Gentile Kingdom to Conquer Israel and Jerusalem

Nebuchadnezzar and the Beginning of the Times of the Gentiles
  • ✦
    The first Gentile conquestBefore Nebuchadnezzar, no Gentile nation had succeeded in dominating God's people. God allowed Babylon to conquer Israel as part of His plan to discipline His people for their sins.
  • ✦
    Nebuchadnezzar as a foreshadowing of the AntichristAs studied in Daniel, the man at the head of Babylon is also a foreshadowing of the Antichrist. Babylon becomes the first of the nations to control Israel during the Times of the Gentiles.
  • ✦
    Judgment promised to BabylonGod allowed the conquest but also promised, in Jeremiah, to eventually punish Babylon for its cruelty against Israel: "And I will repay Babylon and all the inhabitants of Chaldea for all the evil they have done in Zion" (Jer 51:24).
The Pattern Repeats at the End
  • ✦
    A final Gentile rulerThe Times of the Gentiles will end in a similar way to how they began: a single Gentile ruler controlling the entire world and persecuting Israel, controlling both Jerusalem and Babylon. This will be the kingdom of the Antichrist.
  • ✦
    Discipline, not destructionLike the first Babylon, this last one will be used by God to discipline His people. However, as God promised, the discipline will not be for the destruction of Israel.
  • ✦
    Rule of associationWhenever we hear Babylon, we must think of "God's instrument for disciplining Israel."
XVIII

Babylon Dimension 4 — Central Headquarters of the Antichrist in the Last Days

Babylon: Physical and Spiritual at the Same Time
  • ✦
    Literal and symbolic simultaneouslyScripture makes clear that Babylon in Revelation is both the literal place and the biblical symbol. It is like creation: the seven days are literal, yet the number seven also carries symbolic meaning.
  • ✦
    The Antichrist resides in geographic BabylonAlready in Revelation 16, the Antichrist resides in geographic Babylon, near the Euphrates River. When the Euphrates dries up, it provides the opportunity to march his armies toward Israel (from east to west).
  • ✦
    Satan's home explains the choiceThe Antichrist moves the seat of world power to Babylon at the moment Satan takes up residence in him. It makes sense: Satan's home has always been in Mesopotamia; he will naturally locate the seat of world government there.
A City That Rises Again Only to Be Destroyed
  • ✦
    Gradual destruction befitting its purposeThe destruction of the city of Babylon stands out precisely because the city has once again become a center of power. It will be destroyed just before the Antichrist himself is destroyed.
  • ✦
    Center of political, military, and financial powerIn the last days of the Tribulation, the Antichrist makes Babylon the seat of his military and financial power. Chapter 18 will describe the judgment of this physical city, ruining its wealth, power, and prestige.
  • ✦
    Rule of associationWhenever we hear Babylon, we must think of "the seat of power in the last days."
XIX

Babylon Dimension 5 — Symbol of All False Religions

Only Two Religious Systems on Earth
  • ✦
    God's wayGod's way, made available through His promises in His Word and through our faith in the work of Christ.
  • ✦
    Everything else: BabylonThe enemy's false religion that began in the Garden, took shape in Babel, and gave birth to a countless number of derivative religions. The heterogeneous mixture of religious options today is testimony to the proliferation of the enemy's lies.
  • ✦
    Does not represent a single false religionBabylon does not represent one specific false system, but rather all the false religious systems that Satan has invented.
How the Multiplicity of False Religions Benefits Satan
  • ①
    Camouflages the truthMany religions generate confusion and make it difficult for anyone to find the truth amid so much noise.
  • ②
    Perpetuates the myth of many pathsIt makes it seem as though there are many paths to heaven, so all religions appear equally valid. This dilutes the exclusivity of Christ.
  • ✦
    Rule of associationWhenever we hear Babylon, we must think of "all false religion."
Summary: Babylon Is and Represents — Two Antagonistic Cities
🔴 Babylon — Dwelling Place of Satan

It is a physical place where sin began, the spiritual seat of Satan and rebellion. Starting point of idolatry. Source of all hatred and oppression against the people of God. Symbol of everything Satan is and does to undermine truth and oppose God. To the east of Jerusalem: the direction of evil.

✨ Jerusalem — Dwelling Place of God

It is the city of God and a place of redemption. Capital of His people and the place of Christ's sacrifice. It will be the seat of Christ's government and the center of the world in the Kingdom. Just as Babylon is for Satan, Jerusalem is for Jesus. To the west: the direction of good.

XX

The Cardinal Directions as Spiritual Symbol — East vs. West

East = Evil · West = Good
  • ✦
    A consistent biblical patternBabylon is to the east of Jerusalem and therefore the eastern direction represents evil. Jerusalem is to the west, so moving westward is a picture of turning away from evil and drawing near to the Lord.
  • ✦
    After creationAfter the creation of Adam, the Lord placed him in a garden to the east, representing Adam moving toward sin.
  • ✦
    Cain sent to the eastCain is sent to the east after murdering Abel — a movement toward sin and separation.
  • ✦
    Abraham from east to westAbraham was sent from Ur in the east to the Promised Land in the west, representing his passage from paganism to faith.
  • ✦
    Jesus and the temptationsEven Jesus moved from the west side of the Jordan to the east to confront Satan in His temptations in the wilderness.
The Figurative Becomes Literal at the End
  • ✦
    The final battlefieldWhere before Babylon and Jerusalem figuratively represented evil against good, they have now become the literal battlefield. The only two cities still standing at the end of the age are Babylon and Jerusalem.
  • ✦
    The Lord narrows the focusThe Lord has narrowed the world's focus to these two places. Through the Bowl judgments He drives Satan from the east (Babylon) toward the west (Jerusalem) for a final battle.
  • ✦
    The global plan of destructionPhysically: the Antichrist and his power base, the city of Babylon, must be destroyed. Spiritually: all false religion must come to an end, as must all rebellion and idolatry. Babylon seeking to destroy Jerusalem, but in the end Jerusalem destroys Babylon.
XXI

Chapter 17: The Judgment of Spiritual Babylon — The Great Harlot

Revelation 17:1–6 Then one of the seven angels who had the seven bowls came and talked with me, saying to me, "Come, I will show you the judgment of the great harlot who sits on many waters, with whom the kings of the earth committed fornication… So he carried me away in the Spirit into the wilderness. And I saw a woman sitting on a scarlet beast which was full of names of blasphemy… BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND OF THE ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH. I saw the woman, drunk with the blood of the saints…
The Woman as a Symbol of False Religion
  • ✦
    Women symbolize religious systemsIn Scripture, women are commonly used to symbolize religious systems. Israel is called the wife of Jehovah, and the Church is called the Bride and Wife of Christ.
  • ✦
    The harlot: an illegitimate wifeA harlot is an illegitimate and false wife, who gives the illusion of marriage without substance or reality. In that sense she is an apt image of idolatry: a false religious relationship that pretends to be true, substituting the place of a covenant with the true God.
  • ✦
    The Mother of all harlotsThis woman is not simply one of the many false religions; she is the Mother of all false religion. Babylon is where it all began. The source of false religion is Satan, father of all lies, personified in his hometown.
Sitting on Many Waters — Global Dominion
  • ✦
    The waters = all nationsThe waters represent the multitude of nations and peoples of the world (v.15). The harlot sitting on them suggests that she dominates and deceives all of humanity under the false religion that finds its origin in Babylon.
  • ✦
    World leaders commit fornication with herThroughout history, world leaders have used religion to build a power base, to enrich themselves, and to control their subjects. They commit fornication with the harlot in the sense that they use her for their own pleasure and become her partners.
  • ✦
    False religion: sedative and stimulantStimulant: it awakens the lust for greater power and other desires. It becomes worship of oneself — a god I design to satisfy my addiction. Sedative: it numbs the person into a spiritual stupor, inoculating them against the truth (2 Corinthians 4:3–4).
XXII

The Harlot Rides the Beast — Babylon and the Antichrist United

Revelation 17:3, 8 So he carried me away in the Spirit into the wilderness. And I saw a woman sitting on a scarlet beast which was full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns… The beast that you saw was, and is not, and will ascend out of the bottomless pit and go to perdition.
The Beast is the Antichrist
  • ✦
    The same beast from Chapter 13This beast is the same one John saw in Revelation 13: seven heads and ten horns. It is the Antichrist, confirmed in v.17:8: the one who was, is not, and is about to come out of the abyss — a reference to his death and resurrection.
  • ✦
    The harlot existed before the AntichristSpiritual Babylon has existed long before the Antichrist. We just saw her sitting on many waters, which represent the kings of the earth throughout all of history. Satan has been using false religion to control world leaders across the centuries.
A Crucial Shift: The Harlot Now Rides the Beast
  • ✦
    The union at the end of the TribulationIn Chapter 17, Babylon the harlot rides upon the beast (the Antichrist) who rules the world. While Satan dwells in this man, spiritual Babylon and the beast have merged into one.
  • ✦
    One religion: the worstAt the end of the Tribulation, all the world's religion is invested in a single man, the Antichrist. There will no longer be many false religions. The harlot now rides the beast because only one remains: not Buddha, not the virgin, not Muhammad, etc. Only the Antichrist.
  • ✦
    Adorned with riches to honor SatanThe woman dressed as royalty represents that Satan demands worship with great sacrifices. Dan 11:36–38: the Antichrist will exalt himself above every god and honor the god of fortresses with gold, silver, and precious stones.
Drunk with the Blood of the Saints — Judgment Assured
  • ✦
    Cup of abominationsThe harlot holds a cup of abominations and immorality (v.4), promoting these two things that go hand in hand. She revels in them as though addicted.
  • ✦
    Drunk with the blood of martyrsVerse 6 reveals that she is drunk with the blood of the saints, representing both the martyrdom of the Tribulation and that of earlier times. This scene tells the story of religion in all ages, used to persecute the saints.
  • ✦
    The worst enemy: false Christian denominationsThe worst examples are the so-called false Christian religions that Satan uses to discredit Jesus and the true Church. In the last days of the Times of the Gentiles, all his plans are invested in one man: the Antichrist.
XXIII

The Seven Heads — Seven Mountains, Seven Kings: The Times of the Gentiles

Revelation 17:9–11 "Here is the mind which has wisdom: The seven heads are seven mountains on which the woman sits. There are also seven kings. Five have fallen, one is, and the other has not yet come. And when he comes, he must continue a short time. The beast that was, and is not, is himself also the eighth, and is of the seven, and is going to perdition."
The Symbolic Chain: Heads → Mountains → Kings
  • ✦
    Having wisdom = knowing the O.T."Here is the mind which has wisdom" means we must know history and the Old Testament, specifically the context of Daniel.
  • ✦
    Mountains represent supreme kingsWhen the Bible uses mountains to represent kings, it means kings of the highest authority. No one is higher than they are — they have no other human authority above them. They do not have the Antichrist over them.
  • ✦
    Key distinction: not the 7 of the end timesThese seven kings must be distinguished from the seven leaders who will rule under the Antichrist during the Tribulation (who will not be the supreme authority). The seven historical kings rule in sequence throughout history, not simultaneously.
Horns of the Beast vs. Heads — Dragon vs. Antichrist
  • ✦
    The dragon (Satan) in Rev. 12:3He had ten horns and seven heads with crowns. This indicates that Satan holds control over the entire world: all ten of its kingdoms and all seven of its rulers including the Antichrist.
  • ✦
    The beast (Antichrist) in Rev. 13:1He had ten horns with ten crowns, but only seven heads with blasphemous names. And one of those seven heads was slain and then rose again. The seven heads are supreme world leaders who ruled throughout the Times of the Gentiles.
  • ✦
    The Beast incorporates the four beasts of DanielThe beast of Rev. 13:2 (leopard, bear, mouth of a lion, ten horns) represents the entire Gentile age, culminating in the Antichrist's rule over the world. It had parts of the lion, bear, leopard, and the fourth beast of Daniel 7.
XXIV

The Seven Historical Kings Who Controlled Babylon and Jerusalem

Rule for Identifying the Seven Kings

To be counted among the seven kings, rulers had to have controlled both Babylon and Jerusalem at the same time. Israel has remained under Gentile authority from Babylon until the return of Christ. The theme has always been Babylon against Jerusalem.

The Seven Kings Throughout the Times of the Gentiles
King I — Nebuchadnezzar (Babylon)
First Gentile dynasty to control both Babylon and Jerusalem. Foreshadowing of the Antichrist. Beginning of the Times of the Gentiles.
King II — Cyrus (Persia)
The Persian Empire succeeds Babylon and controls both cities under the dynasty of Cyrus.
King III — Alexander the Great (Greece)
Alexander's brief rule over the entire known world. He died without heirs; his Empire was divided into four (like the four wings of Daniel's leopard).
King IV — Ptolemy III (246 B.C.)
From the Ptolemaic Empire, he attacked the Seleucid Empire and temporarily gained control of both Babylon and Jerusalem.
King V — Antiochus IV Epiphanes (170 B.C.)
From the Seleucid Greek Empire, he attacked the Ptolemaic Kingdom and gained control of both cities. By John's day, these five kings had already fallen.
King VI — Titus / Roman Empire (John's era)
General Titus conquered Jerusalem in A.D. 70 and ruled Babylon when he ascended to Emperor of Rome. The Roman Empire and its successors continued control over both cities until 1923. This is the king who "is" in John's era.
King VII — The Antichrist (Tribulation)
The last world leader to seize control of both places. He reigns for a short time (3½ years, the second half of the Tribulation). He had not yet come in John's day. The harlot finally establishes herself upon him.
The Seventh King Who Rises Again as the Eighth
  • ✦
    Death and resurrection of the AntichristIn verse 17:11 the beast is said to be the seventh and also the eighth. John said he was and is not, and then returns. This refers to the death and resurrection of the Antichrist. He was the seventh until he dies; upon rising again he is back in charge, as if he were an "eighth" king.
  • ✦
    The harlot rides the kings throughout historyJust as the harlot sat upon each of the five previous kings in their time, she sits upon the current one (the VI) during John's era, and will finally sit upon the seventh/Antichrist during the Tribulation. She is the facilitator; the kings are her benefactor.
The Ten Horns — The Ten Kings of the Tribulation
Revelation 17:12–13 "The ten horns which you saw are ten kings who have received no kingdom as yet, but they receive authority for one hour as kings with the beast. These are of one mind, and they will give their power and authority to the beast."
  • ✦
    Sole purpose: support the AntichristThe only purpose of these ten kings is to support God's program for bringing the Antichrist to power, consolidating world government under 10 rulers. It is easier for one man to rule over 10 nations than over 190+.
  • ✦
    They had not yet come in John's dayIt would be 2,000 or more years before they come to power. They will rule for a short time (one "hour"). Ultimately, they and their kingdoms end with the coming of Christ.
XXV

Destruction of the Harlot — The End of Spiritual Babylon

Revelation 17:15–18 "The waters which you saw, where the harlot sits, are peoples, multitudes, nations, and tongues. And the ten horns which you saw on the beast, these will hate the harlot, make her desolate and naked, eat her flesh and burn her with fire. For God has put it into their hearts to fulfill His purpose… And the woman whom you saw is that great city which reigns over the kings of the earth."
The Antichrist Destroys False Religion
  • ✦
    At the midpoint of the TribulationWhen Satan indwells the Antichrist, he commands that all worship be directed to himself. He therefore puts an end to every form of worship and religious practice. The ten horns come to hate the harlot.
  • ✦
    Destruction of all religious institutionsAll religious institutions, temples, churches, mosques, and other places of worship are destroyed. These physical structures and the relics within them are burned with fire (v.16). These things constitute the "flesh" of the harlot.
  • ✦
    Desolate and nakedAll religious institutions are stripped of their wealth, so the harlot is left desolate and naked. The angel says in verse 17 that these kings are working to fulfill God's purposes, even though they believe it is their own idea.
God's Sovereign Plan — Only One Man Remains
  • ✦
    The world goes from many gods to oneAfter the seventh Bowl is poured out, the only false religious worship that will remain is the worship of Satan. One god. Which has always been the goal of spiritual Babylon.
  • ✦
    God used Satan to destroy his own systemDuring the Times of the Gentiles, God put Satan's false religious system to work for His own purposes in disciplining Israel. Now the seventh judgment brings this corrupt system to an end by using Satan himself to complete that destruction.
  • ✦
    In the end: only one manThe Lord used the seventh judgment to vastly simplify the problem of cleansing the world of false religion. At His Second Coming He needs only to destroy one man to put an end to all idolatry. The battle comes down to God against a single man: the 666 against the one true God.
The Lamb Will Overcome
Revelation 17:14 "These will make war with the Lamb, and the Lamb will overcome them, for He is Lord of lords and King of kings; and those who are with Him are called, chosen, and faithful."

In the end, the entire structure that has been governing the world throughout all of history — multiple governments and religions — will be destroyed. Only one man will remain ruling the world as political and religious leader. And that man will face the Lamb. Next comes the physical destruction of the city of Babylon (Chapter 18).

✦
Chapters 16 and 17 reveal that the history of the world is not the chaos it appears to be, but rather the unfolding of two great themes that have stood opposed since Eden: the redemption of Christ and the rebellion of Babylon. Satan, from his stronghold in Mesopotamia, has woven across millennia a web of false religions, corrupt governments, and deceptions to counter God's plan — but at the culminating moment, the Lord Himself turns every piece on the board to His advantage: He uses the seventh Bowl to cause the Antichrist to destroy all false religions except his own, reducing the problem of evil to a single man, and He positions the armies of the world at the precise location where the Lamb will meet them. The outcome is inevitable: the Lord of lords and King of kings prevails, and Babylon, in all its forms, receives the cup of burning wine that has borne its name since eternity.
