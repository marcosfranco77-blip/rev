I

The Revelation of Jesus Christ — Introduction and Chain of Custody

What Is the Book of Revelation?
  • ✦The only prophetic book with a direct divine authorNo other book of the Bible declares itself to be the direct revelation of Jesus Christ himself. In Greek, "Apocalypsis" means revelation.
  • ✦Verified chain of custodyThe revelation comes from the Father → Jesus Christ → Angel → Apostle John → the Church. It certifies the authenticity of the content, guaranteeing it is the unadulterated original — critically important given the proliferation of false teachers of the era.
  • ✦Addressed to the servants, not to the worldThe letter is not addressed to the world at large as a witness, but specifically to the church of Christ.
  • ✦John saw, he did not narrateJohn did not write an interpretation of what he heard; he faithfully recorded what was shown to him visually, so that we might interpret it.
The Unique Blessing of Revelation
Revelation 1:3 Blessed is he who reads and those who hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written in it; for the time is near.
  • ✦Promised rewardIt is the only book of the Bible that expressly promises a reward to those who read and hear it — deeper understanding of God's plans and character, peace, discernment, and a desire to study further.
  • ✦Total understanding not requiredThe blessing is for those who read and hear with a heart open to the Holy Spirit, regardless of their level of theological knowledge. It does not say "for those who understand."

  • ✦Sharp two-edged sword from his mouthHis Word destroys the wicked (Isa 11:4: he will strike the earth with the rod of his mouth).
Theological Significance of the Vision
  • ✦Temporary appearance vs. eternal appearanceDuring his first coming Jesus had a temporary appearance (Phil 2:6–8: he emptied himself, taking the form of a servant). Now he appears as who he truly is.
  • ✦King of kings in all his majestyRighteousness (loins), Faithfulness (belt), Purity (white robes), Priesthood and Kingship (robes), Light of truth (face shining).
  • ✦Consistency with Daniel and IsaiahThe vision is identical to Dan 10:4–6 and Isa 11:1–5 — confirming the coherence of Scripture.
  • ✦John's reactionI fell at his feet as though dead. Christ revives him with his right hand: Fear not; I am the first and the last. (Rev. 1:17)
Revelation 19:11–16 He who sat on him was called Faithful and True... Out of His mouth goes a sharp sword... And He has on His robe and on His thigh a name written: KING OF KINGS AND LORD OF LORDS.
The Mystery of the Seven Stars and the Seven Lampstands (Rev. 1:19–20)
  • ✦Seven stars = angels of the seven churchesMessengers under the authority of Christ (he holds them in his hand), dedicated to the service of those who are heirs of salvation. (Heb 1:14)
  • ✦Seven lampstands = the seven churchesThe churches illuminate the truth by reaching into the darkness — the Great Commission. (Phil 2:15: you shine as lights in the world)
  • ✦The number seven = totalityThe 7 letters were written to 7 churches, even though there were more at the time (Rome, Corinth, Galatia, Colossae, Thessalonica, Jerusalem, etc.). The biblical 7 represents completeness, everything — the finished work of God.
  • ✦Structure of the book revealedRev. 1:19 establishes the threefold outline: (1) things that John saw, (2) things that are, (3) things that are to take place after this.

The Seven Letters — To Whom Were They Addressed and When?

Two Key Conclusions
  • ❶Conclusion #1: Totality of the churchesThe letters describe 7 universal characteristics manifested in 7 main churches. The biblical 7 = totality. Therefore, they are addressed to the entire Church of Christ of that era.
  • ❷Conclusion #2: All historical periodsThe letters are written in clockwise order — suggesting time. From the beginning of the church (Pentecost) to the end of the church age, including the present church. (Identified ~150 years ago from a historical perspective)
Rules of Biblical Interpretation
  • ❶LiteralSpecific and historical. If the simplest and most straightforward interpretation makes common sense, do not seek another.
  • ❷UniversalGeneral and timeless. Applicable to any era, including the present.
  • ❸PropheticSymbolic and speaks of future events. **All three must be used and in that order.**

Letter to Laodicea — The Church of Our Age

The City of Laodicea — Historical Context
  • ✦Meaning of the name"People who rule or are governed by their own judgment" — an independent democratic mindset.
  • ✦Administrative, banking, and commercial centerThe wealthiest, most prosperous, and highly independent city. After the earthquake of 60 AD it refused imperial aid and rebuilt itself by its own means. It held the title of "free city."
  • ✦Lukewarm and sulfurous waterIt received water from two cities: one cold (mountain/snow) and one hot (thermal spring). Combined they produce lukewarm, sulfurous water that causes nausea — a perfect metaphor for spiritual lukewarmness.
  • ✦Known for linen, black wool, eye salve, and bankingJesus uses these three elements as direct metaphors in his letter.
Christ's Diagnosis of Laodicea (Rev. 3:14–22)
Revelation 3:17 Because you say, "I am rich, have become wealthy, and have need of nothing" — and do not know that you are wretched, miserable, poor, blind, and naked —
  • ✦Refined goldTrue spiritual riches vs. material impurity. It lacks the riches of Christ.
  • ✦White garmentsTo cover the shame of spiritual nakedness — they are not clothed with the garments of Christ.
  • ✦Eye salveTo see spiritually. The church suffers from total spiritual blindness.
Visible Church vs. Invisible Church — Hot, Cold, and Lukewarm
Visible vs. Invisible Church
  • ✦VisibleThe entire congregation, including true believers and unbelievers.
  • ✦InvisibleThose who truly know the Lord — born again by the Holy Spirit. This circle shrinks in the last times. No place in Scripture sends us to inspect others; only to examine our own hearts.
The Danger of the Lukewarm State
  • ✦Hot (believer)Recognizes his condition and can be reached.
  • ✦Cold (unbeliever)At least recognizes his unbelief — can repent.
  • ✦Lukewarm (the danger)"Would that you were cold or hot." The lukewarm claims to be on one side but is really on the other. He does not recognize what he truly is — that is the danger. (Matt 7:21)

VIII

The Present Age, the Age to Come, and the Last Days

Definitions of Time
  • ✦Age / EraA prolonged but finite time within God's great plan. It is not eternal — it has a definite beginning and end.
  • ✦Present ageThe current period that will come to an end to give way to another. By faith, we have already received the promise of eternal life. Once in our eternal body, we will take possession of what was promised. (Mark 10:30)
  • ✦Age to comeThe next age — it will begin when the present age ends. It includes the Millennium and the new creation.
  • ✦Last daysThe final period of the present age. Paul already declared himself to be in the last days in the 1st century (Heb 1:2). That is, the "last days" have lasted 2,000 years — the entire church period is within them.
The Present Age Is Larger Than the Church Age
Conceptual Key

The Present Age began long before the church period. When the church began, they were already in the last days of the present age. This implies that God's plan is not primarily about the church — the plan began earlier with Israel and will conclude with Israel. For us as the church, it is everything; but in the grand scheme, the plan revolves around the redemption of Israel and the establishment of the promised kingdom.

The Old Covenant, Its Blessings, Curses, and the Door of Salvation

The National Covenant of Israel (Deuteronomy 29)
  • ✦A group covenant, not individualAll Israel entered into covenant with God: heads of tribes, elders, officers, men, children, women, and foreigners — at every level of society.
  • ✦Includes future generationsNot only with those present but with "those who are not here today" — all generations from Abraham.
  • ✦Lake Superior analogyIt is the same lake with the same body of water, even though each year it is composed of completely different molecules. God made the covenant with the entire group descended from Abraham, not with isolated individuals.
Blessings and Curses of the Covenant (Leviticus 26)
  • ✓If they obeyRain in its season, fruitful land, prosperity, peace — blessings in the kingdom. (Lev 26:3–4)
  • ✦If they disobeyTerror, wasting disease, fever, seed destroyed by enemies, defeat in battle. If a single Israelite breaks a single law, the entire nation receives the consequences — a very binding covenant.
  • ✦The Times of the Gentiles = curses in actionThe dispersion of Israel, the loss of the temple, and Gentile dominion are the literal fulfillment of the Old Covenant curses.
The Door of Salvation for Breaking the Law (Leviticus 26:40–45)
Leviticus 26:40–42 But if they confess their iniquity and the iniquity of their fathers, with their unfaithfulness in which they were unfaithful to Me... then I will remember My covenant with Jacob, and My covenant with Isaac and My covenant with Abraham I will remember.

  • ✦Individual salvation already availableEvery Jew who individually comes to faith in Christ exits the Old Covenant just like any Gentile — saved solely by faith (Rom 10:12–13).

The 70 Weeks of Daniel — The Timeline of Israel's Salvation

Daniel's Error and the Correction of the Angel Gabriel
  • ✦Daniel's errorAt the end of the 70 years of captivity in Babylon, Daniel mistakenly believed that: (a) those 70 years represented the total punishment for sin, and (b) that he could make the confession individually on behalf of the entire nation.
  • ✦The realityThe 70 years corresponded to the disobedience of not letting the land rest every seventh year (Lev 25:2–4; Jer 25:11). An individual confession could not substitute for a national one.
  • ✦70 weeks of yearsThe angel corrects: they are 70 weeks of years. The Jews group in sevens (as we group in dozens). 70 × 7 = 490 years.
Purpose of the 70 Weeks (Daniel 9:24)
  • ✦To finish the transgressionThe rejection of the Messiah must be acknowledged and confessed.
  • ✦To make an end of sinThe era of national disobedience will come to an end.

  • ✦To seal up vision and prophecyEverything prophesied concerning Israel will be completely fulfilled.

The Olivet Discourse — Matthew 24 and Luke 21

The Four Questions and the Additional Topic
  • AWhat are NOT signs of the end?False christs, rumors of wars, wars and uprisings — but the end is not yet. (Matt 24:4–6; Luke 21:8–9)
  • BWhat are the signs of the end of the age?Beginning of birth pains: nations against nations, famines, earthquakes, pestilences, terror, and signs in the heavens. (Matt 24:7–8; Luke 21:10–11)
  • CWhen will the destruction of the Temple take place?Only in Luke 21:12–24. Fulfilled in 70 AD under Titus. A warning to flee when Jerusalem is surrounded by armies.
  • DWhat are the signs of your coming?The Tribulation precedes the Second Coming. False christs with signs, the abomination of desolation, the unprecedented great tribulation, cosmic signs. (Matt 24:9–31; Luke 21:25–28)
  • EWarning and Resurrection of the Church (Rapture)As in the days of Noah. Two in the field, one taken and the other left. Watch, because you do not know the hour. (Matt 24:32–44; Luke 21:28–36)
The Coming of the Lord vs. The Day of the Lord
😊 The Coming of the Lord (Rapture)

A joyous day for the church. It can happen at any moment. "The Lord himself... will descend from heaven; and the dead in Christ will rise first. Then... we will be caught up in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air." (1 Thess 4:16–17). Judgment for reward, not condemnation (2 Cor 5:10).

😟 The Day of the Lord (Tribulation)

A grievous day for unbelievers. Also called: Great Day of the Lord (Mal 4:5), Tribulation (Deut 4:30), Time of Jacob's Trouble (Jer 30:7), The Indignation (Dan 11:36), Day of Darkness (Joel 2:2). These are the 7 years of Daniel's 70th week.

2 Thessalonians 2:1–3 Now, brethren, concerning the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ and our gathering together to Him... that Day will not come unless the falling away comes first, and the man of sin is revealed, the son of perdition.
Order of Events Established by Paul (2 Thessalonians 2)

The church of Laodicea in its lukewarm and apostate state. Already underway.
② The Coming of the Lord and our gathering to Him (possibly simultaneous with ③)
The Holy Spirit, who dwells in the church, is "taken out of the way" along with his bride. This allows the Antichrist to be revealed.
③ Manifestation of the Antichrist
The little horn of Daniel 7 — the "man of lawlessness, son of destruction" who sits in the temple, claiming to be God.
④ The Day of the Lord — The Tribulation (Week #70)
The 7 years determined for the people of Israel (Dan 9:27). The church is not destined for wrath (1 Thess 5:9).
⑤ The Second Coming of Christ
Exactly 7 years after the start of the Tribulation. Christ comes with his heavenly armies (the church clothed in white linen, Rev 19:14). He destroys the fourth kingdom. He establishes the Millennium.
