I

Current Situation: The Midpoint of the Tribulation

State of the World as Revelation 15 Begins
  • ✦
    Satan indwells the AntichristSatan has resurrected the Antichrist and indwells his body. The world celebrates him for having stopped the judgments with the death of the Two Witnesses.
  • ✦
    The Antichrist dominates the worldWith Satan's power he governs the planet, being worshiped as the messiah and savior of the world.
  • ✦
    The False Prophet and the new religionSatan has raised up a false prophet to lead the world in a single religion that worships the Antichrist; a supernatural image is installed in the Jewish temple.
  • ✦
    The mark of the beastEveryone is called to worship the new god of the world. Those who refuse cannot buy or sell and, when found, are beheaded (Rev. 20:4).
Groups that Resist
  • ①
    Believers in Jesus (Jews and Gentiles)They persevere in faith even under threat of beheading.
  • ②
    Devout Jews faithful to YahwehThey remain faithful to the God of Israel, especially under persecution in Jerusalem.
  • ③
    Refugees in Petra / BozrahSome are safe in Petra; others are martyred and enter heaven to rest and await their resurrection in the kingdom.
  • ✦
    The unbelieving worldThey enjoy an uneasy peace for most of the second half of the Tribulation. The Seal and Trumpet judgments were already completed in the first 3½ years; what remains is the seventh trumpet: the bowls.
II

Transition to the End: What Comes Next?

John's Narrative Jump
  • ✦
    The text jumps to the endSince the Bowl judgments are the next step, the text of Revelation jumps from the midpoint to a point near the end of the seven years. The Bible does not describe more of that intermediate period because perhaps there is nothing more to say.
  • ✦
    Chapter 15 as a bridgeChapter 15 leads us along that path beginning with another sign in heaven.
  • ✦
    How much time remains?The bowls and the coming of Christ are separated by weeks or days, not months or years. These are the final events before the Lord's return to end the 7 years of tribulation and the Gentile age.
The Great Tribulation — Timeline
Years 1–3½ (First half)
Seal and Trumpet judgments. The first 3½ years are completed.
Midpoint — Mid-Trib
Antichrist resurrected, false prophet, image in the temple, the mark, massive persecution.
Final weeks of the Tribulation
The seven Bowl judgments. John jumps directly to this point in Ch. 15.
Final days — Second Coming
The Battle of Armageddon (5 stages) concludes with the return of Christ.
III

Chapter 15: The Great and Marvelous Sign — The Seven Angels

Revelation 15:1 Then I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvelous: seven angels having the seven last plagues, for in them the wrath of God is complete.
The Seven Bowls as the End of the Old Covenant
  • ✦
    Last judgments of the TribulationThe seven angels hold the seven bowls: the last judgments of the Tribulation and the last of God's judgments required by the Old Covenant.
  • ✦
    Daniel's 70th WeekThe Tribulation period is a consequence of the terms of the Covenant between the Lord and Israel. As Gabriel told Daniel: "Seventy weeks are determined for your people and for your holy city" (Dan. 9:24). The last seven years are designated for Israel (not for the Church).
A Judgment to Refine Israel
  • ✦
    Protection for believers in BozrahThese judgments are not directed at believers. The Lord provides protection to believing Jews in Bozrah. This distinction affirms that the Rapture occurs before the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    The refining fireFor unbelieving Israel, the outpouring of God's wrath refines and purifies them, as Zechariah 13:7–9 describes: two-thirds will be cut off; the third part will remain and be refined as silver, tested as gold until they confess: "The LORD is my God."
  • ✦
    A deferred judgmentSince Isaiah 49, God declared His purpose: to defer His wrath to purify Israel in the furnace of affliction, for the sake of His name. He is finally issuing the judgment He has long been postponing.
IV

The Sea of Glass and the Singing Martyrs

Revelation 15:2–4 And I saw something like a sea of glass mingled with fire, and those who have the victory over the beast, over his image and over his mark and over the number of his name, standing on the sea of glass, having harps of God. They sing the song of Moses, the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb…
The Martyrs of the Tribulation
  • ✦
    The sea of glass mingled with fireAn otherworldly setting where those who were martyred during the Tribulation stand. Their death is called a "victory," because it transfers them from the influence of the beast to the Lord's domain.
  • ✦
    They overcame the enemy through deathThey died without yielding to the enemy's demands, they overcame the mark of the beast because they never loved their physical life more than the Lord. Jesus said that whoever seeks to save his life will lose it, and whoever loses it will gain it.
The Song of the Lamb — For Believers
  • ✦
    Message of the songIt declares that Jesus is the righteous King of the nations, who will be worshiped at the end of the Tribulation and at His return will reign over them. It emphasizes everything that has been anticipated.
  • ✦
    A hope for the persecuted believerThis sign gives the Tribulation believer something to hold on to. The enemy's forces will tear families apart and execute children, but the believer's confidence is in knowing that he will overcome through death and that Christ will return soon.
V

The Two Songs of Moses — Prophecy and Fulfillment

First Song — Deuteronomy 32
  • ✦
    Prophetic warning as Moses diesAs Moses is about to die, he warns Israel that in the future they will become unfaithful, will worship other gods, and will disregard the Rock who saved them.
  • ✦
    Day of vengeance and afflictionThe Lord will bring calamities upon His people, humbling them to break their rebellious hearts. Deut. 32:35: "Vengeance is Mine, and recompense; their foot shall slip in due time; for the day of their calamity is at hand."
  • ✦
    Final fulfillment in the TribulationThis song is clearly prophetic and its final fulfillment is found in the events of the Tribulation. The Lord judges His people according to their covenants.
Second Song — Exodus 15
  • ✦
    The crossing of the Red SeaMoses leads the people in a song of praise after the Lord closes the Red Sea over Pharaoh's army. Israel escapes from slavery, fleeing through the wilderness from a determined enemy.
  • ✦
    A picture of the final rescueThe Exodus is a picture of what will happen in the Tribulation: through miraculous judgments, the Lord brings Israel to Himself so they may dwell safely in the promised land. The Lord rescues them when things seem darkest.
Purpose of Both Songs — Two Audiences
🎵 Song of the Lamb → Believers

Believers are told to eagerly await the Lord's coming and reign at the end of the Bowl judgments. Their confidence rests in Christ's soon return to reign.

🎵 Song of Moses → Orthodox Jews

It gives hope to the Jews of the Tribulation, especially the Orthodox Jew who does not yet believe in Jesus. It reminds them that these events are not outside God's control and that blessing will also come after the atonement.

VI

The Tabernacle in Heaven — The Glory of God Made Visible

Revelation 15:5–7 After these things I looked, and behold, the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened… And out of the temple came the seven angels having the seven plagues, clothed in pure bright linen, and having their chests girded with golden bands.
The Heavenly Tabernacle
  • ✦
    The true tabernacleIn heaven exists the true tabernacle, of which Moses' tabernacle was a copy (Heb. 9:23–24). In the innermost chamber, the Most Holy Place, is the ark and above it the mercy seat where the glory of God rests.
  • ✦
    Satan and the cherubimEzekiel 28 tells us that Satan was one of the cherubim whose role was to guard the mercy seat in the heavenly tabernacle. The Shekinah glory rests there.
  • ✦
    The glory departed in 605 B.C.Just before the Babylonian invasion of Israel, the Shekinah glory of God departed from the temple. It has not returned since. It will not return until the Kingdom begins and is established in the temple (Ezekiel 43).
The Meaning of Opening the Tabernacle
  • ✦
    Announcement of the imminent returnThe opening of the tabernacle means: "My return is about to happen." The visible glory of God — Jesus — is about to return to the earth. It announces His long-awaited return.
  • ✦
    Ezekiel prophesied itEzek. 43:5–9: The glory of the Lord will fill the house. That is the place of His throne where He will dwell among the children of Israel forever. The next time the glory returns to the temple will be with the beginning of the Millennium.
  • ✦
    The bowls emerge from the templeWith that, the angels with the seven bowls come out of the temple. The wrath of God, described as something that is stored or accumulated in a bowl (Romans 2:5), will now be poured out in full measure.
VII

The Temple Filled with Smoke — A Period "Without" Grace

Revelation 15:8 The temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God and from His power, and no one was able to enter the temple till the seven plagues of the seven angels were completed.
Access to the Temple Blocked
  • ✦
    Who enters the temple?Under the Law, only priests could enter the temple. Only the High Priest could enter the Most Holy Place. In heaven, there is only one High Priest: Jesus.
  • ✦
    Jesus cannot intercedeThe indication is that the Father has prevented Jesus from entering the temple until the end of the Bowl judgments. If Jesus cannot enter the temple, He cannot go before the Father to intercede. If He cannot intercede, grace cannot be offered.
  • ✦
    No new faith during the bowlsThis is yet another indication that new faith on earth is not possible until the end of the Bowl judgments. It is a period without Christ's active mediation.
Transition to the End of the Tribulation
  • ✦
    Chapter 15: a transitional chapterWith chapter 15 complete, it takes us beyond the midpoint of the Tribulation and tells us what the second half will look like. With Chapter 16 we move into the final weeks.
  • ✦
    Overlapping eventsJust like the midpoint, the end of the seven years involves a complex set of overlapping events. Charts and diagrams will be used to improve understanding throughout the study.
VIII

Chapter 16: The Seven Bowls of Wrath — Bowls 1 to 5

Revelation 16:1–11 Then I heard a loud voice from the temple saying to the seven angels, "Go and pour out the bowls of the wrath of God on the earth."
The First Five Bowls
  • ❶
    Bowl #1 — Foul soresFoul and loathsome sores on all who have the mark of the beast. They break open the flesh in unimaginable ways — comparable to the scourging of Jesus' body.
  • ❷
    Bowl #2 — The seas turn to bloodAll marine life dies. The seas become blood as of a dead man. The blood would coagulate and become putrid. End of navigation and collapse of the water cycle.
  • ❸
    Bowl #3 — Rivers and springs turn to bloodEvery freshwater source turns to blood. The angel declares that the world deserves to be without water because they shed the blood of saints and prophets. How long can someone survive without water? Only a few days — which confirms that the bowls occur at the end of the 7 years.
Bowls 4 and 5 — Heat and Darkness
  • ❹
    Bowl #4 — The sun scorches menThe sun suddenly increases in intensity to the point of scorching men with fire. No rain (seas turned to blood), no freshwater sources, and now a scorching sun. The heat would increase the rate of decomposition of dead animals.
  • ❺
    Bowl #5 — Darkness over the beast's throneThe Antichrist's kingdom is covered in darkness so intense it causes unbearable pain. It is not simply the absence of physical light — it is spiritual darkness. Similar to how the Father withdrew His presence from Jesus on the cross; the world is left at the mercy of spiritual forces.
  • ✦
    The world's response: blasphemyIn verses 9 and 11: men continue to blaspheme God without repentance. The sinful nature of an unbeliever is eternal; therefore their rebellion will also be. And for the same reason, the punishment of an unbeliever must be eternal.
IX

Theological Significance of the Bowls — A Preview of Hell and the Exodus Parallel

A Preview of Hell
  • ✦
    The pattern of the bowlsScorching heat, darkness, no water, no relief, at the mercy of the enemy, no respite from physical torment. The world is experiencing a preview of hell.
  • ✦
    Hell is the wrath of God poured outHell itself is a place that continuously experiences the outpouring of God's wrath. It makes sense that wrath poured out on earth would feel similar.
  • ✦
    The wrath Jesus took for usThe wrath of God — His absence and what that absence produces — was meant for us. But it was Jesus who experienced it for us on the cross. In exchange, we receive salvation and eternal life.
The Exodus Parallel
  • ✦
    Liberation, not destructionIn the Exodus, Israel was captive in Egypt and the Lord used judgments to free them. Here again, Israel is captive to the Antichrist, and the Lord pours out judgment to free His people, not to destroy them.
  • ✦
    Bowls 1, 2, 3, and 5 have parallels in the ExodusThe connection is intentional: it reminds us that these judgments ultimately lead the nation to a good outcome. The Exodus judgments brought freedom and entrance into the Promised Land; these will bring Israel into the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    Purpose of the first five judgmentsThey are a systematic delivery of hell to earth in preparation for the Lord's return. They serve as plagues (echoing the Exodus), and as the backdrop for the events of chapters 17, 18, and 19.
X

The Battle of Armageddon — Introduction and Stages

What Is the Battle of Armageddon
  • ✦
    Not just a single battleThe Battle of Armageddon is not just a military confrontation, but a series of events spanning days and weeks. It takes place in different locations and involves many places, groups, and figures.
  • ✦
    Five stagesThis war brings the Tribulation to an end and leads to the Second Coming of Christ. The first two stages are set in motion by the two final Bowl judgments (the sixth and the seventh).
  • ✦
    Details in the O.T.Revelation gives a general summary; the crucial aspects are found in the Old Testament prophets. The descriptions are spread throughout Rev. 16–19, which bring resolution to the great themes of Scripture from Genesis.
Babylon at the Center
  • ✦
    The most important enemyIn particular, God judges and eliminates the enemy and his most important influence upon humanity: Babylon. It is a mysterious and complex theme that requires careful explanation.
  • ✦
    Relationship between the bowls and ArmageddonThe first five bowls gave the world a sense of urgency. The sixth bowl gave the opportunity by drying up the Euphrates River. The two final judgments launch the war that ends everything.
XI

The Sixth Bowl — The Euphrates Dries Up and Armageddon Stage I

Revelation 16:12–16 Then the sixth angel poured out his bowl on the great river Euphrates, and its water was dried up, so that the way of the kings from the east might be prepared. And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs coming out of the mouth of the dragon, out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet… to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty. And they gathered them together to the place called in Hebrew, Armageddon.
The Euphrates River Dried Up
  • ✦
    The main river of MesopotamiaThe Euphrates River runs near the ancient city of Babylon, in present-day Iraq. It had already been turned to blood; drying up this river that is now blood is a distinct judgment.
  • ✦
    The Antichrist's seven kingsThe Tribulation begins with ten kings in power, which become seven when the Antichrist rises. The river is dried up to prepare a way for these seven kings to travel from east to west toward Israel.
  • ✦
    Why is drying the river a judgment?It is a judgment because it sets in motion a war that ultimately brings death to all unbelievers and the defeat of Satan and the Antichrist. The Lord is moving the pieces as on a chessboard.
The Unclean Spirits and Har-Megiddo
  • ✦
    Three spirits like frogsFrom the dragon, the beast, and the false prophet come three unclean spirits (demons), described as frogs, which is an O.T. symbol for an unclean spirit. They go to the kings of the whole earth.
  • ✦
    The Jezreel ValleyHar-Megiddo = "har" (hill or mountain in Hebrew) + Megiddo (a city in northern Israel in the Jezreel Valley). Armageddon in English. The armies will gather here to prepare for the great war against God.
  • ✦
    Satan's planSatan determines that he must prepare to destroy Christ at His return. Using the influence of the Antichrist and the False Prophet, he calls the world to fight as one against Christ. He points to the desperate state of the world as the only hope for survival.
Strategy: If He Destroys the Jews, Jesus Cannot Return
  • ✦
    Attack on JerusalemThe Antichrist's forces gather in northern Israel for a massive assault on the Lord's city. The enemy moves his forces to the Valley of Armageddon as a staging area, but the real battle will take place in Jerusalem.
  • ✦
    The goal: make Christ's return impossibleBy destroying the Jews remaining in Jerusalem, he hopes to make Christ's return impossible. What they do not know is that Christ is gathering them all in one place to destroy them all at once.
  • ✦
    Armageddon Stage IThe movement of the Antichrist's forces from his capital city (Babylon, east of the Euphrates) toward Israel (to the west). The drying of the river confirms that Babylon is his headquarters.
XII

Warning to the Believer During the Tribulation

Revelation 16:15 "Behold, I am coming as a thief. Blessed is he who watches, and keeps his garments, lest he walk naked and they see his shame."
For the Believer in the Tribulation
  • ✦
    The coming is expected and nearly datableFor those who believe during the Tribulation, the Lord's coming is expected and its date is practically known. But for the unbelieving world, it will come as a thief in the night.
  • ✦
    Parallel to the letter to Laodicea (Rev. 3)Earlier in Revelation 3, the Lord warned the church about the Rapture: it would come unexpectedly and only believers would be received into heaven. Jesus told the unbelievers in the church to be ready since they would not know the hour of His coming.
  • ✦
    The same problem, a different momentLikewise, the world will not recognize that the Second Coming of Jesus is imminent. Therefore they must know and believe before that day arrives. It is the same problem at a different moment. The call is the same: believe in Me, while today is still called "today."
The Time of Jacob's Trouble
Jeremiah 30:7 Alas! For that day is great, so that none is like it; and it is the time of Jacob's trouble, but he shall be saved out of it.

God is pouring out His wrath in final judgments before the Second Coming. Six of the seven judgments have already come. The first five produced destruction so devastating that life is practically impossible, so the end must come quickly.

XIII

The Seventh Bowl — "It Is Done" and the Fall of Babylon

Revelation 16:17–21 Then the seventh angel poured out his bowl into the air, and a loud voice came out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, "It is done!" And there were noises and thunderings and lightnings; and there was a great earthquake… Now the great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell. And great Babylon was remembered before God… And great hail from heaven fell upon men, each hailstone about the weight of a talent.
The Bowl Poured into the Air
  • ✦
    The atmosphere as an oceanThe final judgment is poured into the air — the atmosphere of our earth is an ocean that surrounds the planet. Just as the previous judgments were poured into bodies of water, this one is poured into the great body of air. Like ink in a pool: God's wrath goes everywhere on earth.
  • ✦
    An unprecedented earthquakeAn earthquake that produces unique destruction unlike any ever seen. The damage must be on par with Noah's flood, except without water. Mountains and islands (which are mountaintops rising from the sea) sink into the earth's crust.
  • ✦
    A hailstorm of 100-pound stonesThe Bible speaks of hail as stones falling from the sky. God destroys the world with 100-pound rocks, resulting in the complete destruction of anything made by man. People recognize this is God's work but blaspheme His name in response.
"It Is Done" — Fulfilled
  • ✦
    God's wrath is completeThe Greek word for "done" can also mean "fulfilled." The wrath of God and His purposes in the Tribulation come to an end with this final bowl. As promised in Rev. 15:1: in these plagues the wrath of God is complete.
  • ✦
    Parallel with the crossWhen Jesus completed the work of redemption, He declared it was also finished: John 19:30: "It is finished." On the cross, Jesus took the wrath of God upon Himself for our sake. Once Jesus received that wrath, He declared that God's wrath against the elect was over.
Upon Whom God's Wrath Is Poured
  • ✦
    Wrath for sin: a just measureAll the sins of all people throughout all of history produced a measure of God's wrath. This wrath is poured out in two ways: on Jesus at the cross (for those who will be saved), or on each one who rejects Jesus.
  • ✦
    Not one drop more or lessGod knew from the beginning all who would be saved and decreed an exact and just judgment upon Christ. Not one drop more or less of wrath was poured out on Jesus. Both sentences were just because they come from a just judgment.
  • ✦
    Free will and sovereigntyThis does not suggest that God decided who would be saved or not — it is a personal choice arising from free will. But it does suggest that God knows the end from the beginning (John 3:36).
The Great City Split in Three — Jerusalem or Babylon?
  • ✦
    The term "great city" only in RevelationThe term "great city" appears in only eight verses in all of Scripture, all in Revelation, and in every case it refers to the same place: Babylon.
  • ✦
    The reference in Rev. 11:8In Rev. 11:8 Jerusalem was described by four names: "the great city," Sodom, Egypt, and the place where Jesus was crucified. Only the fourth tells us clearly that it is Jerusalem; the first three are comparative — Jerusalem is like Babylon, like Sodom, like Egypt in its sin.
  • ✦
    Conclusion: it is always BabylonThe term "great city" is always a reference to Babylon, which will now become the center of the story in chapters 17 and 18.
Purpose of the Seventh Bowl: Removing the Gentile Cities
  • ✦
    All the Gentile cities fallThe Greek word "ethnos" (nations) can also be translated as Gentiles. Every Gentile city anywhere on earth is eliminated.
  • ✦
    Only Jerusalem remainsOnly Jerusalem has been excluded from these judgments. Since Jerusalem sits on a mountain and that mountain remains, Jerusalem stands intact and rises above all other cities — literally the highest point on all the earth.
  • ✦
    The Lord has made very clear which city He favorsAt the end of all the judgments, only one city rises above all others: the city of God. The stage is set for the final battle between good and evil, which will end this age.
The Next Step: The Fall of Babylon
  • ✦
    The most important judgment of the TribulationThe final judgment focuses on the fall of a great city — Babylon — and that fall is the most important event of the Tribulation. It is so important that it is explained in greater detail in chapters 17 and 18.
  • ✦
    The Lord as chess masterThe Lord is directing the enemy's actions like pieces on a chessboard, bringing everything to its appointed end. The fall of Babylon is the next stage of the Battle of Armageddon.
✦
Chapters 15 and 16 of Revelation transport us to the threshold of the end: heaven opens to reveal the glory of God ready to break into history, the martyrs sing their victory from the other side, and the final Bowl judgments transform the earth into a foretaste of hell, while Satan — desperate and aware that his time is running out — moves his pieces toward Israel believing he can thwart Christ's return. But it is the Lord Himself who directs those pieces, gathering His enemies in one place to destroy them together. With the heavenly cry "It is done," God's wrath against sin reaches its just end — just as it was declared finished on the cross — and only Jerusalem remains standing, elevated above all the cities of the world, signaling that the King of kings is about to claim His throne.
