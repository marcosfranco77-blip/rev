I

Introduction: The Battle of Armageddon as a Series of Events

Nature of the War
  • ✦
    Not a single moment The Battle of Armageddon — called in the Bible "the war of the great day of God" — does not occur in a single instant, but is a series of events that take place over days and weeks.
  • ✦
    Conclusion of the Tribulation This war concludes the seven-year Tribulation. At its climax, Jesus returns to put an end to the Antichrist’s rule.
  • ✦
    Revelation 19 is a summary Chapter 19 summarizes the Lord’s return, omitting many details already described in previous Old Testament books. To understand them, it is necessary to step outside the Book of Revelation and consult the Old Testament prophecies.
  • ✦
    Chain triggered by the seventh bowl The final bowl judgments triggered a chain of events like dominoes that initiates the war and moves it through its five stages until the end.
Current State at the Start of This Lesson
  • ✓
    Stage I — studied The sixth bowl dried up the Euphrates River to draw the Antichrist and his forces from Babylon westward, camping in the Valley of Jezreel preparing to invade Jerusalem.
  • ✓
    Stage II — studied Two of the seven kings under the Antichrist rebelled and invaded Babylon. The seventh bowl destroyed both the city and the invaders, forcing the Antichrist to advance against Jerusalem.
  • ✓
    Stage III — studied The Antichrist moves his forces south through the central valleys to the Shephelah, then advances eastward surrounding and besieging Jerusalem. The war returns to a medieval style (horses, swords, ramps) due to the rudimentary level of technology during the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    Second front: Petra The Antichrist also opens a second front by sending what remains of his forces from Babylon toward Petra (Bozrah), where the Lord protects the remnant of Israel during the last 3.5 years of the Tribulation.
II

The Purpose of the Tribulation: Six Reasons Given by Daniel

Daniel 9:24 — The Six Goals of the Seventy Weeks
Daniel 9:24 "Seventy weeks are determined For your people and for your holy city, To finish the transgression, To make an end of sins, To make reconciliation for iniquity, To bring in everlasting righteousness, To seal up vision and prophecy, And to anoint the Most Holy."
  • ❶
    To finish the transgression (injustice) The era of Israel’s disobedience comes to its definitive end.
  • ❷
    To make an end of sins The sin of the nation is completely resolved.
  • ❸
    To make reconciliation for iniquity Christ’s atoning work is fully applied to the nation.
  • ❹
    To bring in everlasting righteousness The beginning of eternal righteousness, the Messianic Kingdom.
  • ❺
    To seal up vision and prophecy The definitive fulfillment of all Old Testament prophecies.
  • ❻
    To anoint the Most Holy The formal installation of Christ as King over Israel and the nations.

In summary: the Tribulation brings saving faith to all Israel and causes the nation to return to the Lord, placing the nation in an incredibly difficult situation after seven years of intense testing.

III

The Mosaic Covenant: The Consequences of Breaking It

A Multigenerational Covenant
  • ✦
    National and hereditary covenant When Israel entered the Mosaic Covenant before entering the Promised Land, Moses made it clear that this arrangement was not only for the Jews of the desert but for all their descendants. Deuteronomy 29:14-15.
  • ✦
    The individual and the nation go together "As the individual goes, so goes the nation; and as the nation goes, so goes the individual." The requirements and consequences apply equally to every person until the covenant is fulfilled.
  • ✦
    The root of bitterness Moses warned against any person whose heart turns away from the Lord to serve other gods, for that would produce "gall and wormwood" (adversity and bitterness) for the entire nation. Deuteronomy 29:18.
How the Covenant Is Fulfilled
  • ①
    For the individual: faith in Christ An individual Jew is freed from the Law when he puts his faith in Jesus Christ, who fully fulfilled it in His own life, on behalf of the believer, and paid the death penalty as the consequence of sin. Thus he enters a new and better covenant.
  • ②
    For the nation: collective confession The Old Covenant requires that the entire nation make the confession, together, without exceptions. As a nation, the only way out is the fulfillment of the law; but for the individual, the way out is Jesus.
  • ③
    Jesus did not remove the Law That is why Jesus said He "did not come to remove the Law." The rest of the nation that did not come to personal faith remains subject to the Law and its punishments. Romans 7:4-6 explains that we are free from it only through the death and resurrection of Christ.
The Curses of the Covenant — Described in Deuteronomy 29
Deuteronomy 29:22-25 The coming generation of your children who rise up after you, and the foreigner who comes from a far land, would say, when they see the plagues of that land and the sicknesses which the LORD has laid on it: 'The whole land is brimstone, salt, and burning; it is not sown, nor does it bear, nor does any grass grow there, like the overthrow of Sodom and Gomorrah, Admah, and Zeboim, which the LORD overthrew in His anger and His wrath.' All nations would say, 'Why has the LORD done so to this land? What does the heat of this great anger mean?' Then people would say: 'Because they have forsaken the covenant of the LORD God of their fathers...'

These judgments described in Deuteronomy have never been experienced in Israel to this day. They are a description of the devastation that will come during the Day of the Lord, the seven-year Tribulation. The curses are severe, but they evidence the grace of God because they lead toward the good: the salvation of Israel.

IV

Grace in the Midst of Judgment: Israel’s Disobedience as an Opportunity for the Gentiles

Israel set aside temporarily
Romans 11:7-8, 11 What then? Israel has not obtained what it seeks; but the elect have obtained it, and the rest were blinded. Just as it is written: "God has given them a spirit of stupor, Eyes that they should not see And ears that they should not hear, To this very day." ... I say then, have they stumbled that they should fall? Certainly not! But through their fall, to provoke them to jealousy, salvation has come to the Gentiles.
  • ✦
    The Church Age made possible because of Israel The Lord sets aside His people for a time so that salvation is available to the Gentiles. The period of the Church Age is possible precisely because of the temporary hardening of Israel.
Judgment leads to Israel’s repentance
  • ✦
    The Lord will use judgment as an instrument The period of Israel’s judgment will bring the nation back to the Lord, as indicated by Daniel and Zechariah. In their trial, the nation will cry out to the Lord and He will hear them.
  • ✦
    The condition for Christ’s return Jesus established that this must happen before He is willing to return to His people and give them the Kingdom. Luke 13:34-35: "you shall see Me no more till you say, 'Blessed is He who comes in the name of the LORD!'"
V

The Triggering Cause of the Second Coming: Israel’s National Confession

What Jesus Requires
Luke 13:34-35 "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, the one who kills the prophets and stones those who are sent to her! How often I wanted to gather your children together, as a hen gathers her brood under her wings, but you were not willing! See! Your house is left to you desolate; and assuredly, I say to you, you shall not see Me until the time comes when you say, 'Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord!'"

Jesus established the terms of His second coming: Israel must cry out for Him as their Messiah. When they call Him, He will return as He promised. Beyond the gospel reaching every corner of the world, the true trigger is Israel’s national confession.

The "escape hatch" in Leviticus 26
Leviticus 26:40-42 "But if they confess their iniquity and the iniquity of their fathers, with their unfaithfulness in which they were unfaithful to Me... then I will remember My covenant with Jacob, and My covenant with Isaac and My covenant with Abraham I will remember..."
  • ①
    Personal confession Each Jew must confess his own sin and his need of a Savior — just like any believer. This is sufficient for individual salvation.
  • ②
    National confession of the sin of the ancestors This future generation must confess that their ancestors crucified Jesus at His first coming, that the Messiah already came and was rejected. The entire nation will repent of having rejected Him.
Romans 11 — All Israel Will Be Saved
Romans 11:25-27 For I do not desire, brethren, that you should be ignorant of this mystery, lest you should be wise in your own opinion, that blindness in part has happened to Israel until the fullness of the Gentiles has come in. And so all Israel will be saved, as it is written: "The Deliverer will come out of Zion, And He will turn away ungodliness from Jacob; For this is My covenant with them, When I take away their sins."
  • ✦
    The heavenly Zion The "Zion" mentioned in Romans 11:26 is the heavenly Zion — a reference to the Second Coming of Christ from heaven, not the earthly Zion.
  • ✦
    The old covenant referred to in Romans 11:27 It refers to the Mosaic Covenant, not Abraham’s. The Abrahamic Covenant promises the Kingdom; the Mosaic establishes the condition of confession that releases its fulfillment. When the nation confesses, the Lord will remember the covenant with Abraham and establish the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    He who endures to the end Matthew 24:13 — This is the only moment in history in which "staying alive" guarantees salvation if one is Jewish: only at the last moment, when Israel is about to be destroyed and only a third remains, do they come to confess Christ by the power of the Holy Spirit.
VI

The Situation at the End of the Tribulation: The Full Panorama

Snapshot of the Situation
✅ What Is Still Standing
  • ✦
    Jesus in heaven with His bride (the Church) ready to return.
  • ✦
    The believing Jews in Petra, alive and protected by the Lord, waiting for His return.
⚠ What Is in Crisis
  • ✦
    Babylon gone; the rest of the world in ruins; food and water scarce.
  • ✦
    The unbelieving Jews in Jerusalem pleading with God for rescue while the Antichrist surrounds and attacks them.

The only thing missing is for the unbelieving Jews in Jerusalem to obey the Lord’s demand to declare Him Messiah. The moment they say "Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord!", Jesus will hear them and return to save them all (Luke 13:34-35).

VII

Zechariah 14: What Happens Inside Jerusalem

The Final Siege According to Zechariah
Zechariah 14:1-2 Behold, the day of the LORD is coming, And your spoil will be divided in your midst. For I will gather all the nations to battle against Jerusalem; The city shall be taken, The houses rifled, And the women ravished. Half of the city shall go into captivity, But the remnant of the people shall not be cut off from the city.
  • ✦
    Unfulfilled prophecy until now The gathering of all the nations of the earth against Jerusalem has never happened before — it is prophecy yet to be fulfilled, describing Stage III of the Battle of Armageddon.
  • ✦
    Partial victory of the Antichrist The walls fall, half the city is exiled and the women are assaulted. God intervenes to prevent total conquest, keeping a remnant under extreme pressure.
God’s Trap for the Antichrist
Zechariah 12:2-3 "Behold, I will make Jerusalem a cup of drunkenness to all the surrounding peoples, when they lay siege against Judah and Jerusalem. And it shall happen in that day that I will make Jerusalem a very heavy stone for all peoples; all who would heave it away will surely be cut in pieces, though all nations of the earth are gathered against it."
  • ✦
    The partial victory is preparation for total defeat The Lord has set a trap for the Antichrist: He draws him to besiege the city and allows initial success only to prepare his total defeat. The extreme pressure on the city will create the conditions for collective repentance.
  • ✦
    The Lord defends what remains Zechariah 12:8-9: "In that day the LORD will defend the inhabitants of Jerusalem... In that day I will make the governors of Judah like a firepan in the woodpile..." The Lord will seek to destroy all the nations that come against the city.
VIII

The Outpouring of the Holy Spirit and the National Confession (Zechariah 12:10)

The Climactic Moment
  • ✦
    The city seems lost If no Jews remained, there would be no one to invoke Jesus — the condition for His return. Theoretically, if Israel is destroyed, Jesus would not return a second time. That is why the enemy has opposed Israel from the beginning: Israel was the key to the first coming and also to the second.
  • ✦
    The turning point begins with the Spirit As with all works of salvation, the Lord moves first. When the nation appears on the brink of annihilation, God sends His Spirit.
Zechariah 12:10 "And I will pour on the house of David and on the inhabitants of Jerusalem the Spirit of grace and supplication; then they will look on Me whom they pierced. Yes, they will mourn for Him as one mourns for his only son, And grieve for Him as one grieves for a firstborn."
On Whom and How It Occurs
  • ✦
    Two target groups The house of David (the ruling class of Israel) and the inhabitants of Jerusalem still alive in the city — the remaining group that was not exiled.
  • ✦
    Instant recognition The effect of the arrival of the Spirit is an instant recognition that Jesus is the Messiah. They look on the One they pierced and mourn for Him as for an only son.
  • ✦
    Recognition does not bring initial joy Belief does not result in immediate joy, because the Spirit also reveals that Israel killed their Messiah. The Jews assume it is too late, that they lost the opportunity. They think: "We killed Him. Why would He save us now?"
  • ✦
    It is not a group experience This happens while each family is isolated. It is not the result of conventional evangelism. Each Jew is convicted individually and simultaneously by the Spirit.
Four Groups That Represent the Entire Spectrum of Israel (Zechariah 12:11-14)
Group Representation
House of David The ruling class, the governors of the nation
House of Nathan The prophets, the spokesmen of God
House of Levi The priests, the religious ministers
Family of Shimei The common Jews, the ordinary people

The great and the small, the learned and the unlearned, the privileged and the common — all are coming to the same faith in Jesus. All the families that remain in the city weep and confess, each one separately, independently.

IX

The Psalms of Israel’s Repentance at the End of the Tribulation

Psalm 79: The Repentant Voice of the Nation
Psalm 79:1-13 (key fragment) O God, the nations have come into Your inheritance; Your holy temple they have defiled; They have laid Jerusalem in heaps... Do not remember against us the iniquities of our forefathers; Let Your tender mercies come speedily to meet us... Help us, O God of our salvation, For the glory of Your name; And deliver us, and provide atonement for our sins, For Your name’s sake.

Psalm 79 captures the repentant voice of the nation at the precise moment when the Antichrist presses his attack against the city. It is the cry of the besieged people who recognize their sin and seek divine mercy.

Psalm 80: The Climax of the Confession
Psalm 80:14-19 (NKJV) Return, we beseech You, O God of hosts; Look down from heaven and see, And visit this vine And the vineyard which Your right hand has planted, And the branch that You made strong for Yourself. ... Let Your hand be upon the man of Your right hand, Upon the son of man whom You made strong for Yourself. Then we will not turn back from You; Revive us, and we will call upon Your name. Restore us, O LORD God of hosts; Cause Your face to shine, And we shall be saved!
  • ✦
    They invoke the Son of God In poetic language they invoke the "man of Your right hand" — the Branch of Jesse, Jesus (Isaiah 11:1). They recognize that He was "burned with fire and cut down" — reference to the rejection of the Messiah at His first coming.
  • ✦
    They ask for a second chance "Send Him again" — they ask that the hand of the Lord be upon the Son of God. They promise that if the Lord revives them, they will not turn their back on Him again and will call upon His name, just as Jesus required.
The Culminating Moment: Not the Return, but the Call

The culminating moment of Jesus’ second coming is not His return, but the call to Jesus that precipitates His return from heaven, after Israel’s repentance. There is a sense of mourning, but also hope of receiving a second chance.

X

Hosea: The Exact Chronology of the Final Battle and the Return

Hosea’s Confirmation: Three Days
Hosea 6:1-3 "Come, and let us return to the LORD; For He has torn, but He will heal us; He has stricken, but He will bind us up. After two days He will revive us; On the third day He will raise us up, That we may live in His sight. Let us know, Let us pursue the knowledge of the LORD. His going forth is established as the morning; He will come to us like the rain, Like the latter and former rain to the earth."
Chronology of the Three Days
Days 1 and 2
The final battle in Jerusalem. The Antichrist attacks the city. Half of the Jews are killed and the women assaulted (Zechariah). The Spirit is poured out; Israel recognizes their Messiah.
End of day 2 — Beginning of day 3
The repentant nation cries out to Christ collectively. Israel invokes the name of the Lord as Jesus required (Matthew 24:13). The last third of the city survives until the third day.
Day 3
The Spirit is poured out on the survivors; the nation responds. Jesus returns. God raises them up and they will live in His sight. The Lord comes "like rain that waters the earth" — an image of the grace of God.

Hosea speaks of the final battle in Jerusalem: the war lasts three days and it is Jesus who ends it. He who endures to the end will be saved (Matthew 24:13).

XI

The Second Coming of Christ: Revelation 19:11-16

The Rider on the White Horse
Revelation 19:11-16 Now I saw heaven opened, and behold, a white horse. And He who sat on him was called Faithful and True, and in righteousness He judges and makes war. His eyes were like a flame of fire, and on His head were many crowns. He had a name written that no one knew except Himself. He was clothed with a robe dipped in blood, and His name is called The Word of God. And the armies in heaven, clothed in fine linen, white and clean, followed Him on white horses. Now out of His mouth goes a sharp sword, that with it He should strike the nations. And He Himself will rule them with a rod of iron. He Himself treads the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God. And He has on His robe and on His thigh a name written: KING OF KINGS AND LORD OF LORDS.
Description of Christ at His Return
  • ✦
    Glorified form Jesus appears in the glorified form He had before and after His time on earth — the same image described in Chapter 1 of Revelation. Eyes like a flame of fire; His earthly image was the exception, not the norm.
  • ✦
    Many crowns They reflect His natural right to rule all nations, in contrast to the limited crowns of the Dragon and the Beast.
  • ✦
    An unknown name He has a name that no one knows yet. This suggests that the name "Yeshua" will not be the final name He will bear in the Kingdom. In the end, all controversy about how to call Jesus will be meaningless. Jesus will continue to be known as the Word of God, but He will bear a new name.
  • ✦
    Robe dipped in blood The blood no longer represents His redeeming blood of the first coming, but the blood of judgment that He applies at His second coming (Revelation 19:15).
  • ✦
    White horse Symbol of ruling. The Antichrist also appeared on a white horse (false ruler), but now the true King appears to replace the false one.
The Armies That Follow Him
  • ✦
    The saints of the Church The heavenly armies clothed in fine linen, white and clean — previously we saw the saints of the Church dressed this way. It is us, the believers, the army that follows Jesus. We will not fight, but we will be present.
  • ✦
    The army of angels Matthew 16:27 — "the Son of Man will come in the glory of His Father with His angels." A second army of angels accompanies Christ at His return.
  • ✦
    Certainty of participation "We cannot say with certainty what we will do tomorrow, but we can be sure by our faith that we will be there on a future day." We are reading about an event in which we have the assurance of participating.
First Coming vs. Second Coming — Fundamental Contrast
First Coming
  • ✓
    He came to preach salvation.
  • ✓
    He showed mercy and forgiveness.
  • ✓
    He did not come to judge.
  • ✓
    Redeeming blood on His garment.
Second Coming
  • ✦
    He comes for judgment and to rule.
  • ✦
    He brings a sword and treads the winepress of wrath.
  • ✦
    He will rule with a rod of iron.
  • ✦
    Blood of judgment on His garment.
XII

The Earthly Perspective of the Return: The Cosmic Blackout

Cosmic Disturbances — Matthew 24
Matthew 24:29-30 "Immediately after the tribulation of those days the sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light; the stars will fall from heaven, and the powers of the heavens will be shaken. Then the sign of the Son of Man will appear in heaven, and then all the tribes of the earth will mourn, and they will see the Son of Man coming on the clouds of heaven with power and great glory."
The Total Blackout of the Universe
Zechariah 14:6-7 It shall come to pass in that day That there will be no light; The lights will diminish. It shall be one day Which is known to the LORD— Neither day nor night. But at evening time it shall happen That it will be light.
  • ✦
    Absolute darkness There is a total blackout of the Universe, leaving the earth in complete and absolute darkness. Every source of light — sun, moon, stars — ceases to give its light.
  • ✦
    Christ: the only source of light In the midst of total blackout, the window to heaven opens and the Lord comes on the clouds. At that moment, the Lord Himself is the only source of light in the entire Universe. Everyone will see Him.
  • ✦
    No one will miss His coming That is why Jesus warned not to follow anyone who claimed to be the Messiah. When He returns, there will be nothing else to see except Him. The effect: the nations will see Him and mourn, knowing that their Judge has returned.
XIII

Conclusion and Outlook: What Comes Next

Closing Point of This Lesson
  • ✦
    Jesus in the air, returning The lesson ends with Jesus in the air, returning to save Israel. The study continues with Stages IV and V of the Battle of Armageddon, which will be battles carried out by Jesus Himself.
  • ✦
    Result of stages IV and V The complete end of the Antichrist and his forces, and the end of the seven-year Tribulation.
  • ✦
    What follows the Lord’s return After the war, the study of the Tribulation comes to an end and transitions to the events after the Lord’s return, including some of the most fascinating prophecies in the entire Bible.
✦

This lesson reveals that the Second Coming of Christ is not a unilateral act, but God’s sovereign response to the cry of His people. Israel, brought to the extreme limit of its existence by the Battle of Armageddon, experiences the outpouring of the Holy Spirit and collectively confesses Jesus as Messiah — fulfilling the conditions of the Mosaic Covenant established in Leviticus 26. Only then does the Lord, Faithful and True, open the heavens, extinguish every source of light in the universe and descend as the only light, at the head of His armies, to establish His eternal Kingdom.
