XII

The Final Interval: 7 Unique Years in History

A Period Without Parallel
  • ✦
    More than 7 years of post-war cleanup After the war of Gog and Magog, the earth undergoes a period of cleanup — 7 months of burial and 7 years of burning weapons — before this creation is eliminated.
  • ✦
    Testimony of faithfulness to Israel This additional time at the end of the Kingdom is itself a testimony of God’s faithfulness to Israel: He fulfilled every promise of the Abrahamic covenant down to the last detail.
  • ✦
    The period most similar to the Garden of Eden It will be a unique time: all humanity believes and is saved, there will be no enemy of God operating on the earth. The closest thing to when Adam and Eve walked with God in a perfect world.
What Still Remains to Be Done
  • ❶
    The judgment of Satan Satan is cast into the lake of fire immediately after the war of Gog and Magog (Rev 20:10), reuniting the three members of the False Trinity in condemnation.
  • ❷
    The Second Resurrection All the unbelieving dead throughout history are resurrected in new bodies to appear before the throne.
  • ❸
    The Great White Throne Judgment The final judgment of all unbelieving humanity and the fallen angels before Christ.
  • ❹
    The New Heavens and the New Earth The creation of the new cosmos and the descent of the New Jerusalem — the era of eternity.
XIII

The Lake of Fire and Brimstone

The Judgment of Satan — Revelation 20:10
Revelation 20:10 “The devil, who deceived them, was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone where the beast and the false prophet are. And they will be tormented day and night forever and ever.”
  • ✦
    It is not the end of his existence but the beginning of a new one Satan’s judgment does not annihilate him. It is the beginning of a new existence in eternal torment — “forever and ever”, an unequivocal statement confirmed by numerous passages in the Old and New Testaments.
  • ✦
    The False Trinity reunited in condemnation The Antichrist and the False Prophet entered the lake of fire at the beginning of the Kingdom. Satan joins them at the end — the three members of the False Trinity gathered in their eternal destiny.
  • ✦
    Where does this place exist now? According to Revelation 14:9–10, the lake exists in the heavenly realms out of our sight, “in the presence of the holy angels.” Currently empty — or containing only the Antichrist and the False Prophet during the Millennium.
Why Is the Torment Eternal?
2 Thessalonians 1:6–9 “...since it is a righteous thing with God to repay with tribulation those who trouble you, and to give you who are troubled rest with us when the Lord Jesus is revealed from heaven with His mighty angels, in flaming fire taking vengeance on those who do not know God, and on those who do not obey the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ. These shall be punished with everlasting destruction from the presence of the Lord and from the glory of His power.”
  • ✦
    It is not a place of restitution The Lake of Fire is not a prison where the wicked “pay their debt” and eventually go free. It is an eternal dwelling for those who cannot enter the presence of God.
  • ✦
    The reason: their fallen nature is permanent They are sinners forever — continually and eternally offending God — because they never had the opportunity to be born again. It is not because of what they did, but because of what they are.
Spiritual Death and the Second Death
  • ✦
    Definition of spiritual death To be eternally separated from the presence of God. Just as the future of the believer is “eternal” life, the future of the unbeliever is also eternal — eternal perdition.
  • ✦
    The Second Death The Bible calls it the “Second Death” — spending eternity away from God. It is eternal because evil, the fallen nature of Satan, his demons, and all unbelieving humanity lasts forever.
  • ✦
    Judgment without formality for the three The Lord cast the Antichrist, the False Prophet, and Satan without formality. Their judgments are perfectly just. However, the rest of humanity will be granted a public hearing.
XIV

The Second Resurrection

First vs. Second Resurrection — Revelation 20:5–6
Revelation 20:5–6 “But the rest of the dead did not live again until the thousand years were finished. This is the first resurrection. Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection. Over such the second death has no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with Him a thousand years.”
First Resurrection — For Believers

At the end of the Tribulation, the Lord resurrected all the different groups of believers to reign with Him during the 1,000 years. It includes Old Testament saints, New Testament saints, and Tribulation saints. Blessed is the one who has part in it — the second death has no power over them.

Second Resurrection — For Unbelievers

At the end of the Millennium, God resurrects all the unbelievers of all history for the final judgment. It is not an event of blessing. They receive new bodies to appear before Christ’s throne and then enter the Second Death.

Logic of the Timing of Judgment
  • ✦
    The parallelism with the First Resurrection Just as the Kingdom could not begin until all believers were ready to enter together, the final judgment had to wait until all unbelievers had lived, died, and were ready to be judged.
  • ✦
    At the end of the Kingdom everyone is ready The last unbeliever dies during the post-millennial period. Only then, when the complete set of unbelieving humanity throughout history is “available,” does the judgment arrive.
The Nature of the Human Being — Two Components
  • ❶
    The eternal spirit (or soul) It comes from the breath of God. It lives forever — it never ceases to exist — in one of two conditions: in the presence of God or separated from it.
  • ❷
    The material body Temporal, it came from the earth and returns to it. However, in the resurrection (first or second), the body is restored — new and permanent — for eternal existence.

XVI

The Great White Throne Judgment (Revelation 20:11–15)

Foundational Text — Revelation 20:11–15
Revelation 20:11–15 “Then I saw a great white throne and Him who sat on it, from whose face the earth and the heaven fled away. And there was found no place for them. And I saw the dead, small and great, standing before God, and books were opened. And another book was opened, which is the Book of Life. And the dead were judged according to their works, by the things which were written in the books. ... And anyone not found written in the Book of Life was cast into the lake of fire.”
The Judge: Jesus Christ
John 5:21–22 “For as the Father raises the dead and gives life to them, even so the Son gives life to whom He will. For the Father judges no one, but has committed all judgment to the Son.”
  • ✦
    Jesus: Judge of the living and the dead Previously Jesus judged the “living” (believers) at the Judgment Seat of Christ assigning eternal rewards. Now He judges the “dead” (unbelievers) at the Great White Throne.
  • ✦
    Acts 10:42 Peter confirms: “He is the one God has appointed as Judge of the living and the dead” — explicit designation of the Father to Christ for this function.
Creation Disappears Instantly
Luke 21:33 “Heaven and earth will pass away, but My words will by no means pass away.”
  • ✦
    “Pheugo” — they fled The Greek word pheugo means “to flee.” The same word Mark uses to describe himself running naked in Gethsemane. That is how quickly the entire physical universe will disappear in an instant.
  • ✦
    No earth, no sun, only the light of Jesus All humanity of history — billions — will be present without any physical creation to sustain them. The only light will be Jesus Himself, since the sun will have gone.
From Where the Dead Will Be Gathered — Three Places
① Hades (Hell)

For unbelieving humanity. Every unbeliever from Cain until the last one of the Kingdom is resurrected. Exception: the Antichrist and the False Prophet already went to the Lake of Fire before the Millennium — the only ones with special treatment.

② The Sea / Abyss (Tehom)

The “sea” in Hebrew tehom is a metaphor for the Abyss: place of confinement for demons and fallen angels. Satan was bound there during the Millennium. There is no evidence of connection between Hades and the Abyss nor of demons tormenting humans.

③ Death

It is not a place but a state of being. “Death gave up the dead” means the first death comes to an end — the bodies are resurrected and leave that state. Death and Hades are distinguished from the Abyss because they are for human beings with bodies.

XVII

The Development of the Judgment — The Books Opened

Every Knee Will Bow
Philippians 2:9–11 “that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those in heaven, and of those on earth, and of those under the earth, and that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.”
  • ✦
    A confession that does not save For the unconverted this confession is no longer valid for salvation. It is not faith in action — it is the evident truth of having Jesus directly before them. Since it does not require faith, there is no salvation possible.
  • ✦
    No one will be able to accuse God Every unrepented sin of the world will be exposed in a single moment. Every unrepented sin, every rebellion, along with all those responsible and the hearts behind it — no one will have anything to say in their defense.
The Book of Life
  • ✦
    The register of the family of God The Book of Life records the names of all who receive the Lord’s mercy. These names were written before the foundation of the world — the family of God perfectly defined before Adam and Eve.
  • ✦
    Why is it present at the judgment of unbelievers? To prove that none of the souls present is worthy of absolution. None confessed Christ, none is in the family of God. If they were, they would have participated in the First Resurrection.
  • ✦
    “If anyone was not found written” John says “if” — not as a real possibility, but to emphasize that the search does not intend to vindicate but to confirm the condemnation. None will be found.
The “Other” Books — Two Interpretations
Interpretation 1 — Record of Personal Works

The books are a recorded history from birth to death of each person — all the sinful actions that make them deserving of judgment. The life of each person is a long history of sin without the grace of God. Duration of the judgment: potentially very long when reciting all the works of billions.

Interpretation 2 — The Five Books of Moses (The Law)

The books are the Law of Moses — the standards of justice. Each person is judged according to whether they meet the requirements of the Law. Duration of the judgment: much shorter, because one single sin is enough to condemn. Breaking one law makes someone a violator of the whole law.

  • ✦
    Identical result in both cases All fail. All are cast “alive” into the lake of fire — conscious, with all their senses, perfectly aware of their situation. This confirms that the Second Death is not annihilation but a new eternal existence.
  • ✦
    The final product of all Creation What remained as the final result of 7,000 years? The family of God — the relationship with Him, the knowledge of His glory, love, mercy, and grace — known by each one of them. Of everything else nothing remained.
  • ✦
    A call to humility for believers “We were born like them and if it were not for the grace of God, we would be in that place of judgment. Let us always remember that we were saved from this moment only by the pure mercy of God.”
The Demons Are Also Judged
Matthew 8:28–29 “What have we to do with You, Jesus, You Son of God? Have You come here to torment us before the time?”
  • ✦
    The demons anticipate their judgment The demons who interacted with Jesus knew that the time of judgment and the Lake of Fire was approaching — “before the time” implies they know their final destiny.
  • ✦
    The Great White Throne judgment is dual The Great White Throne Judgment includes both ungodly humanity (from Hades) and demons and disobedient angels (from the Abyss). All appear before Christ.
XVIII

The Next Era: Eternity and Progressive Revelation

What Remains After the Judgment
  • ✦
    Only three elements persist Once the judgment is concluded: saints in eternal bodies — the faithful angels — the Trinity of the Deity. Everything else has passed.
  • ✦
    Only two chapters describe eternity Everything we know about what follows the Kingdom is found in Revelation 21 and 22 — the last two chapters of the Bible. It raises more questions than it answers.
The Pattern of Progressive Revelation
Hebrews 1:1–2 “God, who at various times and in various ways spoke in time past to the fathers by the prophets, has in these last days spoken to us by His Son.”
  • ✦
    We stand before eternity as the Old Testament stood before Christ The Old Testament saints had revelation, had the Word, prophesied about Christ — but they did not know Him as we know Him. So we stand before the New Heavens and the New Earth: we know only a little.
  • ✦
    Perhaps in the Kingdom we will receive more understanding The pattern of progressive revelation suggests that during the Millennium the Lord may grant us more understanding of what is to come — a mystery that has not yet been fully revealed.
XIX

The New Heavens and the New Earth — Revelation 21:1–8

Central Text — Revelation 21:1–8
Revelation 21:1–4 “Now I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away. Also there was no more sea. Then I, John, saw the holy city, New Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. And I heard a loud voice from heaven saying, ‘Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and He will dwell with them, and they shall be His people. ... God will wipe away every tear from their eyes; there shall be no more death, nor sorrow, nor crying. There shall be no more pain, for the former things have passed away.’”
Revelation 21:5–6 “Then He who sat on the throne said, ‘Behold, I make all things new.’ ... ‘It is done! I am the Alpha and the Omega, the Beginning and the End. I will give of the fountain of the water of life freely to him who thirsts.’”
The Parallelism with Genesis — Revelation as Restoration
  • ✦
    Genesis 1:1 ↔ Revelation 21:1 “In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth” ↔ “Now I saw a new heaven and a new earth.” The parallelism will continue through chapters 21 and 22, revealing that all Creation is returning to the perfect state before Adam’s sin.
  • ✦
    Not the same earth, but corrected They are not the same heavens nor the same earth, but all imperfections have been corrected. All the consequences of Adam’s sin — losses, deaths, damages, destruction — have been rectified and restored.
  • ✦
    Genesis 2:8 ↔ Revelation 21:2 In Genesis, God planted a garden and put man in it (Adam descended). In eternity, it is we who are on the earth and the city descends toward us — a beautiful inversion of the original pattern.
The Mystery of the Absent Sea
  • ✦
    “There was no more sea” The new earth will completely lack seas — in marked contrast to the current earth that is more sea than land. This detail will be explained later in chapter 22.
  • ✦
    It was not a design error Scripture says God makes no mistakes nor changes His mind. The sea had a purpose in the first creation. There must be a reason why it is not necessary in the second — the text of Revelation 22 will reveal it.
Five Restored Realities in the New Heavens and the New Earth
  • ❶
    The Trinity dwells among men For the first time since the Garden of Eden, the Father Himself will dwell on the earth — not only the Holy Spirit nor only Jesus. We will live with God as Adam once did.
  • ❷
    No sign of sin remains No more tears, no more death, no more sorrow, no more crying, no more pain. Everything that exists because of sin — which corrupted humanity and brought curses upon all creation — will have disappeared forever.
  • ❸
    Jesus makes all things new “Behold, I make all things new” (Rev 21:5). He is the Alpha and the Omega — in the beginning the Word created the heavens and the earth; in the end, Jesus creates all things again. He truly is the Beginning and the End of Creation.
  • ❹
    Jesus: source of all life and righteousness “To him who thirsts, I will give of the fountain of the water of life freely” — allusion to the Spirit of life. Those who overcome sin, death, and the enemy are those who put their faith in Jesus the Alpha and Omega.
  • ❺
    The overcomer inherits all things “He who overcomes shall inherit all things, and I will be his God and he shall be My son” (Rev 21:7). The intimate relationship between God and His children — promised since Eden — completely restored and magnified.
XX

The New Jerusalem — The City That Descends

A City Prepared in Heaven
Hebrews 11:15–16 “But now they desire a better, that is, a heavenly country. Therefore God is not ashamed to be called their God, for He has prepared a city for them.”
Philippians 3:20 “For our citizenship is in heaven, from which we also eagerly wait for the Savior, the Lord Jesus Christ.”
  • ✦
    It already exists right now Scripture teaches that the New Jerusalem already exists now in the heavenly realm, waiting for the day it will appear. It is not something that will be built — it is already prepared.
  • ✦
    Not built with human hands The Lord causes the city to descend to the new earth — made in heaven, not by human work. Its appearance will be witnessed from the earth.
The Adorned Bride — A Dramatic Image
  • ✦
    “Prepared as a bride adorned for her husband” Imagine the moment when we turn to look down the aisle and see a bride walking — she has never looked better than at that moment. Dramatic, anticipated, and celebrated. That is how the appearance of the New Jerusalem will be.
  • ✦
    No one will have seen it before We can safely assume that we will never have seen it before this moment — not even while we were present with Jesus in heaven. It will be a special moment, something to look forward to even during the Millennium.
The Inhabitants of the New Jerusalem (Hebrews 12:22–24)
Hebrews 12:22–24 “But you have come to Mount Zion and to the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem, to an innumerable company of angels, to the general assembly and church of the firstborn who are registered in heaven, ... to God the Judge of all, to the spirits of just men made perfect, to Jesus the Mediator of the new covenant.”
  • ❶
    The Deity The Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit will dwell in the city — the full presence of the Trinity on earth.
  • ❷
    The entire host of angels “An innumerable company of angels” — the complete heavenly host will live in the New Jerusalem.
  • ❸
    The Church of the Firstborn — The Bride of Christ “General assembly and church of the firstborn who are registered in heaven” — the saints of the New Testament church, the Bride of Christ.
  • ❹
    The spirits of just men made perfect All the other believers: Old Testament saints and Tribulation saints. “It will be a great neighborhood that far exceeds the most exclusive zip code of today.”
XXI

The Call to Salvation — Revelation 21:8

A List of Sins That Characterize Unbelief
Revelation 21:8 “But the cowardly, unbelieving, abominable, murderers, sexually immoral, sorcerers, idolaters, and all liars shall have their part in the lake which burns with fire and brimstone, which is the second death.”
  • ✦
    Not an exclusive list of today’s unbelievers A believer may share one or more of these sins from their past or even present. The point is that they are the natural fruit of unbelief, while they are the unnatural fruit of believers.
  • ✦
    In the new era: exclusive to unbelievers On the day of judgment these behaviors will be exclusive to unbelievers — all sin of the believer will have disappeared. Then it will be just to say that those who practice them go to eternal punishment.
  • ✦
    The ultimate criterion: unbelief The definitive criterion of exclusion is not the works themselves, but being an unbeliever — and therefore entering the Lake of Fire. The works are evidence of the nature, not the cause of the condemnation.
  • ✦
    The evangelistic purpose of Revelation As we reach the end of the Bible, we will find more and more calls to believe and receive salvation in Jesus. Revelation exists not only to encourage the believer — but to persuade unbelievers to seek Jesus while they can.
✦

Part 16 closes the arc of judgment and opens the door to eternity. Satan is finally cast into the lake of fire, completing the condemnation of the False Trinity. Every unbeliever in history — from Cain to the last one of the Kingdom — appears before the Great White Throne where Christ judges with perfect justice. Then, the entire universe flees in an instant, and in its place emerges something radically new: new heavens, a new earth without sea, and the New Jerusalem descending from heaven like a bride. All the consequences of Adam’s sin are rectified, the Father Himself will dwell among men, and eternity begins — still wrapped in the mysterious silence of progressive revelation, which promises more than we can yet comprehend.
