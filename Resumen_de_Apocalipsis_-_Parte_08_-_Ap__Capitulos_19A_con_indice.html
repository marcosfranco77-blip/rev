I

Visión General: La Tribulación y sus Tres Grandes Períodos

Map of Revelation Chapters 6–19
Period Chapters Key Events
First Half (3.5 years) Revelation 6–9 7 Seals: war, famine, earthquakes. ¼ of the Earth dies (seal 4). 6 Trumpets, 2 Woe judgments (trumpets 5 and 6). ⅓ of mankind dies (trumpet 6).
Midpoint / Great Tribulation (3.5 years) Revelation 10–15 144,000 sealed. Multitude from the nations in heaven. Satan cast out. Remnant of Israel persecuted but protected. Antichrist dies and rises. Abomination of Desolation. End of sacrifices. Mark of the Beast 666. Death of the 2 Witnesses. 144,000 martyrs in heaven.
Second Half — Final Revelation 16–19 Trumpet 7, third Woe. 7 Bowls. Destiny sealed. Mark and worship of the beast. Martyrs. War of Armageddon. 2nd Coming of Christ.
Summary of Chapters 16–19: The Stages of Armageddon
Ch. 16–18 → Stages I and II

Bowl 6 dries the Euphrates (Stage I). The Antichrist's armies move out of Babylon toward northern Israel. Stage II: destruction of Babylon by human hands and then supernaturally by God.

Ch. 19 → Stages III, IV, and V

Stage III: Antichrist's forces at Bozrah and Jerusalem. Stage IV: Christ's return, supernatural defeat of the armies. Stage V: conclusion of the war and establishment of the Kingdom.

II

Situación al Inicio del Capítulo 19: El Anticristo Acorralado

The Antichrist's Situation
  • ✦
    Driven by Satan, He Has Lost Everything His headquarters Babylon was destroyed in an instant. His allies turned against him and invaded the city. He now sits in northern Israel with what remains of his army.
  • ✦
    Only Jerusalem Remains All that remains is Jerusalem and the last group of Jews on earth still resisting his reign. Since his headquarters is gone, he has no choice but to make Jerusalem his new capital.
  • ✦
    Final Objective: Destroy the Jewish Resistance The plan becomes to kill that final resistance and, in the process, prevent the return of Christ. Destroying all the Jews is the enemy's last hope of saving himself.
The Second Coming: A Process, Not a Moment
  • ✦
    Not a Single Instant The Lord's return is not a simple matter or a single moment. It is a series of events spread over several days, whose climax is Chapter 19.
  • ✦
    Details Outside of Revelation Chapter 19 reports on Christ's return, but the details surrounding His return are found outside of Revelation — in the Old Testament prophets: Isaiah, Jeremiah, Joel, Zechariah, Micah, and Daniel.
  • ✦
    Heavenly → Earthly Pattern Characteristically, Revelation moves from a discussion of heavenly events followed by their impact on earth. Chapter 19 follows this same pattern.
III

Alabanzas en el Cielo: ¡Aleluya! (Ap. 19:1–5)

The Heavenly Song — Rev. 19:1–5
Revelation 19:1–5

Alleluia! Salvation and glory and honor and power belong to the Lord our God! For true and righteous are His judgments, because He has judged the great harlot who corrupted the earth with her fornication; and He has avenged on her the blood of His servants. Again they said, "Alleluia!" And her smoke rises up forever and ever! And the twenty-four elders and the four living creatures fell down and worshiped God…

  • ✦
    "After These Things" — Cause and Effect The chapter opens with "After these things," implying a cause-and-effect sequence. The events of Chapters 17 and 18 are precipitating these events in heaven.
  • ✦
    Hallelujah! — Sung 3 Times All of human history has been pointing to this moment for thousands of years. The drama is undeniable. The great heavenly host acknowledges the significance of this moment — and we are part of that multitude, singing our own future in advance.
  • ✦
    What They Declare They declare that salvation, honor, glory, and power belong to God. He has brought His righteous judgment. He has avenged the persecuted and martyred as He promised the souls under the altar. He has brought an end to the chief adversary: spiritual Babylon in all its forms.
  • ✦
    Worship: A Necessary Christian Discipline Worship is not merely an internal feeling — it only fulfills its purpose of glorifying God through outward expression: words, songs, postures. It is a proper and necessary Christian discipline to express devotion both individually and collectively.
The Church in Heaven — Irrefutable Evidence
Revelation 19:5

Praise our God, all you His servants and those who fear Him, both small and great!

  • ✦
    Servant = Church Saints The term "servant" is a specific reference to Church saints. Their presence in heaven refutes any post-Tribulation claim that the Church remains on earth until the Second Coming.
  • ✦
    Leaders = Worship Leaders The leaders in the heavenly kingdom are also the worship leaders in that kingdom. The elders and four living creatures fall down and worship.
The Seventh Bowl Judgment Precipitated the Second Coming
  • ❶
    Stage I Accomplished The seventh bowl judgment made it possible for the Antichrist to move his forces out of Babylon toward northern Israel.
  • ❷
    Stage II Accomplished This resulted in the destruction of Babylon, the great harlot, by rebel forces and then by God Himself.
  • ❸
    The Way Made Clear With the disappearance of Babylon, the way has been cleared for Christ's return because opposition to His rule is rapidly fading away.
IV

Las Bodas del Cordero (Ap. 19:6–9)

The Great Proclamation: Christ Reigns — Rev. 19:6–9
Revelation 19:6–9

Alleluia! For the Lord God Omnipotent reigns! Let us be glad and rejoice and give Him glory, for the marriage of the Lamb has come, and His wife has made herself ready. And to her it was granted to be arrayed in fine linen, clean and bright, for the fine linen is the righteous acts of the saints. Blessed are those who are called to the marriage supper of the Lamb!

  • ✦
    An Innumerable Great Multitude Declares Like the voice of many waters and thunderings — the greatest possible number. What this great multitude says becomes the focus of the next verses: the time to reign has come.
  • ✦
    The Bride of Christ — The Church Alone The "Bride of Christ" is a New Testament reference to the Church saints — those baptized in the Spirit through faith in Jesus, corresponding to a single dispensation: from Pentecost to the Rapture. John the Baptist and the 144,000 are not part of this body.
The Church as Bride — Grounded in Paul (Eph. 5)
Ephesians 5:25–27, 32

Just as Christ also loved the church and gave Himself for her, that He might sanctify and cleanse her… that He might present her to Himself a glorious church, not having spot or wrinkle… This is a great mystery, but I speak concerning Christ and the church.

  • ✦
    Only With the Indwelling Holy Spirit Paul defines the "Church" as all who have the indwelling of the Holy Spirit (Rom. 8:11). The Church age is the only period where the Spirit indwells people, not a building as in the O.T.
  • ✦
    A Mystery to Be Revealed in Its Day The concept of being the "bride" is metaphorical, not literal like a husband-wife relationship. Paul himself calls it a mystery in Ephesians. We assume it will be revealed to us on that day.
Fine Linen: The Righteous Acts of the Saints
2 Corinthians 5:10 — The Judgment Seat of Christ

For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, that each one may receive the things done in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad.

  • ✦
    Only Truly Righteous Works We will do many works "for" Jesus, but not all are righteous. Those done with selfish motives, for human attention or praise, or without the Spirit's direction — do not count toward reward.
  • ✦
    Preparing for Our Inheritance The Church is preparing to go to the earth and receive our inheritance and the rewards for our service in the Kingdom. The linen represents those righteous works done in genuine faith.
V

La Boda Judía como Metáfora del Rapto y la Segunda Venida

The Jewish Wedding Process — A Mirror of God's Plan
The Betrothal — Without Meeting Face to Face The betrothal occurs before the bride and groom have met face to face — just as we enter into a covenant with Jesus without knowing Him physically. It is a faith that precedes the encounter.
The Waiting Period — The Church on Earth Today There is a waiting period before the groom claims the bride. This is the period in which the Church currently finds itself on earth — waiting for her Bridegroom.
The Bridegroom Comes for the Bride — The Rapture The Bridegroom will come for His Bride in the resurrection (the Rapture) and we will return to the Bridegroom's house for a time in Heaven. It is equivalent to God's presence leaving the earth — exactly as when God's presence departed the temple before Nebuchadnezzar invaded Jerusalem.
The Wedding Banquet — In the Father's House (Rev. 19) After a time with the Bridegroom, the wedding and a celebration take place in the Bridegroom's house. This is what we see in Revelation 19:7 — the marriage supper of the Lamb in heaven.
The Return Journey — The Second Coming The groom and bride travel back to the bride's house for another celebration. This will take place after the Second Coming of Christ — the inauguration of the Kingdom on earth.
Matthew 26:29 — Jesus Promised This Cup

But I say to you, I will not drink of this fruit of the vine from now on until that day when I drink it new with you in My Father's kingdom.

  • ✦
    The Cup Left in Waiting The Lord concluded the Passover meal with a cup of wine that He left in waiting, promising that the next would await the celebration of the Kingdom. That celebration begins with the marriage supper.
The Guests at the Marriage Supper of the Lamb
Revelation 19:9

Blessed are those who are called (Greek: invited) to the marriage supper of the Lamb!

  • ✦
    They Are Not the Bride — They Are the Guests The Bride is not a guest at her own wedding. These are other saints apart from the Church saints: every believer from Abel to John the Baptist, and all those who died in the Tribulation after the Rapture.
  • ✦
    Only in Spirit Form They remain there without bodies, awaiting their turn for resurrection. They watch from their spirit form as the Bride — believers in physical bodies — participates in the marriage supper.
The Rapture as a Parallel to the Departure from the Temple
  • ✦
    Before Babylon: The Presence Departs the Temple Before Babylon invaded Jerusalem, God's presence departed the temple leaving it desolate, surrendering power to Nebuchadnezzar — the beginning of Old Covenant judgment.
  • ✦
    The Rapture: The Presence Departs the Earth In the same way, the Rapture represents the same thing — God's presence departs the earth (He who dwells in the Bride of Christ) immediately before the Tribulation judgment begins.
VI

Juan Adora al Ángel: La Corrección (Ap. 19:10)

Worship God Alone — Jesus Is the Spirit of Prophecy
Revelation 19:10

And I fell at his feet to worship him. But he said to me, "See that you do not do that! I am your fellow servant, and of your brethren who have the testimony of Jesus. Worship God! For the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy."

  • ✦
    The Angel Refuses Worship John marvels at the spectacle and falls to worship the angel. Recall that the last time an angel received worship he was called Satan — so anyone would be unnerved by this. The angel immediately stops John.
  • ✦
    Only a Messenger, Not the Author The angel says: he is only a servant, a messenger. Jesus by His Spirit is the source of all revelation. The author of the message deserves glory, not the conduit. Like water in pipes: we praise the water, not the pipes.
  • ✦
    A Correction to False Worship This corrects much false worship of angels, saints, the virgin, animals, and creation. God alone is worthy of worship — Jesus Himself, the author of the plan of redemption.
From the Heavenly to the Earthly: Pending Events
  • ✦
    A Pause Before Verse 11 In verse 11, John moves to describe the Lord's return, but we are not yet ready for that step. There are several events on earth that were left pending — we need to know how they conclude before returning to Chapter 19.
  • ✦
    Situation on Earth Satan's kingdom has been annihilated, but the Antichrist continues ruling and pursuing the remaining Jews in Jerusalem. His army is moving to take the only city still standing: Jerusalem.
VII

Repaso de las Etapas I y II — El Telón de Fondo

The Three Stages of Armageddon to This Point
Stage I
Movement East → West from Babylon to the Valley of Jezreel Prompted by the Lord drying up the Euphrates River (blood) with the sixth bowl, preparing the Antichrist's mind to attack Jerusalem from northern Israel in Judea.
Stage II
Destruction of Babylon — Human and Supernatural Armies of the kings of the north razed Babylon (human hand). Shortly after, the Lord destroyed the entire city with the final judgment of the seventh bowl (supernatural hand of God). Both stages orchestrated from heaven.
Stage III — Ongoing
The Antichrist Moves Toward Bozrah and Jerusalem The attack on Babylon motivated the Antichrist to move toward Jerusalem. The three focal points of this war are: Jerusalem, the Mount of Olives, and Bozrah / Petra. The action will move back and forth among these three places.
VIII

El Anticristo según Daniel 11: Su Carácter y Sus Movimientos

The Profile of the Antichrist — Daniel 11:36–39
Daniel 11:36–39

Then the king shall do according to his own will: he shall exalt and magnify himself above every god, shall speak blasphemies against the God of gods, and shall prosper till the wrath has been accomplished… But in their place he shall honor a god of fortresses; and a god which his fathers did not know…

  • ✦
    He Exalts Himself Above Every God The Antichrist will do according to his own will and magnify himself above every god. He speaks blasphemies against the God of gods, prospering until God's wrath is accomplished — the timing marked by divine purpose.
  • ✦
    He Worships the God of Fortresses He honors the "god of fortresses" — a god his fathers did not know. With a foreign god he takes control of fortresses and divides the land among his allies.
Two Kings Press the Antichrist — Dan. 11:40–41
Daniel 11:40–41

At the time of the end the king of the South shall attack him; and the king of the North shall come against him like a whirlwind, with chariots, horsemen, and with many ships; and he shall enter the countries, overwhelm them, and pass through. He shall also enter the Glorious Land…

  • ✦
    "At the End" — End of the 7 Years Daniel says "at the time of the end" — that is, at the end of the seven years. These events are parallel to those of Revelation Chapter 18, confirming the destruction of Babylon.
  • ✦
    North (chariots) + South (ships) A king of the north attacks with chariots and horsemen; one from the south with ships. The Hebrew word for "attack" means to push as in a press — they press him toward Israel, which is all he has left.
  • ✦
    Edom, Moab, and Ammon Escape Three areas will escape the Antichrist's hand: Edom, Moab, and Ammon — ancient names for present-day Jordan, east of the Jordan River. This is the region that includes Petra / Bozrah.
The Antichrist Troubled by Reports — Dan. 11:42–45
Daniel 11:44–45

But news from the east and the north shall trouble him; therefore he shall go out with great fury to destroy and annihilate many. And he shall plant the tents of his palace between the seas and the glorious holy mountain; yet he shall come to his end, and no one will help him.

  • ✦
    The News of Babylon's Destruction Reports from the east and north trouble him. Just like Isaiah's report — he learns of Babylon's destruction, which fills him with great fury and drives him to annihilate many.
  • ✦
    Location of the Camp: Between Two Points The glorious holy mountain = Mount Moriah with the temple in Jerusalem (to the east). The seas = the Mediterranean Sea (to the west). He pitches his palace tents between the two — a moving military camp on the western side of Jerusalem, in the Shephelah.
  • ✦
    He Will Come to His End with No Help Daniel 11:45 declares: he shall come to his end and no one will help him. No allies or forces to support him — and nothing could oppose Christ anyway.
IX

Etapa III: Las Fuerzas del Anticristo en Bosra y Jerusalén

Bozrah (Petra): Second Attack on the Protected Jews
Jeremiah 49:13–16, 21–22

Bozrah shall become a desolation, a reproach, a waste, and a curse. And all its cities shall be perpetual wastes… Behold, he shall come up and fly like the eagle, and spread his wings over Bozrah; the heart of the mighty men of Edom in that day shall be like the heart of a woman in birth pangs.

  • ✦
    Bozrah = Petra = "sheepfold or enclosure" At the midpoint of the Tribulation, the Lord escorted the believing Jews to a rocky canyon in southern Jordan (Petra). In Hebrew, Bozrah means sheepfold or enclosure — the Jews will be protected there like sheep.
  • ✦
    The Antichrist's Second Attempt The Antichrist already tried to destroy them at the mid-Trib when the woman fled to the wilderness on eagle's wings. Now he tries again. Jeremiah says the Antichrist's forces there will be ruined.
  • ✦
    The Lord Swoops Down Like an Eagle Jeremiah says the Lord Himself will swoop down like an eagle to defend His people. Bozrah is the first place the Lord visits in His Second Coming.
The Distribution of the Antichrist's Forces
  • ✦
    Forces at Bozrah Remnants who escaped the destruction of Babylon and watched its fall from afar. Jeremiah says the Antichrist sends messengers (probably on horseback) to these forces to instruct them to attack the Jews at Bozrah.
  • ✦
    Forces in the Valley of Jezreel → Shephelah The bulk of his army concentrates in the Valley of Jezreel in northern Israel. It then moves toward the Shephelah (the hills on Jerusalem's western side) where he pitches his tent.
  • ✦
    The Army Surrounds the City on All Sides The forces concentrate in two places: Bozrah and Jerusalem. In Jerusalem, the armies advance from the west and finally surround the city from the east as well — positioned on every side.
X

El Asedio de Jerusalén: Profetas del A.T. dan los Detalles

Joel: The Attack in the Valley of Jehoshaphat (Joel 3:9–12)
Joel 3:9–12

Proclaim this among the nations: "Prepare for war! Wake up the mighty men…" Beat your plowshares into swords and your pruning hooks into spears; let the weak say, "I am strong." Assemble and come, all you nations from all around… for there I will sit to judge all the surrounding nations.

  • ✦
    Improvised Weapons — Agriculture Turned to War The warriors will come having converted agricultural implements into improvised weapons. The Tribulation has reduced men to attacking on horseback with ancient armaments. Stone walls now become effective defenses. This is not a symbolic description — by this point, only this type of weaponry will be available.
  • ✦
    Valley of Jehoshaphat = Kidron Valley They arrive at the Valley of Jehoshaphat — another name for the Kidron Valley along the eastern side of the city. The armies advance from the west and also surround the city from the east. Joel 3:12 says that is where God will judge them.
  • ✦
    Orchestrated by the Lord They come because the Lord has brought them here, in the sense that He has orchestrated the events that led to this battle. The Lord has set His enemies in this trap.
Isaiah: The Siege of Ariel (Isa. 29:1–7)
Isaiah 29:1–7 — Ariel = Jerusalem

Woe to Ariel, to Ariel, the city where David dwelt!… For I will encamp against you all around, I will lay siege against you with a mound, and I will raise siegeworks against you. You shall be brought down… The multitude of your enemies shall be like fine dust… It shall happen instantly, suddenly.

  • ✦
    Ariel — Place of Sacrifice / Lion of God Ariel literally means "altar or place of sacrifice" in Hebrew, and also "Lion of God" — the emblem of the tribe of Judah. It suggests the Lord will make a sacrifice of the people who die in the battle, as an atoning sacrifice for the sins of the nation.
  • ✦
    The Nation Prostrate, Speaking From the Dust During the siege the inhabitants faint, expecting to die. In verse 4, the nation will bow with its face to the ground — a posture of humility and worship. Their voice will rise like a spirit from the dust. They are Jews who do not believe in Christ, but neither did they receive the mark.
  • ✦
    Supernatural and Instantaneous Defeat Isaiah 29:5 says the enemies will be struck down instantly, suddenly. Like chaff carried by the wind, or dust that vanishes. Isaiah 29:7 compares it to a dream — as if that army had never been there.
Micah: God's Trap (Mic. 4:9–12)
Micah 4:11–12

Now also many nations have gathered against you… But they do not know the thoughts of the Lord, nor do they understand His counsel; for He will gather them like sheaves to the threshing floor.

  • ✦
    Why Does Israel Cry Out? — A Pointed Question Micah asks why Israel cries out and why there is no king. These are somewhat pointed questions — God indicates they turned their backs on their king in times past, and now they suffer for it.
  • ✦
    The Trap Is About to Spring The nations rejoice at the opportunity to possess the city, but they do not know the Lord has set this trap for them. The Lord's purpose: to destroy all the Antichrist's forces. Neither party (Israel nor the nations) understands the Lord's purpose at that moment.
XI

Zacarías: El Plan de Dios para Presionar a Israel (Zac. 13–12)

My Shepherd and My Associate — Jesus and the Antichrist (Zech. 13:7–9)
Zechariah 13:7–9

"Awake, O sword, against My Shepherd, against the Man who is My Companion," says the Lord of hosts. "Strike the Shepherd, and the sheep will be scattered…" And it shall come to pass in all the land, says the Lord, that two-thirds in it shall be cut off and die, but one-third shall be left in it: I will bring the one-third through the fire, will refine them as silver is refined, and test them as gold is tested. They will call on My name, and I will answer them. I will say, 'This is My people'; and each one will say, 'The Lord is my God.'

The Shepherd = Jesus / My Associate = The Antichrist
  • ✦
    Reference to the First Coming The Shepherd would be struck down in His day and Israel would be scattered — a clear reference to the Lord's first coming. After Jesus' death, the Lord scattered the Jews in 70 A.D. as punishment for rejecting their Messiah.
  • ✦
    Connection to Genesis 3:15 Zech. 13:7 is a clear reference to Genesis 3:15: "And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel." The conflict between the Messiah and the Antichrist/Satan was promised since Eden.
The Two-Thirds and the Refined Third
  • ✦
    Two-Thirds Cut Off The two parts of northern and southern Israel will be cut off and perish. The Jews living there will die when the Antichrist enters the land.
  • ✦
    One-Third Survives and Is Refined Only the Jews remaining in the middle of the nation — in Jerusalem and also in Bozrah — will be left alive. They will be put through a furnace of testing to refine them like silver and gold. This will lead them to call on the Lord.
The Deliverance of Jerusalem — Zechariah 12:1–9
Zechariah 12:2–9

Behold, I will make Jerusalem a cup of drunkenness to all the surrounding peoples… And it shall happen in that day that I will make Jerusalem a very heavy stone for all peoples; all who would heave it away will surely be cut in pieces… In that day I will make the governors of Judah like a firepan in the woodpile… and they shall devour all the surrounding peoples on the right hand and on the left… In that day I will seek to destroy all the nations that come against Jerusalem.

  • ✦
    Jerusalem: A Cup That Reels and a Stone Too Heavy to Lift The Lord will turn the city into a stone too heavy to lift. When the nations try to carry it or do what they wish with it, they will injure themselves in the attempt. It is a supernatural defense.
  • ✦
    Horses and Riders in Panic and Madness The horses will be struck with panic and blindness, and their riders with madness. The attackers and their animals will become confused and disorganized — this is supernatural. The people of the city will recognize that the Lord is coming to their defense (Zech. 12:5).
  • ✦
    Defended Inside and Outside the Walls The Lord will defend not only those in the city but also the Jews camped outside the walls' protection (Zech. 12:7 — "tents of Judah"). Judah is the one-third God would refine. Even the weakest among them will be like David before Goliath.
XII

El Asedio de Asiria: Imagen Histórica de la Tribulación

The Historical Parallel: Hezekiah and the Assyrians (Isa. 37 / 2 Kings 19)
  • ❶
    Assyria: Sent to Destroy the North Assyria was sent by God to destroy the Northern Kingdom of Israel as punishment for its sins. But Assyria became proud and thought it also had the right to conquer Judah.
  • ❷
    She Overstepped — Besieged Jerusalem The king of Assyria was greedy and decided to attack the south (Judah). The Assyrians invaded and camped around Jerusalem. They had a massive army and it seemed as though the city was defeated.
  • ❸
    The Surrender Letter — The People Were Faint The Assyrian commander sends a letter to King Hezekiah demanding surrender or annihilation. The people were faint with fear, waiting to be crushed.
  • ❹
    Hezekiah Spreads the Letter Before the Lord King Hezekiah took the commander's letter and spread it before the Lord and prayed: "Save us… that all the kingdoms of the earth may know that You are the Lord God, You alone" (2 Kings 19:19).
God's Supernatural Response — 185,000 Dead in the Night
2 Kings 19:28, 34–35

I will put My hook in your nose and My bridle in your lips, and I will turn you back by the way which you came… For I will defend this city, to save it for My own sake and for My servant David's sake. And it came to pass on a certain night that the angel of the Lord went out, and killed in the camp of the Assyrians one hundred and eighty-five thousand; and when people arose early in the morning, there were the corpses — all dead.

  • ✦
    185,000 Dead in a Single Night The Lord entered the Assyrian camp as the angel of the Lord and destroyed the entire army. There was no battle — the Lord Himself destroyed them all in one night.
  • ✦
    A Picture of the Tribulation This story is a picture of what will happen in the Tribulation with Jerusalem and the Antichrist. Israel will feel the pressure, fear for their lives, and finally turn to their Lord. When they bow and cry out for rescue, He will rescue them — just as He Himself destroyed the Assyrian army.
  • ✦
    There Will Be No Real Battle In the Tribulation there will be battle preparation and troop movements, but it is Jesus Himself who fights and destroys them. Beginning at Bozrah, then with the army at Jerusalem.
XIII

Lo que Viene: Por qué Satanás Quiere Destruir a Todos los Judíos

The Open Questions for the Next Part
  • ✦
    Why Is It So Important to Destroy All the Jews? The Antichrist wants to kill every living Jew. We have not yet examined why. The reason has to do with Satan's plan to prevent the return of Christ and his own destruction.
  • ✦
    Why Is It So Important That Israel Survive? Likewise, we will study why it is so important to God that the Jewish people survive — and the unbreakable promise God has made to Israel.
  • ✦
    The Moment When Israel Calls on Jesus We will look closely at the moment when Israel calls on the name of the Lord — the turning point that triggers the physical return of Christ. We will also see the Lord's return beginning at Bozrah and then arriving at Jerusalem.
  • ✦
    The Mount of Olives — Second Place Visited The Mount of Olives is the second place Christ will visit in His Second Coming. This scene will be addressed later along with the full description of Revelation 19:11 and beyond.
✦

Part 08 brings us to the threshold of the most climactic moment in all of redemptive history: the Second Coming of Christ. In heaven, the Hallelujah! resounds three times because the Church is already present as the Bride, the marriage supper of the Lamb is celebrated, and God's perfectly orchestrated plan unfolds on earth — the Antichrist, cornered and out of options, advances toward Bozrah and Jerusalem; the prophets Isaiah, Jeremiah, Joel, Zechariah, Micah, and Daniel confirm each step in detail. The siege of the Assyrians under Hezekiah reveals the pattern: Israel humbles itself, calls on its Lord, and He Himself — with no real battle — supernaturally destroys their enemies. The stage is set for the King of kings to appear.
