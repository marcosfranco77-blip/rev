I

Context: Timeline of the Kingdom

    Four Areas of the Kingdom Studied Order of Creation / geography / government — daily life and death — temple, worship and law — the Final War (current topic).
  • ✦
    The Millennium (Revelation 20:6) Jesus rules with the saints for exactly one thousand years. This period was the focus of the previous study.
  • ✦

Calculation of the Total Kingdom Period

Why Does This Distinction Matter?
Reason 1 — Purpose of the Millennium

Separating the 1,000-year period from the War of Gog and Magog allows us to clearly understand the specific purpose of the Kingdom: to demonstrate Christ’s perfect government over humanity without interference from the enemy.

Reason 2 — Identification of the War

The chronological detail confirms that the war in Revelation 20:7 and the war in Ezekiel 38–39 are the same event: the last war in human history.

II

The Text of Revelation 20 — The Final War

Key Text — Revelation 20:7–9
Revelation 20:7–9 “Now when the thousand years have expired, Satan will be released from his prison and will go out to deceive the nations which are in the four corners of the earth, Gog and Magog, to gather them together to battle, whose number is as the sand of the sea. They went up on the breadth of the earth and surrounded the camp of the saints and the beloved city. And fire came down from God out of heaven and devoured them.”
  • ❶
    Clearly Post-Millennial Start Verse 7 explicitly states “when the thousand years have expired.” The Millennium clock has already ended before the war begins.
  • ❷
    Innumerable Army The invading army is “as the sand of the sea” — multitudes from all Gentile nations, coming from every direction toward the plain surrounding Jerusalem.
  • ❸
    The Target: Jerusalem and the Temple They surround “the camp of the saints and the beloved city” — Jerusalem. The wide plain formed before the Millennium (Zech. 14:10) surrounds the only mountain: Mount Zion with the Temple.
  • ❹
    Instant Supernatural Destruction God sends fire from heaven and devours them. The battle ends before it really begins. Israel does not participate in the combat.
The Plain of Zechariah 14
Zechariah 14:9–10 “And the LORD shall be King over all the earth... All the land shall be turned into a plain from Geba to Rimmon south of Jerusalem...”

This topography confirms the setting: a wide plain formed prior to the Millennium, in whose center rises the Temple Mount — the exact place where the army surrounds Israel.

Three Questions Answered by Ezekiel
  • ①
    Why Was Satan Bound? To make possible Christ’s perfect government during the Millennium without interference or deception.
  • ②
    Why Is He Later Released? To be definitively defeated according to the eternal plan, putting an end to all enemy authority (1 Cor. 15:24–28).
  • ③
    Who Are Gog and Magog? Ezekiel 38–39 provides the complete details of their identity, motivations, and the development of the final battle.
III

The Role of Satan: Why He Was Bound and Released

The Two Ways Satan Provokes Sin
  • ❶
    The Great Deceiver Satan is the author of every lie. Jesus is the Truth; every lie in the world has its origin in Satan. John 8 says he is the “father of lies” from the Garden of Eden.
  • ❷
    The Tempter of the Flesh He tempts the carnal nature inherited from Adam to act against the Word of God. Paul in Romans 7 describes this internal conflict. Even so, God always provides a way of escape (1 Cor. 10:13).
Revelation 12:9 “So the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world; he was cast to the earth, and his angels were cast out with him.”
Postmodernism as an Example of Deception
  • ✦
    Cancellation of Truth Postmodernism promotes “your truth” instead of absolute “the truth” — a tool of satanic deception on a cultural and national scale.
  • ✦
    Consequences of a World Without Truth Narcissism — relative ethics — tribal identity — disdain for effort and moral values — substitution of thinking (I think → I feel).
  • ✦
    The Believer’s Response Do not withdraw. “You are the salt of the earth” (Matt. 5:13). “I am the light of the world” (Matt. 5:14). The believer must remain present as a witness.
Why Was Satan Bound During the Millennium?
Romans 7:18–19 “For I know that in me (that is, in my flesh) nothing good dwells; for to will is present with me, but how to perform what is good I do not find. For the good that I will to do, I do not do; but the evil I will not to do, that I practice.”
  • ✦
    Sinful nature remains in the Millennium Natural (unglorified) people born during the Millennium possess a sinful nature. If Satan were loose, he would deceive and tempt these people, undermining Jesus’ perfect government.
  • ✦
    The rod of iron is sufficient without external catalyst Christ judges sin instantly and perfectly. Without Satan’s deception as a catalyst, the peace of the Millennium remains intact for a thousand years.
  • ✦
    The demons were probably also bound Although Scripture does not state it explicitly, it would not make sense for the king of demons to be imprisoned while his troops roamed freely. The Scriptures confirm that peace will not be disturbed.
Why Is Satan Released? — The Answer from 1 Corinthians 15
1 Corinthians 15:24–25 “Then comes the end, when He delivers the kingdom to God the Father, when He puts an end to all rule and all authority and power. For He must reign till He has put all enemies under His feet.”
1 Corinthians 15:28 “Now when all things are made subject to Him, then the Son Himself will also be subject to Him who put all things under Him, that God may be all in all.”
  • ❶
    The last enemy to defeat: Death Death is personified in Satan, because he introduced sin and death into existence. Defeating him is defeating death itself.
  • ❷
    Without a final enemy, all authority ends Once Satan is defeated, there will be nothing left to rule. A 100% sinless world needs no government. Christ will then deliver all authority to the Father.
  • ❸
    Why wasn’t he destroyed before the Millennium? If Satan had been cast into the lake of fire along with the Antichrist and the False Prophet, there would have been no Millennial Kingdom — because in abolishing the last enemy, Christ would have immediately returned all authority to the Father.
IV

The True Purpose of the 1,000-Year Kingdom

6,000 Years of Human Failure
Jeremiah 17:5–6, 9 “Cursed is the man who trusts in man and makes flesh his strength... The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked; who can know it?”
  • ✦
    6,000 years of human government The accumulated testimony is: death, war, misery, destruction. Humanity has proven it cannot govern itself since the day of the Fall.
  • ✦
    The number 6 = the number of fallen man It is no coincidence: in the Bible the number 6 represents sinful and fallen man. Six thousand years of human government demonstrate the need for a divine King.
1,000 Years: The Definitive Contrast
  • ✦
    The number 7 = Perfection and completeness 6,000 years + 1,000 years = 7,000 years. Seven is the biblical number of perfection and completeness. The complete history of the earth culminates in a perfect number.
  • ✦
    The purpose of the Kingdom To be the cornerstone of the earth’s existence: to demonstrate that only through the Messiah come righteousness and justice, even in a world with a sinful nature.
  • ✦
    Promise of Psalm 8 Until there is no opposition at all, this world will continue. The moment the last enemy is put under Christ’s authority, this age will end and all authority will be abolished.
V

Ezekiel 38–39: Framework and Structure

Structure of Ezekiel 33–48 (The 16 Kingdom Chapters)
  • ✦
    General purpose The last 16 chapters of Ezekiel (33–48) are entirely dedicated to Kingdom prophecies. They are the fulfillment of the Abrahamic Covenant given to Israel.
  • ✦
    Thematic rather than chronological organization The chapters are not arranged chronologically — they are organized according to the four promises God made to Abraham. Each pair of chapters corresponds to one promise.
  • ✦
    Ezekiel 38–39 in the whole They narrate the war that guarantees the security of the land and demonstrates God’s glory to the nations — two of the four Abrahamic promises.
The 4 Promises of the Abrahamic / Davidic Covenant
  • ❶
    Inheritance of land A land in Canaan with great prosperity — milk and honey.
  • ❷
    Long line of secure descendants Even though Abraham had no son at the time, his descendants would live securely in this land.
  • ❸
    God’s presence God would dwell among His people in glory.
  • ❹
    An eternal King (Davidic Covenant) A king to rule the earth with perfect justice — the Messiah, the Lord Jesus Christ.
When Does the War of Ezekiel 38–39 Occur? — Common Error vs. Correct Position
❌ Incorrect Position — Before the Tribulation

Many teachers place the War of Gog and Magog before the Tribulation, in our current time: Russia and Iran invading Israel for oil or natural gas. They argue that God intervenes supernaturally in our days to defend Israel.

✅ Correct Position — Post-Millennial

A careful study of Ezekiel 38–39 compared with Revelation 20 reveals that all the details point to the end of the Millennium: Israel fully regathered, the formerly desolate land now prosperous, without walls, without military technology, weapons of wood.

  • ✦
    The Gog–Gog connection is definitive There are only two mentions in the entire Bible of “Gog and Magog” in relation to a war against the people of God: Ezekiel 38 and Revelation 20. This connection alone requires that they be the same event.
  • ✦
    John did not explain Gog because it was already known By mentioning Gog in Revelation 20 without further explanation, John expected his readers to know Ezekiel and recognize the reference.
VI

Identity of Gog and Magog (Ezekiel 38:1–8)

Foundational Text — Ezekiel 38:1–8
Ezekiel 38:1–6 “Son of man, set your face against Gog, of the land of Magog, the prince of Rosh, Meshech, and Tubal, and prophesy against him... Persia, Ethiopia, and Libya are with them, all of them with shield and helmet; Gomer and all its troops; the house of Togarmah from the far north and all its troops...”
Who Is Gog?
  • ✦
    A title, not a personal name Gog is a title similar to “Pharaoh” or “Caesar.” It indicates the supreme leader, not his personal name. It changes from person to person in each era but designates the same function.
  • ✦
    Magog: the region of origin From the Table of Nations in Genesis 10, Magog corresponds approximately to Eastern Europe, including present-day Turkey. Noah’s grandsons (Magog, Meshech, Tubal) settled in that region north of Israel.
Key Details of When It Occurs (Ezekiel 38:8)
  • ✦
    “In the latter years” It occurs after God has called Israel back to their land to live in peace — at the end of the Time of the Gentiles, which concludes with the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    “All Israel” regathered At the end of verse 8 it says “all.” Not just some Jews — all the Jews inhabit that land that was previously a desolation.
  • ✦
    Defenseless land — without walls Israel will have laid aside the sword. Without military armament, without walls, without bars or gates. They live in total confidence — only possible under Christ’s government.
VII

Details of the Invasion (Ezekiel 38:9–16)

Text — Ezekiel 38:9–16
Ezekiel 38:9–13 “You will ascend, coming like a storm, covering the land like a cloud, you and all your troops and many peoples with you... You will say, ‘I will go up against a land of unwalled villages; I will go to a peaceful people, who dwell safely, all of them dwelling without walls, and having neither bars nor gates’ — to take plunder and to take booty...”
Motivations of Gog: Opportunity and Greed
  • ❶
    An evil thought An evil thought enters Gog’s mind — Revelation 20 reveals that the true instigator is Satan, although Ezekiel does not mention him.
  • ❷
    The opportunity: defenseless land Gog sees that the land is in total rest, without walls or gates — like a house without a front door. A perceived opportunity for an easy attack.
  • ❸
    The greed: Israel’s abundance Israel in the Kingdom will be blessed above all nations. Gog sees the livestock, possessions, and accumulated goods and desires to take them for himself.
Satan’s Role as the Real Instigator
  • ✦
    Satan deceives Gog Satan convinces Gog that he can win the battle and that God was wrong in blessing Israel so much. He exploits Gog’s lust and greed to provoke the attack.
  • ✦
    The neighbors: surprised and incredulous In verse 13 Israel’s neighbors are bewildered by the invaders. After 1,000 years without wars or conflicts, no one has even seen an army form. They are literally stunned.
  • ✦
    God Himself summons Gog Ezekiel 38:4 says it is God who brings them to battle — He uses Satan as an instrument to provoke the planned end of rebellion and demonstrate His faithfulness to Israel.
The Art of War Was Lost in the Millennium
Isaiah 2:4 “He shall judge between the nations, and rebuke many people; they shall beat their swords into plowshares, and their spears into pruning hooks; nation shall not lift up sword against nation, neither shall they learn war anymore.”
  • ✦
    Rudimentary war equipment Gog’s army uses exclusively wood: sticks, spears, shields, helmets, bows and arrows — all wooden, no metal. War technology has completely disappeared during the Millennium.
  • ✦
    Horses as means of transport The Hebrew word “horse” literally means horse — no symbolic reference. This only makes sense in the post-industrial era of the Kingdom where war technology has disappeared.
  • ✦
    Generations that never knew war No one even knows how to make war. They were born and raised during 1,000 years of perfect peace. War knowledge was lost generationally.
  • ✦
    Why do they think they can win? They were deceived by Satan. Israel is completely defenseless, without walls or army, so these simple wooden weapons are a real threat to the peace. The deception is total.
VIII

The Purpose of the Invasion: God Demonstrates His Covenant Faithfulness

How Would God Demonstrate That He Protects Israel Without a Threat?
The Cancer Analogy

It is as if God promised to cure someone of cancer, but until they contract it, that person cannot know that God fulfilled His promise. Without the threat of Gog, there is no way for Israel to experience and confirm God’s faithfulness in protecting them.

  • ✦
    Double purpose of Gog’s invasion First: it provokes the definitive end of Satan, fulfilling Paul’s eternal plan in 1 Corinthians 15. Second: it gives the Lord the opportunity to show Himself faithful to the covenant of peace promised to Israel in the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    God uses Satan for His own purposes Although Satan tries to destroy Israel’s peace, the Lord uses him as an instrument to demonstrate His glory and faithfulness — and to put an end to the last enemy as planned from before creation.
IX

The Supernatural Destruction and Its Aftermath (Ezekiel 39)

Text — Ezekiel 39:1–6: God Destroys Gog
Ezekiel 39:1–6 “And you, son of man, prophesy against Gog... I will turn you around and lead you on, and bring you up from the far north, and bring you against the mountains of Israel... You shall fall on the mountains of Israel, you and all your troops and the peoples who are with you... And I will send fire on Magog and on those who live in security in the coastlands.”
God’s Supernatural Act
  • ✦
    The Lord removes the weapons from their hands In verse 3, the Lord takes the bow and arrows from Gog’s hands — instantly disabling the entire invading army before it can attack.
  • ✦
    Israel does not participate at all Israel remains safe in their unwalled villages. The Lord fights for them in the plain. The people remain safe while God supernaturally destroys the army.
  • ✦
    Fire from heaven — perfect parallel The fire from heaven in Ezekiel 39:6 is the same fire from heaven in Revelation 20:9 — definitive confirmation that both passages describe the same event.
Aftermath: Burning of Weapons (Ezekiel 39:9–10)
Ezekiel 39:9–10 “Then those who dwell in the cities of Israel will go out and make fires of the weapons and burn them... They will make fires with them for seven years. They will not take wood from the field nor cut down any from the forests, because they will make fires with the weapons...”
  • ✦
    7 years of fuel All the weapons are wooden. Israel collects them and uses them as fuel for seven years — so vast was the army that no one will need to gather wood from the field during that period.
  • ✦
    Wood: the main fuel of the Kingdom This detail confirms that the Kingdom era uses wood as the primary energy source — modern technology completely absent.
The Burial of the Dead (Ezekiel 39:11–16)
Ezekiel 39:11–14 “It will come to pass in that day that I will give Gog a burial place there in Israel... And the house of Israel will be burying them for seven months, in order to cleanse the land... At the end of seven months they will make a search.”
  • ❶
    7 months of burial The bodies are so numerous that the burial process requires seven months. All the people of Israel will participate in this task to purify the land.
  • ❷
    Valley of Hamon Gog The burial place — probably the Jordan River valley, east of the Mediterranean — will be called “Hamon Gog”: the multitude of Gog.
  • ❸
    Meticulous search for bones Teams of people scour the entire land looking for unburied bones. When they find one, they place a marker so a Jew does not stumble over it and become ritually unclean. Then the buriers entomb them in the valley.
  • ❹
    Israel makes a name for itself among the nations In verse 13, the Lord says that Israel will become famous among all the nations for the way they respectfully bury even their enemies.
  • ❺
    Confirmation of the post-millennial interval 7 months of burial + 7 years of burning weapons demonstrate that there is an interval of at least 7 years after the 1,000-year clock, before the New Heavens and New Earth.
X

The Final Sacrifice: Inversion of the Created Order (Ezekiel 39:17–21)

Text — The Great Inverted Sacrifice
Ezekiel 39:17–20 “Speak to every sort of bird and to every beast of the field: ‘Assemble yourselves and come; gather together from all sides to My sacrificial meal... You shall eat the flesh of the mighty, drink the blood of the princes of the earth... You shall be filled at My table with horses and riders, with mighty men and with all the men of war,’ says the Lord GOD.”
The Inversion of Sacrifices
  • ✦
    In the Bible: humans sacrifice animals Throughout all history, the Lord directed people to sacrifice animals as a reminder of sin and the need for a blood covering (pointing to Christ).
  • ✦
    In the final act: humanity is the sacrifice At this culminating moment, the Lord completely inverts the order: He makes rebellious humanity a sacrifice for the animals. The animals receive the benefit; man pays the price; God is the One making the sacrifice.
  • ✦
    Symmetry from the Fall The first act at the beginning of the Fall was the sacrifice of an animal (in the Garden, to cover Adam and Eve). The last and final act of this age, God reverses it: the animal is the beneficiary and rebellious man is the sacrifice.
The Final Theological Message
Ezekiel 39:21 “I will set My glory among the nations; all the nations shall see My judgment which I have executed, and My hand which I have laid on them.”
  • ✦
    Sacrifices have come to an end The Lord Himself carries out the final and definitive sacrifice. The offering is those who oppose Him — the unbelievers. The need for any kind of sacrifices ceases forever.
  • ✦
    Unbelief disappears from Creation With the destruction of Gog’s army, all militant unbelief in Creation has been eliminated — although the final judgment of the souls of all unbelievers throughout history is yet to come.
  • ✦
    What follows: The Final Judgment The next study will cover the moment of final judgment for the souls of all unbelievers who have ever lived. After that moment, the earth and its works will be burned and the new world will appear.

✦

The War of Gog and Magog is not an immediate future or pre-tribulational event: it is the culminating act of human history, occurring at the end of the thousand years of the Millennial Kingdom. Satan is released not by chance, but by divine design — to be definitively defeated and to allow Christ to deliver all authority to the Father. Through the failure of Gog’s army, God fulfills His promise to protect Israel, inverts the sacrificial order of the fallen world, and closes this era of Creation forever. The final judgment of unbelievers throughout history and the New Heavens and New Earth await in the final chapters of Revelation.
