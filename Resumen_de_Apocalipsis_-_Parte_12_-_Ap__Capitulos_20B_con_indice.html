XII

The Beginning of the Kingdom: What Revelation Says (and Does Not Say)

The State at the Beginning of the Kingdom
  • ✦
    Everything ready in 75 daysThe earth restored to its beauty. The temple cleansed and renewed. The wickedness of the world set aside for a time. The citizens of the Kingdom present and ready to receive their inheritance.
  • ✦
    The citizens presentThe resurrected saints of the O.T., the Church and the Tribulation entering the Kingdom. The surviving Gentile believers who lived through the Tribulation. All living unbelievers — only Gentiles at this point — have already been sent to Hades.
The Silence of Revelation 20
Revelation 20:6–7

"Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection… they shall reign with Him a thousand years. Now when the thousand years have expired, Satan will be released from his prison."

  • ✦
    Between v.6 and v.7The entire period of the Kingdom — 1,000 years of history — takes place between these two verses. Revelation does not describe what happens during that time; it only gives its duration.
  • ✦
    Why the silenceThe rest of the Bible is literally full of those details. The Kingdom is described in the Torah, the minor and major prophets, the Psalms, Jesus’ parables, and the epistles. That is why Revelation omits it.
XIII

The Progressive Concept of the Kingdom: Four Stages

From “Hallmark” Theology to Biblical Reality
  • ✦
    The problem: simplistic understandingMany Christians operate with a superficial understanding of their eternal future — the “Hallmark theology”: clouds, harps, pearly gates. As a result, our understanding lacks substance or meaning and many live their entire lives with a wrong concept of the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    The biblical realityThe Bible speaks extensively about the Kingdom using a variety of terms, descriptions, and images, from Genesis to Malachi. It is one of the most frequent themes in the O.T. after the theme of the Messiah. In the N.T. there are 160 mentions of the kingdom, 125 in the Gospels.
The Four Stages of the Kingdom Concept
Stage 1 — The Promise (Genesis → Malachi)
The theme of the Kingdom begins in Genesis as a promise to correct Adam’s sin. Articulated in the covenants with Abraham and David: one nation, one land, one perfect king, and infinite peace. Entire generations died without having received it, seeing it from afar (Hebrews 11:13).
Stage 2 — The Proposal (Jesus’ Ministry)
The Father sent Jesus to fulfill the promise. Jesus made a proposal to Israel: "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand" (Matthew 4:17). Israel rejected their King, and as a result the Kingdom was taken from that generation. The proposal was withdrawn temporarily.
Stage 3 — The Program (Church Age)
The withdrawn proposal gave way to a program of recruiting Gentiles: the Great Commission (Matthew 28:19–20). Whoever believes receives their “passport” and becomes part of a spiritual kingdom not yet physically established. This program lasts until the Rapture. We are currently living in the final part of the Program.
Stage 4 — A Place on Earth (Second Coming)
At the Second Coming, the Kingdom is established as promised: a literal place on this same earth, renewed, with Israel as a nation and people from all nations who participated by faith — also fulfilling the promise that all nations would be blessed through Abraham.
The True Kingdom: What Awaits Us
  • ✦
    A real homeLands and possessions that can never be taken away. Absence of sickness and sorrow. Nothing will disturb the peace or joy.
  • ✦
    Meaningful workWork that has meaning, that is not difficult but enjoyable. Full relationships, natural beauty on the earth.
  • ✦
    Intimate communion with the LordTo know, worship, and serve the Lord in intimate ways we cannot even imagine today. The world of the Kingdom will be much more enjoyable than the current world.
  • ✦
    Present preparationAll the transformation of this life is nothing more than preparation for our assignments and for our life in the coming Kingdom. As Hebrews 11 says, we are citizens of the Kingdom and strangers in this earth.

XIV

Changes in Creation: The Consequences of Adam’s Sin

Before the Fall: The Original Eden
  • ✦
    The earth produced by itselfWithout man’s labor, the earth produced everything that was good and healthy. It was only necessary to walk out the door to find all the food one wanted. No useless weeds or harmful plants sprang up.
  • ✦
    Eternal bodiesThe physical body was created to live forever. Without sin, as Paul says in Romans, there would be no death, neither for us nor for any other creature.
  • ✦
    Animals without fearAnimals did not fear men nor each other. The predator-prey relationship did not exist. There was man’s dominion over the animal kingdom in peace.
  • ✦
    Vegetarian dietBy God’s design, before the flood human beings ate only plants. Animals possibly did as well.
The Changes after the Fall (Genesis 3)
Genesis 3:17–19

"Cursed is the ground for your sake; in toil you shall eat of it all the days of your life. Both thorns and thistles it shall bring forth for you, and you shall eat the herb of the field. In the sweat of your face you shall eat bread till you return to the ground."

  • ❶
    The curse on the earthGod cursed the earth — not Adam — with an irreversible pronouncement of judgment. The curse comes from God and no human effort can reverse it. The earth will one day be destroyed and replaced.
  • ❷
    Thorns, thistles, and sweatThe earth would naturally produce weeds. Only with the sweat of his brow could man produce the food he needed.
  • ❸
    The death of the bodyAdam’s spirit died when he ate the fruit. God put the physical body on the same level: it too would die. "Adam" in Hebrew means “earth” — created from the cursed earth, to it he would return.
Additional Changes after the Flood (Genesis 9)
Genesis 9:1–3

"Be fruitful and multiply, and fill the earth. The fear of you and the dread of you shall be on every beast of the earth… Every moving thing that lives shall be food for you. I have given you all things, even as the green herbs."

Post-flood changes
  • ✦
    Meat as foodThe Lord gave permission to eat animal meat. Necessary because vegetation was scarce weeks after the flood. Without meat, animals and humanity would have died of starvation.
  • ✦
    Climate changeIt is believed there was a post-flood climate change that made cultivation difficult, reinforcing the need for meat as a source of sustenance.
The predator-prey relationship
  • ✦
    Fear in animalsTo protect animals from rapid extinction, the Lord placed the fear of man in the animals. The predator-prey relationship was established: animals adversarial to each other and to man.
  • ✦
    A change from the beginningAll this represents a departure from the original intention of Creation, produced by man’s sin. In the future, the Lord will correct these consequences in stages.
XV

Animals in the Kingdom: Restoration of the Original Harmony

Isaiah 11:6–9

"The wolf also shall dwell with the lamb, the leopard shall lie down with the young goat, the calf and the young lion and the fatling together; and a little child shall lead them… The nursing child shall play by the cobra’s hole, and the weaned child shall put his hand in the viper’s den. They shall not hurt nor destroy in all My holy mountain."

The Restoration of the Animal Kingdom
  • ✦
    Predators and prey in peaceWolves, leopards, and lions will live in peace alongside lambs, goats, and calves. The Lord eliminates the predator-prey relationship: there will no longer be hunters or hunted.
  • ✦
    No threat to manLarge and dangerous animals will not represent a threat to people. Without reason to fear men, they will instinctively cease attacking with their lethal defense mechanisms.
  • ✦
    Restored dominionA child will be able to tame or impose obedience on any animal. Even a cobra will not represent risk to a small child. We will return to having dominion over the animal kingdom as in the beginning.
  • ✦
    A direct reversalThis is a direct reversal of the pronouncement given to Noah and a step back to the nature of the world in the beginning: all animals will obey the will of man.
Will There Be Pets in Heaven?
  • ✦
    Heaven is not a single placeHeaven is where Jesus is. Jesus moves, and we move too. It begins in the throne room of God after death or the Rapture — a temporary home, not permanent.
  • ✦
    Animals and the soulAnimals do not have a soul that needs to be saved, therefore they will not be found in the temporary heavenly home. But Isaiah confirms there will be animals in the Kingdom when Jesus brings us back to the earth.
  • ✦
    We are made for the earthJust like the animals, human beings are made to live on a physical earth. Our permanent home is the earthly Kingdom, not the heavenly throne room.
The Curse of the Serpent: What Does Not Change in the Kingdom
Genesis 3:14 / Isaiah 65:25

"So the LORD God said to the serpent: On your belly you shall go, and you shall eat dust all the days of your life." / "The wolf and the lamb shall feed together… and dust shall be the serpent’s food."

  • ✦
    The literal serpent and SatanThe serpent was a literal animal in which Satan dwelt to deceive Eve. As an involuntary participant, it was cursed to lose its legs and crawl. Before the fall, the serpent had legs and its belly did not touch the ground.
  • ✦
    A monument for humanityThe curse was not a punishment against the animal but a monument for men. Every time we see a serpent crawl it should remind us of the moment of the fall and our true adversary.
  • ✦
    Not reversed in the KingdomIsaiah 65:25 says that in the Kingdom the serpent continues to eat dust, although it will not harm. Its cursed form does not change because the conditions that produced it have not been reversed: Satan still exists, although he is bound.
XVI

The Earth Will Produce: Reversal of the Agricultural Curse

Ezekiel 34: Covenant of Peace with Israel
Ezekiel 34:25–27

"I will make a covenant of peace with them, and cause wild beasts to cease from the land… The trees of the field shall yield their fruit, and the earth shall yield her increase. They shall be safe in their land, and they shall know that I am the LORD."

  • ✦
    Without tilling the soilThe tree of the field shall yield its fruit and the earth shall produce increase without the need to cultivate the soil. This is a direct reversal of the curse on the earth that made life difficult.
  • ✦
    Sleeping safely in the forestSleeping safely in the forest without fear of enemies, men, or beasts. Showers of blessing in their season. A way of life that resembles that of Adam in the garden of Eden.
Ezekiel 36: The Earth Like Eden
Ezekiel 36:29–30, 34–35

"I will call for the grain and multiply it, and bring no famine upon you. And I will multiply the fruit of your trees and the increase of your fields… This land that was desolate has become like the garden of Eden."

  • ✦
    The Lord does the workAgriculture becomes easy when the Lord does all the work. The point is not that we will become a purely agricultural society — it is a grand picture of grace: the Lord does the work and we receive the blessing.
  • ✦
    Never hunger on the earthThe Lord will provide fruit and grain at the call of His voice. How difficult will it be to cultivate a land that produces food at the voice of God? There will never again be hunger.
Isaiah 30: Rain, Sun, and Brighter Moon
Isaiah 30:23–26

"Then He will give the rain for your seed with which you sow the ground, and bread of the increase of the earth… On every high mountain and on every high hill there shall be rivers and streams of waters… The light of the moon will be as the light of the sun, and the light of the sun will be sevenfold."

  • ✦
    Abundant pastures and livestockPerfect rains, wide and rich pastures for livestock with abundant food. A geography of rivers even on the tops of mountains.
  • ✦
    The brightness of sun and moonThe sun seven times brighter and the moon as bright as today’s sun. It is not clear if it is literal or literary. If literal, it may be brighter without being hotter — with the purpose of increasing the production of plants, fruits, and vegetables. God will provide adaptation.
XVII

Borders and Geography of the Kingdom

The Extent of Israel’s Territory
Period Extent of Israel’s territory
Current Israel Small portion facing the Mediterranean Sea. Lebanon to the north, Egypt to the south, Jordan and Syria to the east.
Under Solomon (historical zenith) As far north as Syria, all of Lebanon, land east of the Jordan, as far as Egypt and southern Jordan. Superpower of its time. Israel has never had as much since then.
Abrahamic Covenant (Gen. 15:18) From the river of Egypt to the great river Euphrates. Territory of ten nations (Kenites, Kenezzites, Kadmonites, Hittites, Perizzites, Rephaim, Amorites, Canaanites, Girgashites, and Jebusites). Much more than under Solomon.
In the Kingdom (Ezekiel 47:15–20) Specific borders described by Ezekiel: north to Hethlon and Hamath, east including Hauran and Damascus, south from Tamar to Kadesh, west the Great Sea. Larger than anything seen before, encompassing all nations historically hostile to Israel.
Israel and Its Historical Enemies
Isaiah 14:1–2 / Isaiah 54:3

"The peoples shall take them and bring them to their place, and the house of Israel shall possess them as servants… They shall take captive those who were their captors, and rule over those who oppressed them."

  • ✦
    Complete reversalIsrael will consume Moab, Ammon, Edom, Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, and others. The borders of the Kingdom fulfill God’s promises that Israel would rule over its historical captors.
  • ✦
    Israel’s path until nowCreated as a nation by God. Received promises and violated them. Disciplined, scattered, suffered great losses. Endured the Tribulation. In the end came to faith and received their Messiah. Now they receive their blessings.
Geographic Exceptions: Edom and Egypt
  • ✦
    Edom — completely emptyIt will exist in the Kingdom but will remain completely empty as a monument to its sin against Israel. It will be the location of the entrance to the pit where Satan is held. Smoke will come out of the pit; no human or animal will be able to set foot there.
  • ✦
    Egypt — empty for 40 yearsIt will exist, but the Egyptians of the Kingdom will not be able to enter their land during the first 40 years. In memory of how Egypt caused Israel to stumble with idols, which led Israel to wander 40 years in the wilderness. After 40 years they will be allowed to inhabit their land again.
Geographic Changes in Jerusalem and Its Surroundings
Micah 4:1 / Zechariah 14:8–10

"Now it shall come to pass in the latter days that the mountain of the LORD’s house shall be established on the top of the mountains, and shall be exalted above the hills." / "And in that day it shall be that living waters shall flow from Jerusalem, half of them toward the eastern sea and half of them toward the western sea… All the land shall be turned as a plain."

  • ✦
    The highest mountain in the worldThe mountain on which Jerusalem sits (Mount Zion) will rise to become the highest mountain in the world. On its summit will be the temple, the house of the Lord where Christ dwells during the 1,000 years.
  • ✦
    Two rivers from JerusalemNew rivers will flow from the top of this mountain: one to the east ending in the Dead Sea, and one to the west ending in the Mediterranean Sea. The seasons will continue as today.
  • ✦
    A great plain aroundThe land around Jerusalem, today mountainous, will be transformed into a great flat plain extending for miles. Result: the mountain of the Lord will be visible from anywhere one walks.
  • ✦
    Jerusalem — capital of the EarthOut of Zion shall go forth the law, and the word of the LORD from Jerusalem. All nations will go to Jerusalem to learn from the God of Israel. The capital of the Earth will be Jerusalem, where the Lord dwells to rule the nations (Micah 4:2).
The Dead Sea Comes to Life (Ezekiel 47:8–11)
Ezekiel 47:8–10

"These waters flow toward the eastern region, go down into the Arabah, and go toward the sea. When they reach the sea, the waters are healed. And it shall be that every living thing that moves, wherever the rivers go, will live; there will be a very great multitude of fish."

  • ✦
    Why the Dead Sea is deadIts salt level is so high that no fish can survive. Boats flip over due to the high density of the water. It only produces minerals extracted from salt deposits.
  • ✦
    Miraculous transformationThe river flowing from Mount Zion will transform the Dead Sea into fresh water, possibly in a miraculous and instantaneous way. The Dead Sea comes to life — a beautiful millennial picture of God’s grace that gives life to the dead.
  • ✦
    Abundant and specialized fishingThere will be so much variety of fish that fishermen will specialize in their capture, from En Gedi to Eneglaim. The low marshy areas will remain as testimony of the change from salty to fresh.
XVIII

Sin in the Millennium and Christ’s Perfect Government

Sin Exists But Does Not Develop
  • ✦
    Thinking differently for the KingdomToday we have imperfect laws, imperfect judges, and little justice applied correctly. We must think differently for the Kingdom: a perfect, omniscient, and omnipotent judge. No one gets away with anything.
  • ✦
    Sin without external consequencesThere will be sin in the hearts of individuals with natural bodies, but it will not be able to manifest to produce any harm. The slightest intention or thought of evil will be known immediately by the Lord, who will react to prevent it.
  • ✦
    Rod of ironGod promised that the world will live with a peace and joy that cannot be disturbed, because the sin around does not impact people’s lives. That is what it means that the Lord rules with a rod of iron (Psalm 2:6–9).
Omniscient and Instant Government
Isaiah 65:24

"It shall come to pass that before they call, I will answer; and while they are still speaking, I will hear."

  • ✦
    Frictionless communicationAnything happening anywhere on the planet, the Lord will know. We will have instant communication with Him to react to any eventuality or need.
  • ✦
    No war — Micah 4:3Instruments of war will be eliminated and the art of war will be forgotten. "Nation shall not lift up sword against nation, neither shall they learn war anymore." Swords will be beaten into plowshares and spears into pruning hooks.
Psalm 2:6–9

"Yet I have set My King on My holy hill of Zion… Ask of Me, and I will give You the nations for Your inheritance, and the ends of the earth for Your possession. You shall break them with a rod of iron; You shall dash them to pieces like a potter’s vessel. Now therefore, be wise, O kings; be instructed, you judges of the earth."

XIX

The Structure of the Kingdom Government

Hierarchy of the Kingdom Government
① Christ Jesus — King of Kings
Jesus presides over the entire government structure from the temple in Jerusalem, the highest mountain on earth. “The government will be upon His shoulder” (Isaiah 9:6–7). The Father remains separate in heaven until Revelation 21.
② David — Prince of Israel
David resurrects and returns to rule the land of Israel under Jesus’ authority. He is called prince because Jesus is King. (Jeremiah 30:8–9; Ezekiel 34:23–24)
③ The Twelve Apostles — over the twelve tribes
“You who have followed Me will also sit on twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel” (Matthew 19:28). All under David, who is the prince of Israel. Matthias replaces Judas.
④ Other O.T. Saints — additional rulers over Israel
Other great saints of the O.T. rewarded with positions of honor for their service will rule over different parts of Israel. The tribes will exist in their land and will have additional representation.
⑤ Church Saints — over the Gentile nations
The saints of the Church will rule the Gentile nations scattered throughout the world (Revelation 20:4). You and I fulfill that need for governmental representation.
A Perfect Bureaucracy
  • ✦
    Perfect because its officers are perfectIn this bureaucracy (in its good meaning), the rulers are not elected by the people but by the ruler. The government carries out its orders to perfection. If the government is perfect, its officers must be perfect — and they are, being made of glorified saints.
  • ✦
    Divided into Jewish and Gentile governmentThere is a Jewish government over Israel and a Gentile government over the Gentile nations. They are not equal: Israel will be the highest nation on earth and all other nations will serve Israel.
The Rewards of Government — Jesus’ Parables
2 Corinthians 5:9–10

"Therefore we make it our aim, whether present or absent, to be well pleasing to Him. For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, that each one may receive the things done in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad."

  • ✦
    Parable of the Minas (Luke 19)One mina equals 100 days of work. Jesus rewards faithfulness with the opportunity to rule over cities. Greater faithfulness, greater governmental responsibility.
  • ✦
    Parable of the Talents (Matthew 25:14)One talent equals 16.5 years of work. Being faithful in little, one enters the kingdom with the opportunity to be faithful in much: greater responsibility in the leadership of the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    A desirable rewardAlthough we already have a high position in the government of the Kingdom, Jesus presents it as a desirable reward. We should aspire to please Him now and also in the Kingdom.
Israel — The Supreme Nation of the Kingdom
Deuteronomy 28:1–2 / Isaiah 14:1–2

"Now it shall come to pass, if you diligently obey the voice of the LORD your God… that the LORD your God will set you high above all nations of the earth." / "The peoples shall take them and bring them to their place, and the house of Israel shall possess them as male and female servants in the land of the LORD."

  • ✦
    Reversal of historyThis is a complete reversal of Israel’s historical position since the Lord began to discipline the nation for its sin. Israel, which saw so many rule over it in the past, will now rule over the world.
  • ✦
    The Lord will set Israel aboveIn honor and position, because it is where Jesus dwells and because He chose them as His people. Serving the people of God, in a completely different world, will be an enjoyable privilege and joy in the promised environment of the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    Next questionThe existence of government implies there will be disobedience. And disobedience implies sin. The question that will be answered in the next class: how and why will sin exist in the Kingdom?
✦
The Millennial Kingdom is not a vague utopia or a diffuse spiritual concept: it is a real place on this same earth, renewed by the supernatural power of God, where sin exists but never prospers, nature obeys man, the earth produces without effort, and Jerusalem — on the highest mountain in the world — is the capital of all humanity. The concept of the Kingdom progresses from Promise to Proposal, to Program, and finally to this Place. A government of perfect glorified saints headed by Christ, with David as prince and the apostles over the tribes, guarantees that no sin will have room to grow. And we — the saints of the Church — will be part of that eternal government, ruling the Gentile nations with the wisdom Christ gives us.
