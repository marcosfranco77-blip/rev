I

The Beast from the Sea — The Antichrist Identified (Rev. 13:1–2)

The Beast and the Dragon
Revelation 13:1–2 I saw a beast rising up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns… Now the beast which I saw was like a leopard, his feet were like the feet of a bear, and his mouth like the mouth of a lion. The dragon gave him his power, his throne, and great authority.
  • ✦
    The Dragon = SatanAs we saw in Rev. 12:3, he pursues and kills all who are aligned with God. Now he does it at a new level — through the Antichrist — for he has been cast down to the earth.
  • ✦
    The Beast = the AntichristThis is the same beast from Rev. 11:7 that killed the Two Witnesses. He was also seen in Rev. 6 under another name: the rider of the four horses. He was not called "the beast" before because he had not yet reached the level of power he obtains at mid-trib.
  • ✦
    Rising from the seaUnder the rules of interpretation, the answer is found in Daniel 7: a beast rising from the sea is defined as a Gentile nation or a Gentile ruler rising to power.
Resemblance to the 4 Beasts of Daniel 7
Daniel 7:3–8 Four great beasts came up from the sea… The first was like a lion [= Babylon]. The second was like a bear [= Medo-Persia]. The third was like a leopard [= Greece]. A fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, with ten horns [= Rome and its successors].
  • ✦
    The embodiment of the Gentile AgeThe beast of Rev. 13 bears traits of all of Daniel's beasts: the lion, the bear, the leopard, and the fourth beast. He is the embodiment of the Gentile Age presented in Daniel's dream — all four beasts now real in a single person.
  • ✦
    The last and most powerfulJust as the age began with Nebuchadnezzar, it will end with the Beast. This man inherits and succeeds all previous kingdoms. He is the final world leader of this age.
  • ✦
    Crowns on the horns vs. headsSatan (Rev. 12:3) wears crowns on his heads; the Beast wears them on his horns. The arrangement is different — and so is the meaning. The transfer of power will be explained in Rev. 17.
The Antichrist — Description from Daniel 11:36–39
Daniel 11:36–39 "Then the king shall do according to his own will: he shall exalt and magnify himself above every god… He shall regard neither the God of his fathers nor the desire of women, nor regard any god; for he shall exalt himself above them all. But in their place he shall honor a god of fortresses."
  • ✦
    No regard for any godHe magnifies himself above every known god — including the God of his fathers. He does not regard the desire of women (the human longing for bonds). He only honors "the god of fortresses" — Satan.
  • ✦
    Divides the landHe acts against the strongest fortresses with a foreign god. He advances his glory and gives his followers rule over many, distributing the land as a reward.
  • ✦
    Isaiah's warningIs. 28:15: the Israelites who sign the covenant with the Antichrist do so convinced he will protect them. Is. 28:18: "Your covenant with death will be annulled." The covenant they made with Satan will be turned against them.
II

Death and Resurrection of the Antichrist — The Great Counterfeit

The Fatal Wound Healed (Rev. 13:3)
Revelation 13:3 And I saw one of his heads as if it had been mortally wounded, and his deadly wound was healed. And all the world marveled and followed the beast.
  • ✦
    An unsustainable woundThe Greek word used for the deadly wound is the same word used for Jesus in Rev. 5:6 — "as though slain." And "was healed" in Greek is sphazo: resurrected.
  • ✦
    Who killed him?It is not specified, but possibly the three kings who refused to submit (Dan. 7:8). They succeed in killing him, but he is resurrected and then eliminates them, leaving only seven kings.
  • ✦
    Who raises him?It is not God. Satan indwells the Antichrist, bringing him back to life and giving him his power, his throne, and great authority. (Rev. 13:2)
Satan Takes Full Control
2 Thessalonians 2:8–9 And then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord will consume with the breath of His mouth… The coming of the lawless one is according to the working of Satan, with all power, signs, and lying wonders.
  • ✦
    All power except God's"All power" — every power apart from the power of God. With the resurrection, Satan seizes the opportunity to bring every unbelieving human being under his control.
  • ✦
    A man with the power of SatanFrom this point on the Beast is a man in a physical sense, but with Satan's power within. He is more powerful than any other man in history.
  • ✦
    Worship of the dragon through the beastThe world worships the Beast and also the dragon. How do they see the dragon? They do not see him directly — they see the resurrected man. Just as we worship the Father through Jesus, the world will worship Satan through the Beast.
  • ✦
    Satan manifests physicallyConfined to the earth, he has taken up residence in the body of the Antichrist. This earth is his domain. He has manifested physically and leads the world to worship him.
The Antichrist in the Temple — The Abomination of Desolation
2 Thessalonians 2:3–4 …the man of sin is revealed, the son of perdition, who opposes and exalts himself above all that is called God or that is worshiped, so that he sits as God in the temple of God, showing himself that he is God.
  • ✦
    He declares himself the Messiah — not another godHe does not declare himself a Buddha, Mohammed, or another god. He sits in the seat of the God of the Jews, on the mercy seat of the Temple, declaring himself to be the Christ, the Messiah. Satan knows there is only one true God — and that is who he is counterfeiting.
  • ✦
    Jesus vs. Satan — the parallelJust as Jesus manifested as a man at the first coming and will return as King (the second Person of the Deity), Satan initially manifests through the Antichrist as a man, and at mid-Tribulation manifests as "god."
  • ✦
    He will bring an end to sacrificeDan. 9:27 — In the middle of the week (mid-trib) he will bring an end to sacrifice and offering. He will enter the Temple, expel the Jews, put an end to Judaism and all other religions. The Temple is desecrated and turned into a center of worship for the Antichrist.
  • ✦
    He is celebrated as SaviorThe world celebrates him at mid-trib as the risen Christ and the savior of the world — because he put an end to the "terror" of the judgments the world associated with the Two Witnesses.
III

Authority over the World for 42 Months (Rev. 13:5–10)

He Was Granted to Make War Against the Saints
Revelation 13:5–8 And he was given a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies, and he was given authority to continue for forty-two months… It was granted to him to make war with the saints and to overcome them. And authority was given him over every tribe, tongue, and nation. All who dwell on the earth will worship him, whose names have not been written in the Book of Life.
  • ✦
    42 months = 3½ yearsThe classic time reference: from mid-trib until the end of the Tribulation.
  • ✦
    BlasphemyThe greatest of blasphemies: attributing the works of God to himself by taking the place of God.
  • ✦
    War against the saintsAnyone who denies that he is God is hunted down to be killed. In Rev. 12 we saw that Satan went against the rest who were not protected in Petra, using the Antichrist and the world as his agents.
The Turn in Persecution
  • ✦
    First halfThe judgments fall on unbelievers. God sealed His own, preserved Israel, the Temple, and its operation.
  • ✦
    Second half — complete reversalPersecution comes upon the believers — now from Satan. The wicked are protected by Satan. The righteous are persecuted by Satan. Until the Bowl judgments arrive, unbelievers live relatively well.
  • ✦
    The Book of LifeThose who worship the Beast do not have their name in the Book. Those written in the Book since the foundation of the earth will not worship the Beast.
The Destiny of the Saints — Perseverance (Rev. 13:9–10)
Revelation 13:9–10 If anyone has an ear, let him hear. He who leads into captivity shall go into captivity; he who kills with the sword must be killed with the sword. Here is the patience and the faith of the saints.
  • ✦
    There will be no resistanceIf a saint attempts to fight against the Antichrist, he is destined to die in the fight. There will be no resistance to the Antichrist's power during the time God has designated for his rule. This is what the "Left Behind" films misrepresent.
  • ✦
    Absolute control permitted by GodGod has allowed him to have absolute control over the world for 3½ years. Nothing the saints can do will prevent his reign.
  • ✦
    The patience of the saintsIt means understanding and accepting what is coming. The believer who enters mid-trib must understand that he will likely be a martyr — and that this is the outcome designated by God. It is confirmed later: it is better to die than to live through what is coming.
  • ✦
    The end of evangelismFrom the moment someone worships the Beast, they are no longer savable. The Book of Life is sealed. This moment becomes a dividing point not only in the timeline but of all humanity.
IV

The Second Beast — The False Prophet (Rev. 13:11–14)

Description and Identity
Revelation 13:11–13 Then I saw another beast coming up out of the earth, and he had two horns like a lamb and spoke like a dragon. And he exercises all the authority of the first beast in his presence… He performs great signs, so that he even makes fire come down from heaven.
  • ✦
    Two horns like a lambThe lamb is used as an image of Christ — the prophet sent to be sacrificed. He appears to be like Christ, a prophet of God.
  • ✦
    Mouth of a dragonHe speaks for Satan. He is the spokesman of the Antichrist's god. This is why he is called the False Prophet.
  • ✦
    Only in the Antichrist's presenceHe exercises the Antichrist's authority only in his presence (v.12). This has profound implications about the limitations of Satan.
The Limitations of Satan
  • ✦
    Satan is not omnipresentHe is a created being — not God. He can only be in one place at a time. He currently dwells in the body of the Antichrist, so he can only be where the Antichrist is.
  • ✦
    The False Prophet depends on his presenceHe cannot perform miracles except in the Antichrist's presence, so that Satan can intervene directly.
  • ✦
    Limited by time and spaceSatan has great power, but being bound to the physical body of the Antichrist, he is limited. This shows the fundamental difference between Satan and God.
V

The Image of the Antichrist and the Mark 666 (Rev. 13:14–18)

The Image — The Abomination of Desolation
Revelation 13:14–15 …telling those who dwell on the earth to make an image to the beast who was wounded by the sword and lived. He was granted power to give breath to the image of the beast, that the image of the beast should both speak and cause as many as would not worship the image of the beast to be killed.
  • ✦
    An animated imageThis image has some kind of breath. It may involve demonic activity. Satan, the father of lies, will have no problem producing whatever deception is necessary.
  • ✦
    The abomination of desolationDaniel 12:11 confirms it: from the moment the image is set up in the Temple, on the mercy seat, the count of 1,260+30 days begins. The image on the mercy seat is the abomination of desolation spoken of by Daniel and Jesus (Matt. 24:15).
  • ✦
    Satan's classic counterfeitIcons, statues, relics. This is why at the beginning of the Ten Commandments God forbids setting up such things. The image in the Temple directs worship toward the creature rather than the Creator — just as Satan has always done.
  • ✦
    Three requirements of the False Prophet(1) Worship the Antichrist. (2) Receive the mark. (3) Those who refuse cannot buy or sell. The choice is: believe or die. There is no middle ground, no agnostics, no indifferent.
The Number 666 — Its Deep Meaning
Revelation 13:18 Here is wisdom. Let him who has understanding calculate the number of the beast, for it is the number of a man: His number is 666.
  • ✦
    The Hebrew numerical systemJews assign numerical values to each letter of the Hebrew alphabet (1–10 for the first 10, 20–100 for the next 10, 100–400 for the last). Yeshua HaMashiach (Jesus the Messiah) = 749 (7, 7×7).
  • ✦
    6 = fallen, sinful manIn the Bible, the number 6 represents fallen man. 66 (double) = magnify. 666 (triple) = the greatest of all.
  • ✦
    The greatest sinner666 means: the greatest of all sinners who ever lived — because he is indwelt by Satan. It does not relate to a specific name but to the nature of the being.
  • ✦
    A coded message for the JewsOnly Jews will be able to perform the calculation (translating the Antichrist's name into Hebrew). It is a coded message delivered to unbelieving Jews not in Petra — for that specific moment, as a warning. Not before.
The False Trinity — The Greatest Counterfeit in History
The True TrinityThe False TrinityMethod of Counterfeiting
The Father — the invisible God over allThe Dragon — Satan, the invisible god of the worldThe power that acts but is never seen directly
The Son — Christ, who rose and receives worshipThe Beast — the Antichrist, center of world worshipDies and rises, convincing the world he is the Messiah
The Holy Spirit — leads the world to know and worship Jesus; seals believersThe False Prophet — displays signs and wondersDirects the entire world to worship the Antichrist
The principle of the lie A lie cannot exist unless there is a truth it opposes. That is the principle of counterfeiting. At mid-trib, the world is faced with choosing between the false trinity or the true one — and everyone must make that decision one way or another.

VII

The 144,000 on Mount Zion — In Heaven (Rev. 14:1–5)

Are They on Earth or in Heaven?
Revelation 14:1–3 The Lamb standing on Mount Zion, and with Him one hundred and forty-four thousand… They sang as it were a new song before the throne, before the four living creatures, and the elders; and no one could learn that song except the hundred and forty-four thousand.
  • ✦
    They are not in JerusalemIf Jesus were standing in Jerusalem, the Antichrist would not still be alive. According to Daniel, Jesus does not return until the end.
  • ✦
    The heavenly Mount ZionHeb. 12:22: "you have come to Mount Zion and to the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem." At this point, Zion is the glory of God on the throne — which will eventually be revealed on the earth during the Kingdom.
  • ✦
    They are clearly in HeavenThey sing before the throne. Only they know that unique song. They were redeemed (purchased, ransomed) from among those on earth. Jesus will come from Zion — not to Zion. (Rom. 11:26)
Characteristics of the 144,000 (Rev. 14:4–5)
  • ✦
    Virgins — menThey were not defiled with women. They were set apart from marriage to devote themselves completely to serving Jesus.
  • ✦
    Firstfruits to GodThey are the first "complete group" to enter Heaven during the Tribulation. The best fruit is gathered first — they are the most mature, the most obedient, the finest martyrs.
  • ✦
    They follow the Lamb wherever He goesObedient and resolute in their task. They obediently followed Jesus wherever He asked them to go and did whatever He asked them to do.
  • ✦
    No lie in their mouthA powerful testimony. They lived at the level of Paul and the apostles in holiness, though they were not sinless.
Why Did They Die If They Were Sealed?
Revelation 7:2–3 "Do not harm the earth, the sea, or the trees till we have sealed the servants of our God on their foreheads."
  • ✦
    The seal protected them from God's judgmentsThey were sealed to protect them from supernatural disasters — just as God protected Israel in Goshen during the Exodus plagues. The judgments did not affect them no matter where they traveled.
  • ✦
    The seal was removed at mid-tribAt the midpoint of the Tribulation the sealing ends. The time has come for evangelism to conclude and for all destinies to be fixed. Human evangelism is over.
  • ✦
    An example of martyrdomThrough their example, the saints will be encouraged to know that death is nothing to fear. They are the model of obedient martyrdom. As an added grace, the Lord allowed them to be the first martyrs, removed before the worst begins.
VIII

The End of Human Evangelism — The Three Angels (Rev. 14:6–13)

The First Angel — The Last Gospel (Rev. 14:6–7)
Revelation 14:6–7 Then I saw another angel flying in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach to those who dwell on the earth — to every nation, tribe, tongue, and people — saying with a loud voice, "Fear God and give glory to Him, for the hour of His judgment has come."
  • ✦
    The everlasting gospelThe same message as always. There has only ever been one way to be saved, one plan of salvation, one message to be believed — from Adam and Eve to the apostles: the righteous shall live by faith in the promises of God.
  • ✦
    From human messengers to angelsGod elevates the proclamation of the gospel to the heavenly realm. If the lesser (men) were not heard, perhaps the greater (angels) will be heard.
  • ✦
    The window is closingThe angel declares that the hour of judgment has come. "Hour" in this context = a brief period of time. "Fear God" — not the Antichrist, not physical death. This is God's final presentation of the Gospel. (Fulfillment of Matt. 24:14)
  • ✦
    To the most remote placesAll those whom God intends to reach will be reached one way or another. No one was excluded. Evangelism has never depended and will never depend on human beings — God has all the power He needs to save whom He desires.
The Second and Third Angels (Rev. 14:8–11)
Revelation 14:8–11 "Babylon is fallen, is fallen…" "If anyone worships the beast and his image, and receives his mark… he himself shall also drink of the wine of the wrath of God… and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone… And the smoke of their torment ascends forever and ever."
  • ✦
    The second angelHe speaks of Babylon — the Antichrist and his kingdom. He warns that this satanic kingdom that has risen will fall. It will not prevail. Chapters 17 and 18 will be devoted entirely to Babylon.
  • ✦
    The third angelHe warns that whoever takes part in that kingdom will be tormented eternally. There is no opportunity for salvation anymore. No more evangelists or churches teaching the truth.
  • ✦
    Hell is real and eternalFire and brimstone, day and night without end and without rest. They are not freed from sin — each day is one more day of sins. The state never ends; neither does the suffering. Those who preach there is no hell contribute to a great deception. Jesus Himself taught about hell.
  • ✦
    Destinies sealedThe destiny of every person is sealed at mid-trib — with one pending exception that will be studied later.
Summary of the Three Angels
1st Angel — The Everlasting GospelPreaches the gospel of the kingdom one last time to every nation, tribe, tongue, and people. God's final presentation before the return of Christ.
2nd Angel — The Fall of BabylonDeclares that the Antichrist's kingdom is satanic and will not prevail. A foretaste of the judgment coming in Rev. 17–18.
3rd Angel — Eternal CondemnationWarns of the eternal consequences of joining the Beast's kingdom. The message of salvation must include hell.
IX

Blessed Are Those Who Die in the Lord — Perseverance (Rev. 14:12–13)

The Blessing of the Martyr
Revelation 14:12–13 Here is the patience of the saints… Then I heard a voice from heaven saying to me, "Write: 'Blessed are the dead who die in the Lord from now on.' " "Yes," says the Spirit, "that they may rest from their labors, and their works follow them."
  • ✦
    Hope today vs. hope in Rev. 14Today: our hope is the resurrection and eternal life. In Rev. 14: dying is a good thing — it avoids the persecution and the coming judgments. Death is not the enemy.
  • ✦
    The 180° testimonyToday, death takes me away from continuing to witness for Christ. In Rev. 14, death brings me closer to giving a good witness for Christ.
  • ✦
    The 1,000-year reignRev. 20:4 — Those who did not worship the beast and were beheaded will live and reign with Christ for a thousand years. The saved who die during the Tribulation also receive their reward of reigning.
  • ✦
    The saved who do NOT dieThose who survive until the Second Coming of Christ, with their mortal bodies, will not reign but will be governed. Christ's government cannot include people with sinful mortal bodies. They will ultimately be resurrected and receive their reward. Knowing this is perseverance.
The Last Opportunity — Who Remains to Be Saved?
  • ✦
    The outlook at mid-tribThe martyrdom of the 144,000 is proof that the time for knowing the truth has come to an end. The time for sharing the faith and persuading people to believe is over.
  • ✦
    Practically no one left to evangelizeThose who believed are already saved. Those who did not have accepted the mark — unsavable. Except for a specific group of Orthodox Jews who have not yet decided.
  • ✦
    False prophets and teachers2 Pet. 2:1–3 warns: there will be false teachers who will introduce destructive heresies, deny the Lord, and exploit believers with deceptive words. Their condemnation does not linger.
X

The Two Harvests — Believers and Unbelievers (Rev. 14:14–20)

First Harvest — The Believers (Rev. 14:14–16)
Revelation 14:14–16 Behold, a white cloud, and on the cloud sat One like the Son of Man, having on His head a golden crown, and in His hand a sharp sickle… and the earth was reaped.
  • ✦
    The Son of Man or an angel?It appears to be Jesus, but the details suggest it is an archangel: he wears a stephanos (a crown of achievement, not royalty), and another angel gives him the order to reap. No angel gives orders to Jesus.
  • ✦
    The reaping = harvest of believersClassic biblical imagery for believers. It begins at mid-trib and lasts through the entire second half — a period of high mortality among believers. God gathers His harvest as believers die.
  • ✦
    Angels as reapersMatt. 24:31 — "He will send His angels with a great sound of a trumpet, and they will gather together His elect." God uses His angels as messengers and as reapers. Jesus Himself is not a direct reaper.
Second Harvest — The Unbelievers (Rev. 14:17–20)
Revelation 14:19–20 So the angel thrust his sickle into the earth and gathered the vine of the earth, and threw it into the great winepress of the wrath of God. And the winepress was trampled outside the city, and blood came out of the winepress, up to the horses' bridles, for one thousand six hundred furlongs.
  • ✦
    The grape harvest = unbelieversThe winepress (where the grapes are trodden) is classic biblical imagery for unbelievers going to judgment.
  • ✦
    Different timingsThe harvest of believers occurs during the second half (high persecution). The grape harvest of unbelievers occurs at the end of the Tribulation with the Bowl judgments and the coming of Christ.
  • ✦
    Blood up to the horses' bridlesThe Kidron Valley. To create a lake of blood 4.5" deep over 200 miles × half a mile would require an impossible quantity. Most likely: in an instant, the mass death of that entire army produces a tsunami or wave of blood that surges through the valley. This will be studied in detail in the Bowl judgments.
XI

Mid-Trib Summary and Review of All Judgments

What We Studied at Mid-Trib — Synthesis
  • ✦
    The Temple and the Two WitnessesThe Temple has been in operation. The Two Witnesses die and are resurrected.
  • ✦
    The two signs in heavenThe woman (Israel) and the dragon (Satan). The enemy is confined to the earth.
  • ✦
    The protected RemnantBelieving Israel ends up protected in Petra for 3½ years.
  • ✦
    The Antichrist and the False ProphetThey pursue an economic and police state that provides temporary peace to unbelievers, bringing great persecution upon believers and Jews.
  • ✦
    The 144,000 martyrsThey are completely martyred at mid-trib.
  • ✦
    End of human evangelismAn angel preaches the Gospel from heaven. The harvest and the grape harvest are announced.
Review of the Three Series of Judgments
7 Seals → contain the 7 TrumpetsThey initiate the Tribulation. They occur during the first half. They cannot begin without the signing of the covenant.
7 Trumpets → the 7th contains the 7 BowlsThe first 6 occur in the first half. The seventh sounds at mid-trib, announcing the Bowls.
7 Bowls — the Great TribulationFollowing mid-trib. They develop the multiple stages that make up the Battle of Armageddon. They come at the end of the 7 years, not at the start of the second half.
When exactly does each judgment occur? We only know the relative positioning or order within the 7 years, not the exact month or year. This was based on the sequence of the chapters of Revelation. The position of the Bowls within the second half will be answered when we reach the final judgments.
The Last 3½ Years — What They Look Like
  • ✦
    At the close of mid-tribThe last of the 144,000 die and enter heaven. Human evangelism has concluded.
  • ✦
    For believersNew believers are continuously martyred. Those who survive do so under severe persecution.
  • ✦
    For unbelieversThere is relative peace for those who accepted the mark. The Antichrist and the False Prophet maintain order and a functioning economic system for them.
  • ✦
    Until the Bowls arriveEverything is reversed at the end: the Bowl judgments fall specifically on unbelievers. The situation of believers improves while that of unbelievers becomes catastrophic.
✦

Chapters 13 and 14 reveal the climax of the satanic counterfeit: the Antichrist resurrected as the false Messiah, the False Prophet as the false Holy Spirit, and Satan as the invisible false Father — an evil trinity designed to deceive the entire world. At mid-trib, all of humanity is irrevocably divided: those who worship the Beast and live only to be condemned, and those who worship Jesus and die to have eternal life. Human evangelism ends and God Himself preaches the last gospel from heaven. Blessed are those who die in the Lord — for their works follow them, and they will reign with Christ for a thousand years.
